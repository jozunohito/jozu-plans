Phase 1: Days 1-30
Audit, Architect, Attack
✓ Audit the existing pipeline. Day 1. Open the CRM, inspect every opportunity. Where did it come from? Who is the economic buyer? What is the quantified business case? What is the next verifiable buyer action? By end of day, I will have separated real pipeline from friendly conversations and identified the deals that can close fastest.
✓ Define the enterprise sales process. Build a stage-gated pipeline architecture calibrated for healthcare: Initial Discovery, Operational Assessment, Clinical Validation, IT/Security Review, Pilot Agreement, Pilot Execution, Full Deployment Contract. Every stage gate requires mutual alignment - a confirmed Sequence of Events agreed to by both sides, with the buyer validating that we are executing against it together. No stage advances on seller activity alone.
✓ Build the economic value model. Create a quantified ROI calculator specific to Sully: FTE equivalents automated per agent, overtime reduction, revenue per provider increase from capacity gains, documentation quality improvement (fewer denials, faster coding), patient throughput lift. Every demo ends with a number the CFO can take to the board.
✓ Develop enterprise pricing architecture. The $199/doctor/month model works for SMB. Enterprise needs tiered pricing by deployment size, multi-agent bundles (Scribe + Coder as the entry, full AutonomousOS as the platform deal), and annual contract structures with expansion triggers. Design pricing that creates land-and-expand dynamics.
✓ Launch targeted outbound. I have already built the initial target list: 25 multi-location physician groups (TeamHealth, SCP Health, Privia Health, US Oncology Network, Sound Physicians, and others ranging from 400 to 18,000 providers) and 25 community and regional hospital systems (Baystate Health, BayCare, Novant, Piedmont, Inova, and others with 200-4,000 beds and acute financial and staffing pressure). Lead with operational pain, not AI features: "Your physicians are losing 2+ hours per day to documentation. Your coders are backlogged. Your call center is understaffed. What if you could staff 30% of those functions with AI that actually works?" Open 15-20 conversations in the first 30 days.
✓ Craft the competitive narrative. Nuance DAX is the incumbent. Abridge and DeepScribe are the other AI scribes. But none of them offer six integrated agents. The message: "You can buy a scribe from Microsoft, or you can deploy an autonomous operations layer that handles documentation, coding, triage, scheduling, and clinical decision support from a single platform." Make the comparison unwinnable for point solutions.

Day 30 Checkpoint

Deliverables: Pipeline audit complete (day 1). Enterprise sales process defined and in CRM. Economic value model built and tested. Enterprise pricing framework proposed. 50-account target list already built - outbound launched immediately. 15-20 conversations opened. Competitive battle cards drafted. First qualified enterprise opportunities identified.

EHR implementation and optimization partners (Epic/Cerner consulting firms, Accenture Health, Deloitte Health) - they are already inside the health system during go-lives and optimization projects. Bundle Sully into EHR deployment workflows.

GPO and purchasing organizations (Vizient, Premier, HealthTrust) - these organizations aggregate purchasing for thousands of hospitals. Getting Sully on a GPO contract means pre-negotiated pricing and procurement fast-tracking across their entire membership.

Critical advantage: leveraging partner MSAs (Master Service Agreements) that are already in place at target health systems accelerates the sales cycle dramatically. Instead of 3-6 months of legal and procurement review, Sully can be added as a line item under an existing partner contract. This is how enterprise healthcare deals close in weeks instead of quarters.
✓ Open the health plan and payer market. This is a strategic expansion most people will miss. Sully's agent architecture is not limited to provider operations. Health plans run massive clinical-administrative workforces doing utilization review, prior authorization, claims adjudication, care management, and member services. These are exactly the workflows Sully's agents are built to automate:

Go-to-Market Channels

How I Would Build Pipeline

Direct Outbound (60% of pipeline)

Targeted outreach to CFOs, COOs, and VP Ops at multi-location physician groups and community health systems. Lead with the economic case: "Your documentation costs $X per provider per year. Your call center costs $Y. Your coding backlog is costing you $Z in delayed revenue. What if 30% of that was handled by AI that actually works?" Use Sully's existing case studies (Apogee: $1M incremental revenue, 92% time reduction) as proof.

Conference and Event Strategy (20% of pipeline)

Two tiers. First: major industry conferences (HIMSS, ViVE, MGMA, Becker's, HFMA) - but not booth-sitting. Executive meetings scheduled in advance, breakfast briefings with targeted CFOs and COOs, on-site demos. The goal is 10-15 qualified executive meetings per event, not badge scans. Second, and more powerful: Millennium Alliance healthcare assemblies. These are pre-confirmed, 45-minute one-on-one sessions with senior healthcare executives who are actively evaluating technology. No cold introductions. Every meeting is with a decision-maker who has already expressed interest in the category. I have used this channel before. It is the highest-conversion event format in enterprise healthcare sales.

Partnership Channel (15% of pipeline, accelerating to 30%+ in Year 2)

Seven distinct partner categories: healthcare IT consultants, EHR implementation firms, RCM companies, staffing agencies, scribing companies, payer partners, and GPOs. Each has existing buyer relationships and, critically, existing MSAs at target health systems. Leveraging partner MSAs collapses the procurement cycle from months to weeks. By Year 2, the partner channel should be generating 30%+ of enterprise pipeline as agreements mature and co-sell motions take hold.

Expansion Revenue and Payer Market (ongoing)

Two expansion engines. First: every single-agent provider customer is a multi-agent opportunity - Scribe today, Coder and Receptionist tomorrow. Second: health plans and payers represent a parallel market where Sully's agents automate utilization review, prior auth, claims, and member services. Payer deals are 2-5x larger than provider deals. Provider deployments inside converged organizations (Optum, CVS/Aetna, Elevance) become the entry point to the payer side. Combined expansion should generate 50%+ of Year 2 revenue.
