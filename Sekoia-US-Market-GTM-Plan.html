Company Overview

Sekoia.io is a French cybersecurity company headquartered in Rennes, operating across four European countries. The company has built the Sekoia AI-SOC platform, which integrates proprietary cyber threat intelligence with automated detection and response capabilities.

The EUR 26M Series B was led by Revaia, with participation from UNEXO and existing investors Bright Pixel Capital, Omnes Capital, and Bpifrance. The company plans to use the funding to strengthen AI and automation capabilities and expand its MSSP partnership network.

02 - Market Analysis

US Cybersecurity, XDR, and MSSP Market

Market Sizing

The US represents over 40% of global cybersecurity spending. The MSSP segment is growing at 15%+ annually as mid-market and enterprise organizations increasingly outsource security operations. The XDR category is consolidating and growing rapidly as buyers seek unified detection and response platforms.

Market Tailwinds

  • MSSP growth acceleration - US MSSPs are actively seeking differentiated technology to layer into their service offerings. Commodity SIEM is not enough to win clients. Intelligence-driven detection creates competitive advantage for MSSPs
  • European sovereign security demand - US enterprises with European operations need security platforms that comply with EU data sovereignty requirements. Sekoia's European heritage becomes an advantage, not a liability
  • Platform consolidation fatigue - US enterprises are tired of being forced into single-vendor platform commitments. Sekoia's open, API-driven architecture appeals to buyers who want best-of-breed flexibility
  • Threat intelligence premium - Generic detection is commoditized. Proprietary threat intelligence that catches what others miss commands premium pricing and longer contract commitments

Buyer Landscape

Segment Primary Buyer Key Pain Point Sekoia Fit
US MSSPs CEO, VP Service Delivery Need differentiated detection capabilities to win and retain clients against CrowdStrike and Palo Alto managed offerings Multi-tenant XDR with proprietary intelligence. White-label MXDR delivery
Enterprise (5,000+) CISO, VP Security Operations Alert fatigue, detection gaps in current SIEM, desire for intelligence-driven detection XDR platform with integrated CTI, reducing false positives and improving detection accuracy
Mid-Market (500-5,000) Director of IT Security Cannot afford enterprise SIEM/XDR platforms but need advanced detection beyond basic EDR Accessible XDR pricing with MSSP delivery model, making enterprise-grade detection available at mid-market budgets
Core Market Thesis
The US MSSP market is a $46B opportunity growing at 15%+ annually. MSSPs are the distribution channel for cybersecurity in the mid-market and increasingly in the enterprise. Sekoia's European MSSP success with 50+ partners proves the channel model works. The US expansion question is not whether the channel approach is right. It is how to replicate it with US MSSPs who have different competitive dynamics, margin expectations, and technology preferences.

Competitive Positioning Strategy

Sekoia should not try to compete head-to-head with CrowdStrike or Palo Alto in the US enterprise direct sales market. The winning play is the MSSP channel: "We do not compete with your existing vendors. We make your MSSP partner more effective at protecting you." For MSSPs themselves: "We give you the detection quality of CrowdStrike without the ecosystem lock-in that limits your service flexibility."

Competitive Moat

  • Channel-first architecture - Purpose-built for MSSP delivery. Multi-tenant by design, not bolted on as an afterthought
  • European sovereign security - For US companies with EU operations, Sekoia provides compliance with EU data sovereignty requirements that US-only vendors struggle to meet
  • Frost Radar Leader recognition - Independent analyst validation as an XDR leader provides credibility with US enterprise evaluators

05 - Partnership Channel Strategy

Replicating the European MSSP Channel in the US

Sekoia's European success is built on the MSSP channel. The US expansion strategy should replicate this model, adapted for US market dynamics. US MSSPs have different margin expectations, different technology preferences, and different competitive pressures than European providers. The partner program must account for these differences.

Tier 1: US MSSP Partners (Primary Channel)

Priority US MSSPs

  • Arctic Wolf - Largest independent US MDR provider. Adding Sekoia's intelligence-driven XDR creates differentiation against CrowdStrike and SentinelOne managed offerings
  • Trustwave - Global MSSP with strong US mid-market presence. Seeking technology partners to improve detection quality and reduce analyst workload
  • Netsurion - US-based managed XDR provider focused on mid-market. Open to European technology partnerships that strengthen their detection capabilities
  • Deepwatch - Growing MDR provider backed by significant venture capital. Building differentiated service offerings against commodity MDR competition
  • Critical Start - MDR provider with a unique "trusted behavior registry" approach. Sekoia's intelligence layer complements their existing detection methodology

Partner Economic Model

US MSSPs expect 40-60% gross margins on technology-enabled services. Sekoia's partner pricing must support this margin requirement while providing enough value to justify switching from or supplementing existing SIEM/XDR platforms. Deal registration protection and tiered pricing based on volume commitments are essential for partner loyalty.

Tier 2: Technology Alliance Partners

Integration Priorities

  • Microsoft Sentinel integration - The fastest-growing SIEM in the US. Sekoia's intelligence layer on top of Sentinel data creates a differentiated offering for Microsoft-centric enterprises
  • Splunk integration - Largest installed base. Many Splunk customers are looking for ways to augment, not replace, their existing investment. Sekoia's CTI complements Splunk detection
  • AWS Security Hub - Cloud-native integration captures the growing market of AWS-first enterprises managing security in the cloud
  • SOAR platforms (Tines, Torq, Swimlane) - Sekoia detects, SOAR responds. Complementary positioning creates joint pipeline opportunities

Value Exchange

Technology integrations create marketplace presence, co-marketing opportunities, and joint solution positioning. When a US CISO evaluates their security stack, Sekoia should appear as a recommended intelligence and detection layer alongside their existing investments.

Tier 3: Security Consulting and Advisory

Target Partners

  • Optiv - Largest pure-play cybersecurity solutions integrator in the US. Their recommendations carry significant weight with enterprise security buyers
  • Guidepoint Security - Cybersecurity advisory firm helping enterprises evaluate and deploy security technology. Strong CISO relationships
  • WWT (World Wide Technology) - Technology solutions provider with advanced technology center for security evaluation. Enterprise credibility

Value Exchange

Advisory firms earn implementation, integration, and managed services revenue. Sekoia earns trusted recommendations and warm introductions to enterprise buyers who are actively evaluating detection and response platforms.

US Partner Program Structure

Adapted from European Model

  • Deal registration and protection - 90-day exclusive registration window. No direct sales competition with registered partner deals
  • Tiered margins - Silver (40% margin), Gold (50% margin), Platinum (55% margin) based on annual volume and certification completion
  • Certification training - 2-week technical certification program. Certified analysts can deploy and manage Sekoia independently
  • Co-sell support - Dedicated partner SE for joint customer evaluations. Sekoia engineering available for complex technical validations
  • Marketing development funds - Tiered MDF based on partner commitment. Supports joint events, webinars, and content creation
Channel Revenue Target
Within 18 months, the US MSSP channel should generate 60-70% of US revenue, mirroring the European model. Target: 10-15 active US MSSP partners within the first year, with 3-5 generating measurable pipeline by month 9. Each productive MSSP partner represents $200K-$1M in annual channel revenue through their client deployments.
06 - 90-Day GTM Execution Plan

Building the US MSSP Channel from Scratch

This plan focuses on channel development as the primary go-to-market motion. Direct enterprise sales will follow as the MSSP channel matures and provides market validation. The 90-day goal is to sign first US MSSP partners and begin generating partner-sourced pipeline.

Phase 1: Days 1-30, Channel Strategy and Partner Targeting

US MSSP Market Mapping

  • Build a comprehensive list of the top 50 US MSSPs and MDR providers, segmented by size, technology stack, client focus, and openness to European technology partnerships
  • Research competitive technology currently used by each target MSSP. Identify MSSPs running Rapid7, Splunk, or open-source SIEM who are most likely to adopt Sekoia as an upgrade or complement
  • Map the decision makers at each MSSP: CEO, VP Service Delivery, SOC Director, VP Partnerships. Channel sales requires multi-level relationships within each partner organization

US Partner Program Adaptation

  • Adapt the European partner program for US market expectations. US MSSPs expect higher margins (40-60% vs. 30-40% typical in Europe), faster technical support response, and US-based partner management
  • Develop US-specific partner collateral: battle cards positioning against CrowdStrike MDR, Palo Alto Cortex, and SentinelOne managed offerings
  • Build a US MSSP demo environment showing multi-tenant deployment, per-client intelligence feeds, and partner reporting dashboards
Phase 2: Days 31-60, Partner Recruitment and Enablement

Partner Recruitment Outreach

  • Initiate partnership discussions with 10-15 priority US MSSPs. Lead with the European traction story: "50+ MSSP partners in Europe, serving EDF, Vinci, SNCF, and the French Ministry of Armed Forces. We are ready to bring this model to US partners"
  • Attend MSSP Alert Live, ChannelE2E, and regional MSSP networking events. US MSSP decision-makers are highly event-driven. In-person credibility is essential
  • Offer 3-month free evaluation licenses to first 5 US MSSP partners. The investment cost is minimal compared to the channel development value of having partners deploy and validate the platform

Technical Enablement

  • Run certification training for first partner cohort. Target: 2-3 certified analysts per partner within 60 days
  • Build co-sell playbooks showing how MSSP sales teams should position Sekoia against CrowdStrike and Palo Alto managed services
  • Develop joint proposal templates that MSSP partners can customize for their client pitches
Phase 3: Days 61-90, First Deals and Pipeline Development

Partner Deal Activation

  • Support first partner-led client engagements. Provide SE resources for joint demos, POCs, and technical evaluations at partner client accounts
  • Target: 3-5 MSSP partners with signed agreements and at least 2 with active client opportunities in pipeline
  • Document first US partner deployment as a case study. The first US MSSP success story becomes the recruiting tool for partners 6 through 50

Direct Enterprise Pipeline (Secondary)

  • Launch targeted outbound to 25 US enterprises with European operations where Sekoia's EU sovereignty compliance is a direct advantage
  • Develop a "Splunk cost optimization" campaign targeting Splunk customers facing price increases after the Cisco acquisition
  • Hire first US-based Channel Manager with MSSP ecosystem experience. Location in the Northeast or Southeast where MSSP density is highest
