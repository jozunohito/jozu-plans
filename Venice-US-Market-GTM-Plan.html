Product Overview

The Venice Platform

Venice maps access across an entire organization and eliminates permanent access to sensitive information. The platform provides uniform and consistent control over the identities of employees, contractors, machines, and AI systems, ensuring that access is granted only to authorized entities and only for the time required. Venice consolidates privileged access sprawl into a single system that handles privileged access across on-premises servers, SaaS applications, and cloud infrastructure.

Core Platform Capabilities

Just-in-Time Access

Replaces permanent credentials with just-in-time permissions scoped to the individual and the moment. Access is granted only when needed and automatically revoked when the session or task is complete. Eliminates the standing privileges that credential abuse attacks exploit.

Unified Identity Control

Single platform governing privileged access for humans, machines, and AI agents across all environments. Covers on-premises servers, SaaS applications, and cloud infrastructure. Eliminates the fragmented access controls that create security gaps in hybrid enterprises.

AI Agent Governance

Purpose-built for the era where every employee will have tens of AI agents acting on their behalf. Extends privileged access controls to autonomous AI agents, ensuring they operate within defined permission boundaries. Legacy PAM tools built for a static world cannot address this expanding attack surface.

Access Consolidation

Maps and consolidates privileged access sprawl across the entire organization. Provides visibility into who has access to what, when, and why. Replaces the patchwork of legacy tools that enterprises have accumulated, reducing complexity and improving security posture simultaneously.

Why Now: The Identity Crisis in Enterprise Security

Machine identities now outnumber human identities 40 to 1 in the average enterprise. As organizations deploy AI agents that access databases, APIs, and internal systems autonomously, the number of privileged identities requiring governance is exploding. Legacy PAM solutions were designed for a world where a human administrator logged into a server with a vaulted credential. That world no longer exists. Enterprises need privileged access management rebuilt for dynamic, ephemeral, and autonomous access patterns.

Enterprise Buyer Pain

  • Legacy PAM deployments taking 6-12 months, creating extended exposure windows
  • Standing privileges creating persistent attack surface for credential abuse
  • AI agent proliferation outpacing existing identity governance capabilities
  • Tool sprawl: separate solutions for human, machine, and cloud privileged access
  • Compliance audits revealing gaps in non-human identity governance
  • CyberArk/Venafi merger creating uncertainty and migration opportunities

Market Tailwinds

  • Cloud and SaaS privilege management growing at 23.9% CAGR, the fastest-growing segment
  • North America holds 38% market share, representing the largest addressable market
  • Zero-trust mandates requiring just-in-time access as a foundational control
  • Insurance carriers beginning to require PAM as a condition of cyber coverage
  • Board-level focus on identity security after high-profile credential abuse breaches
  • AI agent adoption creating entirely new identity governance requirements
