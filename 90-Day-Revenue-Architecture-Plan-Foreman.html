Situation Assessment

Where Scout Out Is Right Now

What You Have

A YC-backed AI estimating and proposal tool for residential contractors. A 2-person team with a working product that solves a real pain: turning plans into accurate estimates, clean scopes, and professional proposals faster than anything else on the market. No GTM infrastructure. No playbook. Founder is doing everything.

What You Need

A repeatable sales motion for selling to general contractors and remodelers. ICP definition, outbound sequences, talk tracks, pricing validation, and a system that generates pipeline without the founder in every conversation. The foundation a future sales hire can run on day one.

The Core Challenge

Residential contractors are not SaaS buyers. They do not read cold emails at 9am. They are on job sites. They buy based on trust, referrals, and proof that a tool will save them time on the thing they hate most: estimating, scoping, and writing proposals. The GTM motion must meet them where they are, in language they use, through channels they actually check.

The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

JEFF
ARAGON
Phase 1: Days 1-30
Learn the Buyer, Close the First Deals
✓ Talk to contractors. Get on the phone with 30+ residential GCs and remodelers in the first two weeks. Not to sell. To learn. How do they estimate today? What do they hate about it? What would make them switch? What channels do they pay attention to? This intel defines everything that follows.
✓ Define the ICP. Not all contractors are the same buyer. Segment by revenue size, project type (remodel vs. new build), team size, and tech adoption level. Identify who buys fast and who needs hand-holding.
✓ Build the message. Translate Scout Out's capabilities into contractor language. Not "AI-powered estimating platform." Something like "cut your estimate time in half and stop losing bids to guys who guess faster than you." Test messaging in live conversations.
✓ Close first deals. Run the full cycle on 5-10 live opportunities. Discovery, demo, objection handling, close. Learn what works by doing it, not theorizing.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Build the Outbound System
✓ Launch outbound. Stand up sequences targeting the ICP. Test channels: email, phone, LinkedIn, contractor forums, trade association lists. Contractors do not respond to the same channels as SaaS buyers. Find what works.
✓ Test pricing and packaging. Run pricing experiments. Monthly vs. annual? Per-user vs. per-project? Free trial vs. paid pilot? Get data on what converts and what creates friction. Report findings to the founder.
✓ Build repeatable systems. Turn what is working into documented, repeatable processes: outbound sequences, talk tracks, demo flow, objection handling, follow-up cadence. Make it so the next hire can pick this up and run.
✓ Product feedback loop. Relay contractor feedback to product and engineering. What features close deals? What gaps lose them? What do contractors compare Scout Out against?
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Validate the Model, Set Up to Scale
✓ Revenue validated. Enough closed deals to prove the unit economics work. CAC, conversion rate, average deal size, and time to close are all known quantities, not guesses.
✓ GTM playbook documented. The complete, tested playbook: ICP, messaging, outbound sequences, demo script, pricing, objection handling, competitive positioning. A new hire reads this and knows exactly what to do.
✓ Hiring profile defined. Based on 90 days of real sales data, define who to hire next. What skills matter for selling to contractors? What background works? What does the comp plan look like?
✓ Upmarket path mapped. Identify when and how Scout Out moves from residential GCs to larger commercial contractors or enterprise remodeling firms. Define the trigger points and what changes in the sales motion.
