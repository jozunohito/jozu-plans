The Competitive Landscape

Axonius (28% mindshare, $200M+ raised) and Armis (19%, $300M+ raised) are the market leaders by funding and awareness. But both depend on agents, credentials, or integrations to discover assets - meaning they only find what is already known. runZero finds what others miss because it does not depend on existing infrastructure. The sales motion must make this distinction clear in economic and operational terms, not just technical ones.

The Plan

90 Days to Predictable Growth

JEFF
ARAGON
Phase 1: Days 1-30
Assess, Align, Activate
✓ Assess the current sales org and pipeline. Day 1. Audit every open deal, rep performance, win/loss data, and pipeline coverage. Understand conversion rates by segment. Identify where deals stall and why. Separate forecast from fiction.
✓ Align with the COO and leadership on targets. Define quarterly and annual revenue targets by segment (enterprise, mid-market, federal). Agree on pipeline coverage ratios, team capacity, and hiring plan. Set the baseline metrics for board reporting.
✓ Install MEDDPICC-aligned forecast discipline. Every deal in the pipeline gets qualified against a confirmed plan letter methodology. Economic buyer identified and engaged. Decision criteria and process confirmed. Metrics quantified. No deal advances without evidence the buyer has validated the mutual path forward.
✓ Activate the federal pipeline. BOD 23-01 is a regulatory mandate creating immediate demand. Engage Carahsoft to accelerate pipeline through existing contract vehicles. Build a target list of federal agencies and defense organizations with asset visibility gaps. The Marine Corps reference is a door-opener.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Close, Coach, Scale
✓ Personally close seven-figure enterprise deals. Get into the largest opportunities and run them through the full cycle. Discovery with the CISO. Technical validation with the security team. Business case to the CFO. Procurement navigation. I will not manage from a dashboard. I will be in the deals alongside the team.
✓ Build competitive positioning against Axonius and Armis. Both are well-funded incumbents. The battle card must be grounded in the fundamental architectural difference: agent-dependent visibility vs. true network-level discovery. Frame it as a risk argument, not a feature argument. "What percentage of your assets does your current tool actually see?"
✓ Coach and develop the sales team. Weekly deal reviews, call coaching, pipeline inspection. Identify skill gaps. Build training around the exposure management value narrative, MEDDPICC execution, and multi-stakeholder deal navigation. Raise the bar on forecast accuracy.
✓ Drive cross-sell and expansion. 500+ existing customers are expansion opportunities. The move from asset discovery to full exposure management (risk prioritization, EASM, vulnerability findings) is the natural upsell path. Build the expansion triggers and customer success handoff process.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale the Engine, Report the Results
✓ Define the team expansion plan. Based on 60 days of data: where do we need more capacity? Enterprise AEs, mid-market reps, federal specialists, SE support? Build the hiring profiles, interview process, and onboarding plan.
✓ Build the partnership channel. MSSPs, MDR providers, and security consulting firms (Optiv, Coalfire, GuidePoint) have existing CISO relationships. VAR and distribution partners beyond Carahsoft for commercial segments. Structure referral and co-sell agreements that generate pipeline without adding headcount.
✓ Deliver board-level reporting. Weekly pipeline reviews, monthly revenue reports, quarterly strategic reviews. Forecast accuracy, deal velocity, segment performance, competitive win rates, expansion revenue. The board gets a number they can trust because the process behind it is inspectable.
✓ Present the 12-month revenue plan. Target ARR by segment, pipeline coverage, team capacity, partnership revenue, expansion projections, and investment required. Data-backed, grounded in 90 days of real execution, not assumptions.
