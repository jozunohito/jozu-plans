Product Overview

The Spirit Platform

Spirit is building an enterprise security platform that embeds advanced AI directly into organizational security infrastructure. The platform targets large organizations operating complex enterprise software environments, enabling intelligent threat detection, behavioral pattern analysis, and real-time response to attacks while significantly reducing the manual workload on security operations teams.

Core Platform Capabilities

AI-Native Threat Detection

Advanced AI models purpose-built for enterprise security environments. Identifies threats through intelligent pattern recognition and behavioral analysis rather than static signature matching. Designed to surface real threats while dramatically reducing false positive noise that overwhelms security teams.

Real-Time Attack Response

Automated response capabilities that act in real time when threats are detected. Reduces the gap between detection and containment from hours or days to seconds. Built to operate at the speed modern attack chains require, without waiting for human analysts to manually triage alerts.

Enterprise-Scale Architecture

Designed from the ground up for large organizations with complex, multi-layered software environments. Integrates across the enterprise security stack without requiring organizations to rearchitect their existing infrastructure. Built to handle the data volumes and complexity of Fortune 500 environments.

Operational Burden Reduction

Core design philosophy centers on freeing security teams from repetitive, manual workflows. Automates investigation, correlation, and initial response actions that currently consume the majority of SOC analyst time. Allows security professionals to focus on strategic decision-making rather than alert triage.

Why Now: The AI Security Inflection Point

Enterprise security is being reshaped by two simultaneous forces. First, attack sophistication is accelerating as adversaries adopt AI to generate polymorphic malware, craft convincing social engineering campaigns, and automate reconnaissance at scale. Second, security teams are drowning in alerts, with the average enterprise SOC processing thousands of events daily while struggling to maintain adequate staffing. Legacy tools built around static rules and manual investigation workflows cannot keep pace.

Enterprise Buyer Pain

  • SOC analyst burnout and staffing shortages across the industry
  • Alert fatigue: 70%+ of alerts go uninvestigated at most organizations
  • Legacy SIEM and detection tools generating excessive false positives
  • Mean time to detect and respond measured in days, not minutes
  • AI-powered attacks outpacing signature-based defenses
  • Board-level pressure for measurable security posture improvement

Market Tailwinds

  • Large enterprises account for 62% of the AI cybersecurity market in 2026
  • Adversaries using AI to automate and scale attacks at unprecedented speed
  • Global cybersecurity workforce gap exceeding 3.5 million positions
  • Regulatory frameworks (NIS2, SEC cyber rules) demanding faster detection and disclosure
  • Cloud migration and hybrid infrastructure expanding the attack surface
  • CISO budget allocation shifting from legacy tools to AI-native platforms
