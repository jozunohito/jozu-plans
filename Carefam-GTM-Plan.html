The Structural Problem

This is not a cyclical downturn. The US healthcare workforce is experiencing a structural collapse. Over 138,000 registered nurses have left since 2022. Nearly 40% of remaining RNs plan to retire within five years. Nursing schools rejected 92,000 qualified applicants in a single year due to capacity constraints. The pipeline cannot keep up with demand.


The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects a shortage of 250,000+ RNs by 2030 and nearly 700,000 across all clinical roles by 2037. This is the defining operational crisis for every US health system.

The Buyer's Pain

  • Average healthcare recruiter manages 70 open roles simultaneously
  • Teams can only speak with 10% of applicants in real time
  • Time to first interview averages 13 days at most health systems
  • 67% of healthcare leaders cite retention as top 2026 priority
  • Travel nurse costs remain 2-3x permanent staff rates

Why Now for Carefam

  • Post-pandemic staffing normalization has not materialized
  • Wage inflation forcing health systems to find efficiency gains
  • Only 16% of hiring teams use AI - massive greenfield opportunity
  • CMS reimbursement pressure drives cost reduction mandates
  • Conversational AI maturity now sufficient for clinical credentialing workflows
KEY INSIGHT

Matan said it well: "HR teams are drowning in administrative noise and often miss qualified candidates simply because they can't respond fast enough." That speed gap is measurable, and it is where Carefam wins deals. Every day a role stays open costs a health system $500-1,500 in agency fees, overtime, and lost revenue.

SEGMENT STRATEGY

Most AI healthcare hiring startups target health systems first because the deal sizes are larger. That is a mistake at this stage. Long-term care and home care providers have shorter sales cycles, less procurement friction, and the highest turnover rates. They are the fastest path to reference-able logos and ARR growth. Health systems become the second act once you have 20+ proof points from Segments 2 and 3.

Execution Plan

90-Day GTM Build

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

Sales Process Architecture

  • Define ICP scoring criteria across all three buyer segments with firmographic and behavioral signals
  • Build qualification framework - what constitutes a real opportunity vs. a tire-kicker at a health system
  • Map the typical buying committee: HR lead, clinical leadership, IT/security, procurement, finance
  • Create stage-gated sales process with evidence requirements at each stage (not rep opinion)

Messaging and Positioning

  • Develop power messages for each persona: VP of TA hears cost reduction, CNO hears clinical coverage, CFO hears agency spend elimination
  • Build ROI calculator: days-to-fill reduction, agency fee displacement, recruiter productivity gains
  • Write case study frameworks from existing customer data - 900% growth means there are stories to tell
  • Create competitive battle cards for Incredible Health, Vivian, Apploi, and legacy ATS vendors

Phase 2: Pipeline Build (Days 31-60)

Outbound Engine

  • Build target account list: 200 accounts across all three segments, prioritized by pain signal density
  • Pain signals to track: active job postings (50+ open clinical roles), agency RFPs, executive turnover in HR/nursing leadership, recent CMS survey deficiencies
  • Launch multi-threaded outbound sequences targeting VP of TA + CNO simultaneously at each account
  • Email copy that leads with the buyer's problem, not Carefam's features. "Your system posted 340 nursing jobs last quarter" beats "Our AI agents streamline hiring"

Channel and Partnership Strategy

  • Identify 5-10 healthcare IT consultants and HRIS implementation partners as referral channels
  • Evaluate integration partnerships with Workday, UKG, and Oracle HCM - these are the systems of record in health systems
  • Explore co-selling with EHR vendors (Epic, Cerner) who have workforce management adjacencies

Phase 3: Revenue Acceleration (Days 61-90)

Deal Execution

  • Run first pilots with 3-5 qualified accounts from Phase 2 pipeline
  • Define pilot success criteria before launch: X% reduction in time-to-fill, X candidates processed, X hours saved per recruiter per week
  • Build mutual action plans (Sequence of Events) for each pilot - buyer commits to evaluation timeline, not just "we'll try it"
  • Weekly deal inspection rhythm: pipeline review, forecast accuracy, stage progression velocity

Scaling Infrastructure

  • Document the sales playbook from first 90 days of learning - what works, what does not, actual objections heard
  • Define hiring profile for first 2-3 AEs based on real deal data, not assumptions
  • Establish pricing model validation: per-facility, per-hire, or platform fee - test with actual buyers
  • Set up CRM hygiene, reporting dashboards, and forecast methodology

Strategic Notes

What I Would Tell You Over Coffee

The Land-and-Expand Math

Healthcare is a land-and-expand market. A health system will not sign an enterprise-wide contract on first engagement. They will pilot at one facility or one department. The economics of this business depend on expansion rate - getting from one facility to five, then to twenty. Your sales process needs to be designed around this from the start. Pilot structure, success criteria, and executive sponsorship for expansion should all be defined before the pilot begins, not after.

Positioning Against Staffing Agencies

Your real competitor is not Incredible Health or Vivian. It is the staffing agency. Health systems spent over $20B on agency nurses during and after the pandemic. That number has come down but remains elevated. Carefam's value proposition is strongest when framed as "reduce your dependency on agencies by making your internal team faster." That message resonates with CFOs, not just HR leaders, and it opens budget conversations that pure HR automation cannot.

What US Healthcare Buyers Actually Need to See

US healthcare buyers do not buy from demos. They buy from people who understand their world - CMS regulations, Joint Commission requirements, nurse-to-patient ratios, the politics of academic medical centers vs. community hospitals, and the budget pressure that comes from spending $20B on agency staffing. I have been in those rooms for over a decade. That is the advantage of having a US-based GTM leader who has already sold into your buyer - no ramp time learning the language, the procurement process, or the compliance requirements. Day one productivity.

Timing
