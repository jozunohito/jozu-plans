Section 02

US Market Opportunity

The US construction estimating software market was valued at $626 million in 2025 and is growing at 8.7% annually. The broader global construction estimating market reached $2.73 billion in 2025 and is projected to hit $5.01 billion by 2030 at a 12.89% CAGR. Takeoff technology leads the segment with 42% market share, and 73.5% of deployments are now cloud based. The market is ripe for disruption by AI-native platforms like AISekisan.

Why the US Market Is Ready for AISekisan

Demand Drivers

  • Severe labor shortage in construction estimating. Experienced estimators are retiring faster than new ones enter the field.
  • Rising material costs and bid competition pressure contractors to estimate faster and more accurately.
  • The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act is generating unprecedented project volume requiring faster bid turnaround.
  • General contractors losing bids because manual takeoff processes cannot keep pace with opportunity volume.

Technology Gap

  • Incumbent tools (PlanSwift, Bluebeam, On-Screen Takeoff) digitize the process but still require manual measurement.
  • No dominant AI-native takeoff solution has captured the US market yet. The window is open.
  • Autodesk launched Autodesk Estimate in late 2025, validating the category but lacking AISekisan's AI depth.
  • Cloud adoption at 73.5% means buyers are ready for modern, SaaS delivered solutions.

The Core Value Proposition for US Buyers

A typical estimating team spends 40 or more hours on a single bid. AISekisan reduces that to 4. The question for every general contractor and subcontractor is straightforward: how many bids are you losing each quarter because your estimating team cannot keep up with the volume? AISekisan does not replace estimators. It gives each estimator the capacity to handle ten times the workload, which means more bids submitted, more bids won, and more revenue captured per estimating dollar spent.

Section 03

Competitive Landscape

The US construction estimation and takeoff market includes both legacy digitization tools and emerging AI entrants. AISekisan's advantage is its AI-native architecture, built from the ground up for automated takeoff rather than retrofitted onto manual workflows.

Competitor Approach AISekisan Advantage
PlanSwift Digital takeoff with manual measurement tools. Strong in residential and light commercial. AISekisan automates the measurement itself. 90% time reduction vs. PlanSwift's manual digitization.
Bluebeam Revu PDF markup and collaboration platform with takeoff features. Popular with architects and engineers. Bluebeam is a collaboration tool with takeoff bolted on. AISekisan is purpose-built for AI-driven takeoff and estimation.
STACK Construction Technologies Cloud based takeoff and estimating. Good UX but still manual measurement workflows. STACK digitized the workflow. AISekisan automates it. Fundamentally different value proposition.
Autodesk Estimate Launched November 2025. Cloud tool linking 2D/3D takeoffs to costs in Autodesk Construction Cloud. AISekisan has years of AI training data from real construction projects. Autodesk is entering with a new product and limited AI maturity.
ProEst Cloud estimating with digital takeoff. Strong in commercial construction. ProEst requires manual takeoff input. AISekisan's AI reads drawings and extracts quantities automatically.

Competitive Positioning Summary

Every incumbent in this market digitized the manual process. AISekisan eliminates it. That is not an incremental improvement. It is a category shift. The positioning for US buyers should be clear: "Your current tool helps you measure faster. AISekisan measures for you." When the estimating labor shortage is the number one constraint on bid volume, automation is not a nice-to-have. It is the competitive advantage that determines which contractors win and which ones watch projects go to competitors.

Section 05

Partnership and Channel Strategy

Direct sales will build initial traction, but partnerships will drive scale. H2 Corporation should pursue four categories of partners simultaneously to accelerate US market penetration beyond what a standalone sales team can achieve.

Construction Technology Platforms

Integrate AISekisan into the platforms contractors already use daily. Integration partnerships create distribution at scale without direct sales effort for each customer.

  • Procore: The dominant construction management platform. AISekisan integration would make takeoff data flow directly into Procore project workflows.
  • Autodesk Construction Cloud: BIM-to-takeoff integration creates a powerful end-to-end workflow. Position as complementary to Autodesk Estimate, not competitive.
  • Oracle Aconex / Primavera: Enterprise-grade construction platforms used by the largest GCs and owners.
  • Partnership Model: Marketplace listings, API integrations, co-marketing agreements, joint customer success.

Building Materials Manufacturers and Distributors

This is a channel H2 Corporation has already validated in Japan. Replicate the model in the US where materials companies have strong contractor relationships and incentive to drive product specification.

  • National Distributors: ABC Supply, Beacon Building Products, SRS Distribution, BMC Stock Holdings
  • Manufacturers: USG, CertainTeed (Saint-Gobain), Owens Corning, Johns Manville
  • Partnership Model: Co-branded AISekisan instances with pre-loaded product catalogs. Distributors offer AISekisan to their contractor customers, driving adoption while ensuring materials are specified from their catalog.

Industry Associations and Trade Organizations

Construction is a relationship-driven industry. Association partnerships provide credibility, conference access, and direct engagement with buyers who trust their trade organizations.

  • Associated General Contractors of America (AGC): 27,000+ member firms. Conference sponsorship and technology showcase opportunities.
  • Associated Builders and Contractors (ABC): Focused on merit-based construction. Strong technology adoption programs.
  • American Institute of Constructors (AIC): Professional estimator community. Ideal for product validation and word-of-mouth.
  • Construction Financial Management Association (CFMA): CFOs and controllers who approve technology purchases.

Japan-US Business Networks

Leverage the Japan connection as a strategic advantage, not a liability. Japanese construction companies operating in the US are a natural beachhead, and Japan-US business organizations provide warm introductions to US partners.

  • JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization): Programs specifically designed to support Japanese companies entering the US market. Conference sponsorships, business matching, and market research support.
  • Japanese General Contractors in the US: Obayashi, Shimizu, Kajima, and Taisei all have active US operations and can serve as reference customers and advocates.
  • US-Japan Business Council: Executive-level introductions to US construction and technology companies.
