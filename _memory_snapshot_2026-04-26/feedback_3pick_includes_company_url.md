## The rule

**Every 3-pick table (and every single-pick recommendation) must include BOTH:**

1. **Apply URL** — the job-posting URL where Jeff submits the application (ATS, LinkedIn, BuiltIn, careers page deep link, etc.)
2. **Company website URL** — the company's public-facing primary site so Jeff can independently research before clicking apply

**Format:** add a column "Company URL" to the 3-pick table, OR include both URLs inline in the row's "Apply URL" cell (formatted clearly as Apply: ... | Company: ...).

**If the company has no public-facing website (stealth mode, e.g., AI Fund Build-mode spinouts):** flag explicitly: "Stealth — only LinkedIn / parent fund visible" and provide the parent-fund URL (e.g., aifund.ai) as the closest verifiable proxy. Never leave Jeff guessing.

## Why this matters

Jeff needs to independently sanity-check every role before investing time on a tailored cover letter and resume submission. The apply URL alone forces him to either trust me blindly or chase the company URL himself. Both are bad. The company URL takes me 5 seconds to surface and saves Jeff 5+ minutes of friction every pick.

## When it's not optional

This rule is non-optional for any role surfaced as a 3-pick or single-pick recommendation. No exceptions. If I cannot find a company URL within 60 seconds of search, I flag the stealth condition explicitly and provide the closest verifiable proxy (parent fund, accelerator portfolio page, founder LinkedIn).

## The two-step protocol this rule enables

The whole point of the URL-in-table rule is to let Jeff pre-verify BEFORE I build the ship-package. The protocol is:

1. **Surface step:** I deliver 3 picks with both URLs (apply + company). Stop. Wait for Jeff.
2. **Vet step:** Jeff scans URLs in 30-60 seconds. Sanity-checks role is real, altitude is right, vertical is right.
3. **Pick step:** Jeff picks one. The pick IS the go signal because Jeff already verified.
4. **Build step:** I build the full ship-package (verify JD details, draft cover letter, resume note, form fields).

Do NOT skip step 2 by building the ship-package before Jeff has had a chance to vet. Tokens are Jeff's, not mine. Speculative full-package builds on un-vetted picks waste Jeff's time and tokens.

If a pick turns out to be dead or not-fit on Jeff's scan, the cost is the time it takes me to refresh 3 new picks. Cheap. The cost of the wrong pattern (ship-package before Jeff vets) is cover-letter wasted, resume work wasted, and the trust friction of "you keep doing work I wasn't ready for."
