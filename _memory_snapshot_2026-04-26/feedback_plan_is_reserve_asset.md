## The rule

**Jeff sells the plans. He does not give them away.**

- No plan URL in outreach emails
- No plan URL in LinkedIn connects or first messages
- No plan URL attached to applications unless specifically requested
- No PDF of the plan sent proactively

If someone wants the plan, they talk to Jeff. Jeff walks the plan on a call.

## What is acceptable in outreach

1. **Reference the existence of the plan.** "I have a detailed point of view I would welcome the chance to walk through."
2. **A lean preview at most.** Up to 3 initiative titles, or the architecture sections named (market context, competitive landscape, three initiatives, forecast, etc.). No substance, no numbers, no specifics.
3. **A canonical-case reference.** Naming a past reframe pattern (large national radiology provider, California Health Plan, CalOptima Health) is fine. Details on the current plan are not.

## What is NOT acceptable

- Posting the plan URL in outreach
- Attaching the plan PDF
- Summarizing the three initiatives in an email
- Naming the specific sub-plays, named accounts, or financial targets from the plan
- Leading with "here is a 90-day plan I built for your seat"

## Why

1. Jeff's plan is his **Value Wedge artifact**. Value Wedge is revealed in discovery, not volunteered in a cold email. Same principle he teaches clients applies to his own search.
2. Giving the plan away commodifies the thinking. The CEO keeps the blueprint even if Jeff is not hired. That is unpaid consulting.
3. It signals "I will do the work for free before you commit." Low-confidence framing.
4. It ends the conversation at the resume level. A plan-in-hand buyer has no reason to call. A plan-in-reserve buyer has to call to get it.

## Voice refinement — 2026-04-24

**Do NOT use the phrase "I don't share plans before discovery" or any variant of "my methodology holds that discovery precedes prescription"** in short outbound messages to hiring teams. Jeff explicitly corrected: "that is dumb."

That framing reads as stiff, consulting-speak, lecturing the hiring team about Jeff's methodology. It lands as AI-polished, not operator-grade.

**Use this instead (Jeff's voice, verbatim-approved closing):**

> "I built a 90-day plan for this role and would love to share it with you. Looking forward to next steps."

The plan-as-reserve-asset discipline still applies (don't attach the plan, don't link it, walk it verbally on a call). But the EXPRESSION of that discipline in short messages is warm and simple, not methodological lecture.

Longer cover letters may still reference the Jozu Enterprise Revenue System and the discovery-first principle in one sentence where appropriate. Short outbound messages (LinkedIn notes, direct team messages, application-form text boxes) use Jeff's verbatim closing above.

## The new motion

### First touch (cold)
- Resume
- One-paragraph cover note
- LinkedIn connect note (if applicable)
- NO plan, NO URL, NO PDF

### Second touch (after they engage)
- Reply to their message
- Schedule the call
- Still no plan URL. Say: "I will walk you through the 90-day plan on the call. Easier to discuss live."

### On the call
- Walk the plan verbally. Screenshare the plan URL live if helpful for anchoring.
- Offer to send a written follow-up after the call.

### After the call (if interest is real)
- Send a tailored follow-up with the plan URL as "the plan we discussed."
- Now the plan is doing its intended work: reinforcing a conversation that already has momentum.

## How to apply

Before writing any Jeff-facing outbound touch, verify:
1. Is this a first touch? If yes, plan URL is forbidden.
2. Has the prospect accepted my connect / replied to my email / booked a call? If no, plan URL is forbidden.
3. Did the prospect ASK for the plan or the 90-day approach? If no, plan URL is forbidden.

If all three are "no", the answer is: no plan URL. Resume + short note only.

## The plans still exist

Plans live at jozuconsultinggroup.com/plans/. They are discoverable if a recruiter or CEO searches for Jeff. That passive discovery is fine and reinforcing. What is prohibited is Jeff actively pushing the URL into a first-touch conversation.
