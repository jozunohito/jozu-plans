## The rule

**Roles requiring "scaled a SaaS company to $100M ARR" as a prior accomplishment are too big for Jeff right now.** They are not rejected forever; they are just not the current fit. Jeff's next role is earlier-stage, where he can build and lead rather than inherit and scale a large incumbent revenue engine.

## What IS the right size for Jeff's Tier 1 filter

- **Founding sales leader** / first US sales hire / first commercial hire at a Series A-B company
- **Head of Sales** at a Series B-C company at $5-25M ARR building the first 5-15 person sales team
- **Founding VP Sales** where there is no existing team and no existing motion
- **Head of Americas** or **First US Sales Hire** at an internationally-headquartered company making its first US push (UK, Japan, Israel, EU origin stories)
- **CRO** at a Series B-C company under $25M ARR where the mandate is build-from-near-zero, not scale-the-existing-engine
- **Fractional CRO** or part-time senior GTM advisor where the operator works hands-on with founders

## What IS NOT the right size

- Post-Series D companies at $50M+ ARR looking for a CRO who has scaled to $100M+ prior
- Executive seats on a company with an established 30+ person sales team
- "Scale and optimize" CRO seats where the role is refining a working engine, not building one
- Roles where the listed prior accomplishment is "took a SaaS company from $X0M to $100M+ ARR"
- Roles where the listed experience expectation is 15+ years of CRO-title experience specifically (not just enterprise sales experience)
- Public-company Revenue Acceleration roles targeting operators who have already done the mature-revenue seat

## Why this filter matters

Jeff's actual operator strength is the zero-to-one motion and the player-coach shape. His 25 years include the IC revenue generation of that motion ($24M TCV Ushur, $10M+ CalOptima deal, $150M+ career revenue) AND the methodology for installing it (CustomerCentric Selling coaching, Jozu Enterprise Revenue System). That is valuable at Series A-C where the motion does not yet exist. At Series D+ at $100M+ ARR, hiring committees default to someone who has personally owned a $100M+ revenue line as CRO, which Jeff has not and may not want to compete against.

**Applying to roles above this ceiling:**
- Wastes Jeff's time and application-crafting cycles
- Creates rejections that feel personal but are actually sizing mismatches
- Can signal "job hunt in distress" if pattern is noticed

**Applying to roles inside this filter:**
- Matches Jeff's actual operator strengths
- Positions Jeff as the right fit, not the stretched fit
- Turns "stretch on CRO title" into "founding sales leader" clarity
- Compensation may be lower base but higher equity, which matches Jeff's Jozu-building horizon

## How to apply

Every Tier 1 sourcing agent, application-drafting agent, and recruiter-outreach conversation filters by this rule.

Specifically:
- Exclude job postings that name "$100M+ ARR scale" as a prior accomplishment requirement
- Exclude postings that expect "15+ years CRO title experience"
- Exclude post-Series D companies with mature revenue engines
- Prioritize Series A-C, international-to-US expansion plays, founding sales roles, and under-$25M ARR companies
- If a role is a borderline fit (Series C at $30M ARR, for example), include it but flag the ceiling proximity
