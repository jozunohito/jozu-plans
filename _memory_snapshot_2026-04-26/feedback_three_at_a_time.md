## The rule

When Jeff asks for the next application or next role, surface **THREE candidates from THREE distinct verticals** in a tight table. He picks. I execute the chosen one fully (URL verification, fit score against rubric, message draft, form-field cheatsheet, log-and-ship).

## Why three, not one

- One name = me deciding what's relevant for him. That violates `feedback_volume_over_curation.md`.
- One name = tunnel-vision risk. I caught myself recommending Vatica → Maven → Twill, all payer, after Sword Health was payer. That was lazy momentum-riding. Jeff has 13+ verticals on his resume.
- Three names = panoramic enough that Jeff sees breadth, narrow enough that he can pick in 30 seconds.

## Why three, not five or ten

- Five/ten = batch curation. Jeff already has the master ranked top-200 file for that.
- Three = forces me to surface DISTINCT verticals (not three flavors of the same vertical).
- Three = preserves Jeff's decision agency without choice-paralysis.

## How to apply

When Jeff says "next" / "next on deck" / "what's the next application":

1. Pull from the master ranked top-200 file (or pipeline inbox if not yet built).
2. Pick **THREE roles from THREE distinct verticals** that match Jeff's full arsenal.
3. Surface in a 5-column table: # | Company / Role | Vertical | Score | Apply URL | One-line why.
4. Each row = one vertical. Never two payer roles in the same three-pick. Never two cyber roles in the same three-pick.
5. End with: "Pick one. I verify URL, score, draft, ship."
