## The rule

Jeff's brand asset identity is **Jozu Consulting Group** — a firm, a practice, a group. NOT Jeff Aragon the person-operator.

Applies to:
- Email signature
- LinkedIn banner
- Business cards
- Zoom background
- Plan headers
- Website hero

## What is OUT of brand assets

These lines do NOT appear in signatures, banners, or other brand assets:

- "25 years enterprise sales"
- "4 years Tokyo"
- "Harvard Negotiation Project"

## Why "Group" is big

The word "Group" in "Jozu Consulting Group" is the firm signal. Dropping it to "Jozu" alone reads as a solo shop or a product brand. Keeping "Group" reads as a practice with more than one mind behind it — which matches the positioning (Jeff + published doctrine + methodology that outlives him + future staff).

## How to apply

Before generating any brand asset, verify:
1. Is Jozu Consulting Group (full phrase) the primary identity?
2. Are all person-level credentials (years, coaching, Harvard, Tokyo) absent from the visible composition?
3. Is Jeff's name (if present) subordinated to the firm name?
4. Does the asset read as a practice's seal, not a person's résumé?

If any answer is no, rebuild.
