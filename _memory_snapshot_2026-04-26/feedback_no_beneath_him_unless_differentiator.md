## The rule

**Default: do not surface or recommend roles below Jeff's level (Manager-tier, Director-tier where the title is real, IC-AE seats, sub-$200K base).** Jeff is operating at VP / RVP / CRO altitude with 25 years and $150M+ career revenue. Manager-tier postings will read as "beneath him" and erode his application brand if he keeps applying down-ladder.

**Exception: speculative-elevate (Trojan Horse) play is justified ONLY when ALL of these are true:**

1. The company has a **specific differentiator** that aligns to one of Jeff's unique wedges (Japan-to-US bridge, regulated-procurement RFP discipline, healthcare-payer-MA depth, AT&T/CDK cyber-channel architecture).
2. The company is at a **stage where they likely don't have the senior seat posted yet** (early US scale-up, Series A-B with founder-CEO doing CRO duties, just-funded company building commercial team).
3. There is a credible path for the company to **redirect Jeff to a senior seat** after seeing his profile (CRO, VP Sales, Head of US, etc.).
4. Jeff explicitly opts in.

If even one of these isn't met, **do not surface the role**. The Mujin Western US Channel Sales Manager application cleared this bar (Tokyo HQ + active US scale-up + Japan-bridge differentiator + 100-job pledge in Chicago suggests they're staffing up + Jeff's Jozu identity is the exact wedge). Most other below-his-level postings will not.

## What this prevents

- Jeff feeling like I'm padding his application count with sub-altitude submissions
- Recruiter pattern-matching Jeff as a "spray and pray" candidate
- Jeff's brand drifting downward in the market
- Wasted application velocity on roles that won't elevate

## How to apply when surfacing 3-at-a-time picks

Each of the 3 picks should be at-altitude or above (VP / RVP / CRO / Senior-Director-with-real-scope / Federal-GS-13-and-up). If a pick is below altitude, the speculative-elevate rationale must be in the "Why" column explicitly: name the differentiator + the elevation path, or skip the pick.

Example acceptable:
> Mujin — Channel Sales Manager (Western US) — $120K base, but Tokyo HQ + active US scale-up + Japan-bridge differentiator = Trojan Horse play to surface CRO conversation.

Example NOT acceptable:
> [Generic Series A startup] — Account Executive — Manager tier — fits the cyber vertical.

If I cannot articulate the elevation path, the pick is not valid.

## Reinforcement 2026-04-25 (later) — Travel is NOT a disqualifier on its own

Jeff verbatim, after I flagged 10-15 weeks/year travel at UP.Labs CCO as a concern: "I have spent the last 30 years traveling heavily. Over 100,000 miles per year on average. I am not concerned about it as long as the role is good enough. I won't do it for a junior role. I will for this."

**The rule is NOT "travel is bad." The rule is altitude + travel together.**

- **At-altitude role (Founding Head of Sales / CRO / CCO / Founding GTM at right stage) + heavy travel = FINE.** Jeff has millions of airline miles across multiple carriers from 30 years of doing exactly this work. Heavy travel is not insulating from the seat; it IS the seat at this altitude.
- **Sub-altitude role + heavy travel = HARD NO.** Heavy travel for a junior or Manager-tier seat is the lane he's exiting. The combination is the trap.
- **At-altitude role + remote = better than at-altitude + heavy travel** (lifestyle), but both are acceptable.
- **At-altitude role + pure-in-person metro tie = HARD NO** unless the company is genuinely high-leverage AND Jeff opts in (Sierra-pattern). The disqualifier is metro residency requirement, not the travel itself.

**Stop flagging travel as a soft red flag at altitude.** When the role title and stage are right, the travel cadence is a non-issue. Jeff has done this for three decades. Treat travel concerns ONLY as a disqualifier when the role is sub-altitude (where travel piles insult on a seat that already fails the altitude rule) or when the requirement is pure-in-person residency tied to a single metro (Kaizen-pattern).

This refinement is locked. Future role surfacing should not soft-flag travel at CRO/CCO/Founding-Head-of-Sales altitude.
