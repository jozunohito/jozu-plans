## Rule 1: No metadata footers on resumes. Ever.

Do NOT add anything like:
- "Last updated YYYY-MM-DD"
- "Master resume lives at ..."
- "Vanta-tailored resume ..."
- "Master career fact sheet at ..."

**Why:** Jeff sends the resume to employers. Internal references and file-path notes make him look amateurish and force him to delete them every time before sending.

**How to apply:** 
- Track versions in `VERSIONS.md` (an inventory file in the resume folder), not inside the resume itself.
- Use filename conventions (`jeff_aragon_resume_vanta.md`) to signal target-tailoring, not a footer line.
- Leave the resume body clean. End with Education. Nothing after.

## Rule 2: Education phrasing

Jeff prefers `University of Colorado, Boulder | 2 years`. NOT "Two years attended." Keep it short and factual.

## Rule 3: No disclaimers, no confidentiality tags, no dates on the resume itself

Resumes are dated by the submission context (cover letter, application date). No need to put a "last updated" line on the document.
