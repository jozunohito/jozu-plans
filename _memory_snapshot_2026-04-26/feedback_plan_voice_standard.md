## The standard

As of 2026-04-23, the Vanta plan (post-corrections) is the canonical voice and structure standard for every future Jozu 90-day plan.

## Seven hard rules for every plan

1. **Opener is AI-assisted, pressure-tested, owned.** Use this exact framing or a close variant: "I am applying for the [role] at [company]. I used AI to accelerate research and drafting. I pressure-tested every line before sending. The speed is AI. The plan is mine." No consulting-speak like "A resume is bounded by a page" or "A plan is bounded only by discipline." No "I respect specific mandates, and I answer them in kind." Keep it flat and honest.

2. **Widen, not walk, on the Two Gates.** When either gate fails, Jeff does not exit. He widens the opportunity, surfaces a larger use case, follows the economic pain downstream, and anchors the solution to an existing forcing function inside the account (contract renewal, regulatory deadline, board-level number). The canonical teaching example: a large national radiology provider became $1.6M ARR from a $30K OCR request by anchoring to the incumbent vendor's contract renewal. The renewal was the forcing function. The solution replaced the incumbent's majority-revenue contract.

3. **Anonymize named former client deals.** "US Radiology" is "a large national radiology provider." Avoid specific client names where the client did not consent to be referenced publicly. Numbers and patterns stay.

4. **Use full formal names for public/semi-public entities when first mentioned.** "CalOptima Health (Orange County Health Authority)" on first mention, then "CalOptima Health" or "the plan" thereafter.

5. **Never cite Jeff's personal earnings or commissions in the plan.** No $1M IC year. No $418K commission. Lead with operating numbers: $150M+ career revenue, $8M ARR, $24M TCV in under 2 years at Ushur, $10M+ largest deal, 10x peer outperformance, top 5 percent globally at UKG 5.5 years.

6. **Methodology provenance must be accurate.** The Jozu Enterprise Revenue System is Jeff's own framework built across 25 years. CustomerCentric Selling is a separate methodology Jeff coached for 12 years, which sharpened how he teaches sales process. Do not say the Jozu system was "taught through CCS" or "forged at CCS" — those are different things.

7. **Voice rules (always):** no em dashes, no mid-sentence bolding (headers and list-item leaders only), no hype, direct MBA operator-partner tone, 80/20 framing, bullets, financial-impact framing. Jeff sells deals, does not "build" them.

## Structure reference (17-numbered-section spine)

Same spine as the Vanta / Omada / WunderGraph plans:
1. Executive Summary (5 punch points)
2. Why this document exists (opener + sources + methodology)
3. The System I Run (5-Level Discovery, Power Messaging, Two Gates with widen-not-walk, Plan Letter, Mutual Sequence)
4. Market Context and Opportunity Sizing (TAM triangulation, 4-5 sources)
5. Competitive Landscape (6-8 competitors, each with specific vulnerability, positioning matrix)
6. The Problems I Solve at [Company] (2-3 role-specific)
7. ICP Deep Dive + numbered trigger events
8. Customer Segmentation + ACV bands + named target list
9. First 90 Days: Three Initiatives (Weeks 1-4 audit, 5-8 design, 9-12 deploy)
10. Pricing and Packaging Thesis
11. Win/Loss Hypotheses
12. The Team I Build in Year One (scope to role level)
13. Risk Register
14. Scorecard: 30/60/90 + Year-One Forecast
15. Integration with AI in the GTM Model
16. What I Need to Learn from You
17. Why Me
18. Close
19. References and Sources

## When to apply

Any new plan agent or any plan rewrite uses this standard as baseline. Reference this memory file in agent prompts.
