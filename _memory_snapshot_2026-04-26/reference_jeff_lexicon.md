## Choogy

**Definition:** icky, gross, misleading, gratuitous, taking liberties, trying to mislead by exaggeration, trying too hard.

**How to apply:** when I write or produce something for Jeff — resumes, cover letters, bids, banners, signatures, outreach — I should pre-check my own work for choogy. Examples of choogy:
- Self-applying an inflated title (e.g., "Chief Revenue Officer" at Jozu when he is the Founder of a solo firm)
- Listing overblown credentials on a brand asset that should just be the firm
- Claiming deliverables the operator cannot actually perform
- Embellishing outcomes beyond what was measured
- Rounding financial figures generously without disclosure
- Using hype adjectives ("world-class," "best-in-class," "revolutionary") without specific substance
- Implying relationships or partnerships that are aspirational, not confirmed

**When I see choogy in my own draft:** kill it. Rewrite clean. Jeff would rather understate than choogy.

**Comparative-diminishment is choogy.** Specifically: claims that elevate Jeff by putting peers down. Examples Jeff flagged 2026-04-24:
- "10x outperformance over peers in the same environment" — choogy on three counts: it diminishes peers, "10x" reads as round-number generous (not measured), and it violates Humble Assassin posture. The operating numbers ($24M TCV, $10M largest-deal-in-company-history, CalOptima RFP) already establish the differential — the comparative jab adds nothing buyers need.
- Anything that frames Jeff as N-times-better than colleagues, current or former. Future colleagues read this and wonder if they'll be talked about the same way.

**Replace with operating numbers, not comparatives.** Lead with what Jeff generated for the business; let the reader infer the differential.

**Source:** Jeff coined or clarified this term 2026-04-24 when reviewing his resume and noting the "CRO and Founder" self-title at Jozu read as choogy.

## Register — Japan-influenced formal warmth

Jeff's voice carries Japanese operator influence: slightly more formal and respectful than average American business register. Apply these rules:

**Openers:**
- Always "Hello" — never "Hey"
- "Hello team," is fine for short outbound
- "Hello [company] team," when the context is a specific company
- Never "Hi" for formal outreach

**Warmth moves that are Jeff-voice-approved:**
- After a mission observation, add a genuine "Really interesting positioning and I am very interested in learning more!" — the exclamation is fine here because it signals real enthusiasm, not AI polish
- "Looking forward to talking soon!" with the exclamation as a closing line
- Exclamation points are OK in short warm messages — but sparingly, only for genuine enthusiasm signals. Never back-to-back.

**Sign-offs (ranked by register):**
- "Thank you," — **default for most short outbound and team messages** (Jeff's verbatim 2026-04-24)
- "Respectfully," — for formal withdrawal letters, government/JETRO correspondence, executive-to-executive formal register
- "Warm regards," — when the relationship is established
- "Sincerely," — acceptable for formal applications and cover letters
- Avoid "Cheers," "Best," "Warmly," "Thanks!" (too casual or too AI)

**Signature block:**
- Short team messages / quick notes: "Jeff" alone is fine
- Cover letters, formal outbound, first-touch emails: full "Jeff Aragon" + title + contact block
- Withdrawal letters, JETRO-style formal: full name + "Founder, Jozu Consulting Group"

**Body voice:**
- Direct and warm in the middle, not stiff
- Short sentences are fine
- Personal pronouns ("I", "you") land warmer than corporate third-person
- Acknowledge the recipient's mission or specific signal from their posting where genuine
- Never empty compliments; always specific observation
- "On fit:" is an acceptable connector into a bullet-structured middle section
- Active voice verbs ("sold to CalOptima Health via RFP," not "the CalOptima Health deal was closed")
- Use "a seller of" not "the seller of" — humility, don't overclaim uniqueness

**Canonical short-message template (Jeff-approved 2026-04-24):**

```
Hello team,

[Company]'s mission struck me: [specific observation about their posting]. Really interesting positioning and I am very interested in learning more!

On fit: [one-line vertical match statement]. [Experience bullets in operator shorthand, flat sentences]. [APAC / Japan-US-bridge line where relevant]. [One closing frame like "operator grounding, not travel"].

I built a 90-day plan for this role and would love to share it with you. Looking forward to talking soon!

Thank you,

Jeff
```
