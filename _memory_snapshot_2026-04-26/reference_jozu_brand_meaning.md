## The brand is a deliberate bilingual signal

**Jozu (上手)** = earned mastery, skilled, expert. In Japanese, it is a compliment specifically for competence achieved through effort ("jouzu desu ne"), not natural talent.

**The fox in the Jozu logo is a kitsune (狐)**, the sacred messenger of **Inari Ōkami**, the Shinto deity of:
- Prosperity
- Commerce, industry, business success
- Agriculture, rice, sake
- Protection against malevolence

**32,000 of Japan's ~80,000 Shinto shrines are dedicated to Inari.** Japanese merchants traditionally worship at Inari shrines for commercial prosperity. The kitsune is the patron spirit of commerce.

Kitsune carry these specific attributes:
- Intelligence
- Strategic patience
- Long-term wisdom (accumulates over generations; more tails = more wisdom)
- Shapeshifting (adaptability)
- Ward off malevolent energy

The red-white color palette in the Jozu logo maps to Shinto sacred colors used on kitsune statues at Inari shrines.

## What "Jozu + kitsune" signals together

Earned mastery of commerce + the sacred patron of merchants + Japanese cultural literacy at operator depth. It says, to a Japanese executive reading the logo, "this is a westerner who has earned the right to represent Japanese commercial wisdom to both sides of the Pacific." It is not a decorative fox. It is a bilingual operator seal.
