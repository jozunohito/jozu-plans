## The Jozu Enterprise Revenue System — Doctrine v0.3

This is what Jozu installs inside client companies. It is also the system Jeff personally sells with. The full doctrine lives in the `jozu-plans` repo (see "Repository" below). The pillars:

1. **Value Wedge** — the narrowest provable point of distinction the company can defend against the strongest competitor in the deal cycle.
2. **Five-Level Discovery** — Symptom → Symptom Owner → Cause → Cost → Cost Owner. Discovery does not stop at the symptom; it escalates to the cost owner who can fund the change.
3. **Power Messaging — Why-This / Why-Now** — every executive conversation must answer Why this category, Why this company, Why now. Without all three, the deal stalls.
4. **Two-Gate Buying Cycle Alignment** — every enterprise deal has two buying gates (Gate 1: budget formation. Gate 2: commit / signature). Forecast discipline is the practice of dating both gates and showing both on the Plan Letter.
5. **Nine-Step Sequence** — operational scaffold from first qualified meeting through signature (Discover → Evaluate → Validate → Propose → Negotiate → Sign → Onboard → Expand → Renew, with branching at validation).
6. **Plan Letter** — the single most important Jozu artifact. **The Plan Letter is the container.** Inside the Plan Letter sits the **Mutual Sequence of Events (MSoE)**. The MSoE is never enumerated as a peer to the Plan Letter — it lives inside it. The Plan Letter is sent to the prospect's economic buyer to confirm shared understanding of business outcome, success metrics, decision criteria, decision process, evaluation steps, paper, and signature date. The MSoE inside it is the joint execution timeline both sides sign off on. Without the Plan Letter, the deal is anecdote, not pipeline.
7. **Infrastructure Pillars** — CRM hygiene, sequencing tools, lead enrichment (Clay), domain warming, attribution model, RevOps spine.
8. **Japan Pillars** — bilingual messaging discipline, Japanese pricing / contract / SOW posture, JETRO / J-Bridge / SelectUSA alignment, partner-of-record structures for METI-influenced procurement.

The system was hardened over 5.5 years at Kronos / UKG (precision execution at the enterprise bar) and refined through CustomerCentric Selling coaching since 2014. MEDDPICC was applied at every Ushur and UKG account.

**Disney fist-bump anecdote (canonical, use only when the moment fits):** Jeff once booked a Disneyworld trip months before a $1.6M deal closed because the system forecasted exactly when DocuSign would land. Mid-flight his wife knew the deal had closed from a fist-bump. She did not take off her headphones. That is what selling-the-system-you-build looks like. Never deploy this gratuitously.
