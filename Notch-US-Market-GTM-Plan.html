Product Overview

The Notch Platform

Notch deploys AI agents that automate and execute operational workflows end-to-end, spanning both conversational and back-office processes. The platform handles the entire workflow from intake through resolution: processing documents, verifying coverage, executing payments, and managing escalations without human handoffs. Every action follows defined compliance logic with full audit trails.

Core Platform Capabilities

Autonomous Resolution

Achieves 70-73% autonomous resolution across customer deployments, with some implementations reaching 87% end-to-end resolution within 12 months. The system handles entire workflows from intake through settlement, processing documents, verifying coverage, and executing payments without human handoffs.

Compliance-First Architecture

Deterministic rules control when and how AI engages across claim handling, policy servicing, and customer communications. Every action follows defined workflows and compliance logic. Full visibility into each AI decision, including reasoning and source references, logged for claims review, QA, and regulatory audits.

Omnichannel Coverage

Delivers resolution across chat, email, social, and voice channels with compliant routing, approvals, and escalation built into every workflow. Consistent customer experience regardless of engagement channel, with unified audit trails across all touchpoints.

Insurance Workflow Automation

Autonomously processes FNOL (First Notice of Loss), endorsements, deductible waivers, and cancellations within defined authority levels. Uses deterministic rules, audit-ready decisioning, and escalation paths to maintain compliance and control at every step.

Why Now: Regulated Industries at the AI Inflection Point

Insurance, healthcare, and financial services companies are under simultaneous pressure to reduce operational costs, improve customer experience, and maintain regulatory compliance. AI automation offers the only path to achieving all three simultaneously. However, these industries cannot adopt generic AI tools. They require purpose-built solutions with compliance guardrails, audit trails, and deterministic decision frameworks that generic chatbot platforms cannot provide.

Enterprise Buyer Pain

  • Customer service costs consuming 15-25% of operating budgets in insurance
  • Agent turnover exceeding 30% annually, creating training and quality gaps
  • Compliance requirements making generic AI chatbots unacceptable
  • EU AI Act high-risk provisions taking effect August 2026
  • 23 US states adopting NAIC AI Model Bulletin with more expected
  • Policyholders demanding faster resolution and digital-first engagement

Market Tailwinds

  • 48% of insurance businesses now using agentic AI, up from single digits in 2024
  • AI chatbots projected to save insurance industry $2.3B annually by end of 2026
  • Cost per phone interaction ($8-$15) vs. AI agent ($0.50-$0.70) creates urgent ROI case
  • 92% of health insurers using or planning AI deployment
  • Regulatory clarity emerging, creating confidence for enterprise adoption
  • Competitive pressure as early adopters gain measurable cost and CX advantages

Notch's Market Position

Notch occupies the critical intersection of AI automation and regulatory compliance. While generic AI customer service platforms struggle to meet the demands of regulated industries, and legacy insurance technology vendors lack AI-native capabilities, Notch has built the only platform that delivers autonomous resolution with full compliance guardrails, audit trails, and deterministic decisioning. The 12x ARR growth and 87% resolution rates prove enterprise buyers are choosing Notch because no other solution addresses their specific requirements.
