The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

JEFF
ARAGON
Phase 1: Days 1-30
Foundation
✓ Buyer definition. Define the ideal buyer: VP Engineering or Director of Engineering at companies with 50+ engineers running multi-team projects. Map their buying process and budget authority.
✓ Power Message. Build a Power Message connecting AI project agents to the economic cost of coordination failures: delayed releases, budget overruns, and engineer time wasted on status updates instead of building.
✓ Discovery framework. Create a technical discovery framework: current project tooling, coordination pain points, delivery metrics, and where the biggest time sinks live.
✓ Pipeline architecture. Define a lightweight pipeline with clear stages: technical demo, pilot scoping, proof of value, expansion proposal.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Execution
✓ Outbound activation. Target VP Engineering at Series B-D companies with 50-200 engineers. Lead with the coordination cost problem, not the AI agent solution.
✓ Live deal work. Run the first 3-5 technical evaluations personally, learning what objections come up and what proof points close the deal.
✓ Sequence of Events. Build a pilot framework: 30-day proof of value on one project team, measured against delivery speed and coordination overhead reduction.
✓ Forecast discipline. Simple forecast discipline: every deal has a validated problem, a named champion, agreed pilot scope, and a decision date.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale Readiness
✓ Closed revenue. Close 2-3 paid pilot agreements that generate case study data and prove the business model.
✓ Repeatable process. Document the technical sale end to end: what demo to show, what pilot to propose, what metrics to measure, what objections to expect.
✓ Hiring blueprint. Define the first GTM hire: a technical seller who can demo the product and speak credibly to engineering leaders.
✓ Handoff. Hand off pipeline, process documentation, and a target account list with contacts enriched and ready for outreach.
