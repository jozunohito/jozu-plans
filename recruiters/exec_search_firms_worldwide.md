## How to use this list

- **Prioritize firms with named partner contacts** over firms marked NEEDS MANUAL RESEARCH. Volume wins when the send is high-signal.
- **Use the talent-pool portal where it exists** (lower friction, higher response rate). Submit portal first, then email the named partner as follow-up citing the portal submission.
- **For firms without portals, email the named partner directly** using the attached template.
- **Treat confidence flags honestly.** Send to HIGH-confidence emails first; batch MEDIUM/LOW sends together and expect bounces. When a bounce happens, search the firm's newsroom or press releases for the partner's name in a quote — quoted partners almost always have their real email in the firm's media kit.
- **Follow up in 30 days on no-response** with a one-paragraph re-touch. Reference any new Jozu case study or plan you have published.
- **When a partner responds with a specific role**, send the relevant Jozu 90-day plan from jozuconsultinggroup.com/plans/ — not before. The plan is a reserve asset.
- **Tokyo firms** require bilingual nuance — lead with the Japan specialty in the first line of the email when writing to Tier 4. For JAC and Robert Walters, the portal is the right first move.
- **Tier 5 PE-backed firms** want operator numbers in the first bullet. Jeff's $24M TCV in Ushur 2-year ramp is the single strongest opener for that tier.
- **Do not send to every firm at once.** Batch: Tier 2 first (highest CRO mandate volume for Jeff's profile), then Tier 1, then Tier 5, then Tier 3, then Tier 4. Track responses in Airtable.
