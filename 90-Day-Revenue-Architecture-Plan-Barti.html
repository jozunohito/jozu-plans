What You Need Next

200 practices is less than 1% of the 40,000+ eye care locations in the US. The product works. The AOA endorsement opens doors. Now you need a sales engine that systematically reaches the other 99% before RevolutionEHR, Compulink, and ModMed figure out how to bolt AI onto their legacy platforms. The Core Challenge

Eye care practices are running on fragmented systems that were never designed to work together. The optometrist toggles between an EHR, a billing platform, a scheduler, a phone system, and a patient messaging tool. The practice manager reconciles data across all of them. Doctors stay late doing notes. 30% of calls go unanswered. Staff leave not because of the work but because the tools make everything harder. Barti replaces all of it with one platform. The sale is not "switch your EHR." The sale is "stop running your practice on duct tape."

The Plan

From 200 Practices to 2,000

Phase 1: Days 1-30
Audit, Segment, Launch
✓ Audit the existing sales motion. Day 1. How are the current 200 practices finding Barti? What is the conversion rate from demo to signed? What is the average deal size and cycle time? Where are deals stalling? Extract the patterns that drive the next 2,000.
✓ Segment the market. Three tiers. Tier 1: Multi-location eye care groups and private equity-backed optometry platforms (10-100+ locations, $100K-$500K+ annual contracts). Tier 2: Independent practices with 2-5 doctors (the volume play, $15-25K annual contracts). Tier 3: Solo practitioners (highest volume, lowest touch, product-led conversion). Build the ICP and sales motion for each tier.
✓ Develop the ROI messaging. Quantify the value in terms practice owners care about: staff hours saved per week, additional patients per day from AI Scribe and Quinn (1-2 more patients daily), revenue recovered from AI Receptionist (30% of calls currently go unanswered, 10-20% more appointments booked), billing accuracy improvement, and total system cost reduction from replacing 10+ tools with one.
✓ Activate the AOA channel. The AOA endorsement is your most powerful asset. Build a co-marketing and co-selling motion with AOAExcel. Every AOA member who evaluates an EHR should see Barti first. Conference sponsorships, AOA newsletter placements, state association events. Make the endorsement a pipeline engine, not just a badge.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Enterprise Deals, Repeatable Process
✓ Target multi-location groups. PE-backed optometry platforms (MyEyeDr, EyeCare Partners, Total Vision, Clarkson Eyecare) and large independent groups are the enterprise play. These are $100K-$500K+ annual deals with centralized decision-making. Build the business case for standardizing on Barti across all locations: unified patient records, consistent clinical workflows, centralized billing and reporting.
✓ Close first enterprise deals. Work live opportunities through the full cycle. Discovery with the practice group CEO or VP of Operations. Clinical validation with the lead optometrist or medical director. IT review for integration requirements. Procurement and contract. Install forecast discipline with a plan letter methodology - every meeting produces a confirmed document the buyer validates.
✓ Build the demo-to-close playbook. Document the discovery framework, demo flow, objection handling (switching costs, data migration, staff retraining), proposal structure, and implementation timeline. The Dr. Shyu result (78% increase in patient visits, 36%+ revenue boost) is your proof point. Make it repeatable.
✓ Launch outbound to independent practices. Build a target list of the top 500 independent multi-doctor practices. Lead with the operational pain, not the technology: "How many hours a week does your staff spend toggling between systems? How many calls are you missing? What would 1-2 more patients per day mean for your revenue?"
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale the Engine
✓ Hire and onboard AEs. Define the profile based on 60 days of real selling data. Hire 2-3 AEs who can run the playbook from day one. Onboard them using the documented process. Get them into live deals within their first two weeks.
✓ Build the partnership channel. ACQUIOS Advisors is already a partner - expand this model. Optical equipment distributors, practice management consultants, optometry buying groups, and ophthalmology ASC management companies all have existing relationships with your buyers. Structure referral agreements. Leverage partner MSAs to accelerate procurement at larger groups.
✓ Map the ophthalmology expansion. Barti started in optometry. The $12M raise is partly to expand into ophthalmology. Ophthalmology practices are larger, higher revenue, and more complex (surgical workflows). Define the ICP, the buyer (practice administrator, medical director, ASC director), and the messaging for this segment. Build the roadmap for Year 2.
✓ Present the 12-month revenue plan. Grounded in 90 days of real data: conversion rates, deal sizes, cycle lengths, channel performance. A roadmap for scaling from 200 to 2,000+ practices with clear hiring, pipeline, and partnership milestones.
