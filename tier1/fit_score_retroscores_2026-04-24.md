## Patterns observed

The other five categories combined could not save them. The rubric's 15-point Hidden DQ weight is calibrated correctly; the lesson is to actually score it honestly before submitting, not after.

- **The cliff between 60 and 80 is real and is built almost entirely on Vertical + Hidden DQ.** All four roles scoring 74+ (iVerify 85, Omada VP 86, Fastino 77, Arbiter 77, Vanta 74, Ivo 71) have either direct vertical match OR clean DQ profile. The two fails (runZero 65, Kaizen 61) were vertical-adjacent AND DQ-flagged. Title / stage / comp / motion / HR-gap have less variance across the sample.

- **Stage fit punishes Jeff symmetrically at both ends.** Vanta and Omada CRO score down because they are ABOVE Jeff's $25M-$100M ARR sweet spot. runZero scored down because it was BELOW (Series A builder seat). The runZero post-mortem refinement is now baked into the rubric: target Series B / late-Series-B / Series C in the $25M-$100M ARR band.

The VP Sales variant resume now exists (`..._vp_sales.md`); use it for any role titled VP / Head of Sales / RVP at sub-$100M-ARR companies. Reserve the CRO variant for actual CRO seats at $50M+ ARR companies.

- **SOLICITED_STRETCH and UNSOLICITED tags work as designed.** Strala (68) and Arbiter (77, unsolicited) would have been false-negative declines under a pure-floor rule. The tag taxonomy correctly carved out "stretch tagged upfront, decline doesn't count" and "no active role, silence doesn't count." Keep the taxonomy intact.
