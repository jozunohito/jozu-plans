# Fit Score Rubric — Jeff Aragon Tier 1 Applications

**Owner:** Jeff Aragon
**Author:** Claude (rubric drafted 2026-04-24)
**Locked:** Yes. Categories and weights do not change without an explicit decision log entry.
**Purpose:** One objective 100-point fit score every Tier 1 role must clear before Jeff's time is spent applying or building a 90-day plan. Replaces vibes-based "Fit 5" tags in inbox sourcing files.

---

## How to use

1. Score every role on all 7 categories before applying.
2. Sum to a 100-point total.
3. Apply tag rule (SOLICITED_FIT / SOLICITED_STRETCH / UNSOLICITED) — see taxonomy below.
4. Apply floor rule:
   - **Total >= 70** -> apply (tag-appropriate resume + cover note).
   - **Total 60-69** -> flag, ask Jeff before proceeding **unless** SOLICITED_STRETCH-tagged (then apply with stretch framing).
   - **Total < 60** -> do not apply. Log reason. Move on.
5. Re-score after rejection or interview to refine the rubric calibration.

This rubric is for retro-scoring and prospective-scoring. Jeff's resume facts are the truth basis.

Does Jeff's vertical experience directly map to the role's target buyer market?

## Signal-tag taxonomy

Required tag on every retro-score and every prospective-score. Drives whether decline is signal.

### SOLICITED_FIT
- Jeff applied to a role he is on-pattern for (vertical match, stage match, title match)
- **Decline IS signal** about Jeff's positioning
- Examples: runZero VP Sales (cyber + Series A + remote = on-pattern, decline = real signal); Vanta Head of Enterprise Sales (GRC + scaled = appropriate altitude, decline = real signal)
- Use this tag for the bulk of retro-scoring and treat declines as data points to refine resume / outreach

### SOLICITED_STRETCH
- Jeff applied knowing he has a vertical or scale gap
- **Decline is NOT signal** because the gap was acknowledged upfront
- Examples: Strala VP Sales (P&C insurance vertical Jeff hasn't lived); Lovable Head of Sales NA (Swedish AI dev tooling)
- Tag set BEFORE submission. Cover letter explicitly addresses the stretch.

### UNSOLICITED
- No active matching role on the company's careers page; Jeff submitted to a generic intake or talent pool
- **Silence is NOT signal** — there is no actual seat to fill
- Examples: Arbiter AI generic intake when no sales role was active; talent-pool entries on Greenhouse / Lever
- Do not count silence as rejection. Track separately.

## Worked example — Sword Health Regional VP, Health Plan Sales (Medicare Advantage)

This is the locked anchor case. Use this calibration when other scores feel off.

| Category | Score | Note |
|----------|-------|------|
| Vertical match | 23 / 25 | MA / D-SNP / Medicare Supplement = direct payer ICP overlap with CalOptima ($10M TCV) and California Health Plan ($1.4M ARR). Sword's MSK condition wedge is adjacent product, but the buyer is the same payer-side decision-maker Jeff has personally closed. |
| Hidden DQ risk | 13 / 15 | Late-stage growth (~$3B valuation, IPO-track) creates mild "scaled to $X" pattern risk, but RVP seat is appropriately sized below CRO and remote-friendly. No metro DQ. |
| Title / level fit | 14 / 15 | RVP, Health Plan Sales = exact RVP-band sweet spot. Reports up under National VP / CRO. Owns named-account revenue. Player-coach. |
| Stage / size fit | 11 / 15 | Late-stage growth (~$3B val, est >$100M ARR) is above Jeff's sub-$25M sweet spot, but RVP seat-structure offset means he's running a regional engine, not the whole company. Acceptable shape. |
| Comp fit | 9 / 10 | RVP MA at IPO-track health-tech typically lands $300-400K OTE. Public payer-RVP comp data supports band. |
| Motion fit | 9 / 10 | Player-coach RVP carrying named accounts + small team. Direct match to Ushur shape. |
| Hard-requirement gap risk | 9 / 10 | "Deep payer ecosystem knowledge, 10+ years complex multi-stakeholder payer sales" — Jeff comfortably clears with CalOptima + CHP + UKG healthcare history. |
| **Total** | **88 / 100** | **Tag: SOLICITED_FIT.** Apply with CRO variant resume framed as "regional commercial leader scaling MA wedge." |

Calibration takeaway: anything scoring near 88 is a top-decile shot. 70-79 is a normal apply. 60-69 is a borderline ask-Jeff. <60 is decline.
