## Hypothesized root causes (ranked by confidence)

### #1 (highest confidence): Wrong resume variant — CRO master sent to a founding-tier seat

The CRO master variant leads with the Jozu Consulting Group section, which includes the bullet: *"Deliver Fractional CRO engagements and full-cycle enterprise deal execution for early-stage and growth-stage technology companies."*

For a Founding Head of Sales seat at a Series A AI/LLM infra startup, that bullet reads to a recruiter as: "This candidate runs a parallel consulting practice. He's a flight risk for our first-GTM-hire. Pass."

This is the same pattern that killed runZero (CRO variant for sub-CRO seat) and is why the variant rule was locked on 2026-04-24 evening — AFTER the Fastino submission. Fastino was submitted at midday Friday, before the rule was active.

**Lesson reinforced:** the variant rule is non-negotiable. VP variant for any founding-tier or sub-CRO-altitude seat. Never CRO master with the Fractional CRO bullet for these.

### #2 (medium confidence): Vertical mismatch on proof stack

Jeff's headline proof points are healthcare-payer:
- CalOptima Health (~$10M TCV via formal RFP)
- California Health Plan ($200K → $1.4M ARR reframe)
- National radiology organization ($25K OCR ask → $1.6M ARR platform)

Fastino sells LLM infrastructure to dev teams. The recruiter scan looking for "has sold AI/LLM platforms to engineering buyers" sees healthcare-RFP narratives and rejects.

**Lesson:** for AI/LLM infra applications going forward, surface AI/automation experience first (Ushur Customer Experience Automation = AI/automation) and de-emphasize healthcare-only narrative. Or skip the AI/LLM infra vertical when the company is selling to engineering teams specifically and Jeff lacks engineering-buyer reference customers.

### #3 (lower confidence): Network preference

Series A founding seats often go to candidates already known to founders or investors. Public posting may be a formality. A no-warm-intro cold application has a structurally lower hit rate at this stage.

**Lesson:** for top-priority Series A founding seats going forward, lead with warm intro through investor or founder network where possible. Cold application is the lowest-percentage path.

### #4 (lower confidence): Geographic / SF-presence signal

Fastino is SF-based. Jeff is in Coeur d'Alene. The "operating base + full mobility" framing was in the cover letter but may not have surfaced in a 30-second recruiter scan.

**Lesson:** for SF-based co's, address the location signal earlier in the cover letter or use a recommendation/intro to bypass the recruiter pre-screen.

## Decision-tree improvements

**Before submitting any future founding-tier application:**

1. **Variant check:** is this a founding-tier / Founding Head of Sales / Founding GTM / First CRO seat? → Use VP variant (no Fractional CRO mention). NEVER CRO master.
2. **Vertical check:** does Jeff have direct buyer-side reference customers in this vertical? If no, soften the deepest healthcare-payer narrative and surface adjacent/Ushur-AI experience first.
3. **Warm-intro check:** does Jeff have a path through an investor, founder peer, or alum to the founding team? If yes, use it; cold application is the backup not the lead.
4. **Location signal:** if the company is metro-anchored (SF, NYC), address it in the FIRST paragraph of the cover letter, not the last.
