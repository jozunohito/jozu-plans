## Most likely rejection reasons, ranked

### 1. Vertical pattern-match: Albright's network selects for cyber-product DNA (HIGH confidence)

**Evidence:** Julie Albright's 20-year career is 100% cybersecurity (Forgepoint, Bishop Fox, Insight Partners cyber portfolio, Resolve Systems). She personally hired the prior CRO (Strogis) from Okta/Cybereason — both pure cyber-product. The CMO-promoted-to-COO pattern means she controls hiring and trusts her own network.

Jeff's cyber resume shows CDK (Fortinet/Cisco Meraki/Cisco CMNA cert/endpoint+cloud+mail) and AT&T (global security infrastructure + WAN). This is **adjacent enterprise security**, not pure cyber-product. The JD lists cyber as PREFERRED not REQUIRED, but in an applicant pool with hundreds of candidates, "preferred" effectively becomes "required" at the resume-screen stage when the hiring manager has cyber-only DNA.

**Probability:** This is the dominant filter. Even with Jeff's $150M revenue and 7-figure deal history, a pure-cyber competitor with $40M revenue but Forescout/Armis/Axonius logos likely cleared the screen ahead of him.

### 2. Stage / scale pattern mismatch: Series A operator, not enterprise scale (HIGH confidence)

**Evidence:** runZero is **Series A**, 75 employees, $20M total raised, ~$25–50M ARR estimate (typical for late-Series-A CAASM challenger pre-Series B). They need a sales VP who has **built from $5M to $50M ARR**, run a 15-25 person sales org, and operated with one CRO peer. They are NOT a $200M ARR company.

Jeff's profile reads as **scaled enterprise IC + Fortune 500 named accounts** (UKG jumbo employers, Ushur AI workflow at $24M TCV, CalOptima $10M TCV). The pattern-match here is "scale operator who has carried a $25M number personally" not "founder-stage Series A VP who built the team from 4 to 18."

**Probability:** Strong secondary filter. Jeff's ceiling rule cuts the OTHER direction here: he was likely flagged as "too senior / built for $100M+ ARR companies, will leave when they hit Series B."

### 3. CRO tagline triggered "overqualified / flight risk" (MEDIUM confidence)

**Evidence:** The role title is **VP Sales**, reporting to COO. Jeff applied with the **CRO variant** resume. For a Series A company that just lost their CRO, a candidate signaling "Chief Revenue Officer" is read as:
- Wants the CRO seat, not the VP seat
- Will leave if not promoted within 12 months
- Won't take direction from a non-sales COO

The recruiter / Albright herself would pattern-match this as a flight risk and a likely friction point in the org chart.

**Probability:** Strong contributor. The right move here was the **VP Sales variant** with "VP Sales | Enterprise Sales Leadership" tagline, not the CRO variant.

### 4. Deal-size / motion mismatch: $25K–$250K ACV vs. Jeff's $1M+ TCV track (MEDIUM confidence)

**Evidence:** runZero pricing "starts at $5,000." Most platform contracts likely $25K–$250K ACV. Federal deals (Marine Corps tier) may go 7-figure but those are rare and run by Jim Kovach already.

Jeff's resume leads with $10M TCV CalOptima, $24M TCV across <2 years at Ushur, $1M IC commission years. For a hiring manager looking for someone who can run a high-velocity 50-90 day sales cycle with $50K–$150K ACVs, Jeff reads as "wrong gear ratio."

**Probability:** Real factor. The JD requirement for "personally closing seven-figure deals" is a flag that they DO want big-deal experience for the federal segment, but the bulk of the motion is mid-market velocity.

### 5. Competitive field: Rapid7/Tenable/Forescout/Armis/Axonius alumni in pool (HIGH confidence)

**Evidence:** Public posting on LinkedIn for a remote VP Sales role at a Series A cyber company in CAASM = **literally hundreds of applicants**. Among them will be 20-50 candidates with direct CAASM, vulnerability management, or asset-discovery experience: Rapid7 RVPs, Tenable Regional Directors, Forescout, Armis, Axonius, Qualys, Wiz alumni.

For Albright, choosing between "20-year cyber-only sales VP from Tenable who has run a $40M segment" vs. "25-year multi-vertical CRO with $150M career rev but no CAASM logos" is an easy choice on paper.

**Probability:** This is the implementation mechanism by which #1 and #2 actually played out. The pool was deep with on-pattern candidates.

### 6. Geography preference: Austin / Bay Area bias (LOW–MEDIUM confidence)

**Evidence:** runZero HQ is Austin, TX. The role is Remote per JD, but Albright (Texas roots — UT Austin Masters) may prefer in-region candidates for "executive presence" and on-site closing trips. Jeff in Coeur d'Alene, ID may have read as geographically inconvenient even with "remote OK."

**Probability:** Minor factor. The JD explicitly says Remote and accepts US/UK, so this is at most a tiebreaker.

## What this means for Jeff's next 20 applications

### Resume / positioning adjustments

1. **Build a dedicated VP Sales (not CRO) variant for Series A–Series B roles.** The CRO tagline kills mid-stage VP applications. Tagline: "VP Sales | Enterprise & Federal Sales Leadership." Save the CRO variant for $50M+ ARR companies hiring a true CRO seat.

2. **Build a "Cybersecurity & Network Infrastructure" variant** that surfaces:
   - CDK Global: Fortinet NGFW, Cisco Meraki MX/MR, CMNA cert, endpoint + cloud + mail security
   - AT&T 5 yrs: global security infrastructure + WAN sales to enterprise
   - Federal exposure (CalOptima public-payer work + any DOD/civilian touchpoints)
   - Frame the multi-vertical track as **"sold security into HCM, healthcare, automotive retail, and telecom — buyer is the same CISO/CIO across verticals."**
   - This is the resume that competes against pure-cyber sellers.

3. **Drop the $10M / $24M TCV headline numbers** when applying to **sub-$50M ARR Series A companies.** Lead instead with:
   - "Built Ushur AI workflow segment from $0 to $24M TCV in <2 years" (frames as zero-to-something operator)
   - "Top 5% global sellers at UKG (Fortune 500 + jumbo)" (frames as discipline + quota)
   - The big-ticket numbers REPEL Series A hiring managers who want a builder, not a Fortune-500 closer.

### Filter rules for the next 20 applications

4. **Decline applications to pure-CAASM / pure-EDR / pure-XDR specialists** (Armis, Axonius, Wiz, Tenable, Rapid7, Qualys, CrowdStrike adjacencies) UNLESS Jeff has a warm intro to the hiring manager. The hiring manager pattern-match is unbeatable cold.

5. **Target adjacent-cyber where Jeff's multi-vertical strength is an asset, not a liability:**
   - **Identity / IAM / IGA** (Okta, Saviynt, Beyond Identity, Transmit Security) — buyer overlaps with HCM (Workday/UKG identity)
   - **Data security / DLP / DSPM** (Cyera, Varonis, BigID) — buyer is CISO + CDO, multi-vertical fluency helps
   - **Mobile cyber** (iVerify already on Jeff's pipeline) — federal + enterprise crossover
   - **GRC / TPRM / continuous compliance** (Vanta, Drata, OneTrust, Bitsight) — Vanta already submitted, this is the right band
   - **Healthcare-specific cyber** (Claroty Medigate, Imprivata, Medcrypt) — Jeff's CalOptima + healthcare + cyber combo is a unique trifecta
   - **Network / SASE / SD-WAN / Zero Trust networking** (Cato, Netskope, Zscaler regional) — direct AT&T WAN match

6. **Apply Jeff's $100M ARR ceiling rule correctly.** runZero was NOT $100M+ ARR. It is Series A, ~$25–50M ARR estimate. The actual rejection signal is the **opposite** problem: Jeff was over-pattern for a Series A builder seat. Refine the rule:
   - **AVOID** companies $100M+ ARR (Jeff lacks the scale-to-$200M proof)
   - **ALSO AVOID** Series A companies at $5M-$25M ARR where they want a player-coach builder (Jeff's scale reads as "won't roll up sleeves")
   - **TARGET** Series B / late-Series-B / Series C at $25M-$100M ARR — this is the band where Jeff's scale + multi-vertical + 7-figure deal track is a match, not a mismatch

7. **Pre-screen for the hiring manager's network on every application.** Pull the COO/CRO/CEO LinkedIn before applying. If their last 3 jobs are in a single sub-vertical Jeff hasn't lived in, the cold app will not clear the screen. Either route warm or skip.

### Process adjustments

8. **Plan-as-reserve rule confirmed.** Do not lead with the runZero plan even if asked. Lead with the resume + tight cover note tailored to the hiring manager's network signal. Plan goes to round 2.

9. **For runZero specifically: do not re-apply.** The decline is cold-screen rejection, not interview rejection. Re-application without a warm intro to Albright or HD Moore is wasted effort. If Jeff wants this lane, the path is a referral from a Bishop Fox / Forgepoint / Decibel Partners alumnus.
