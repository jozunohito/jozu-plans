## Story 1 - California Health Plan (wildfire reframe)

**Headline:** $200K commoditized RFP reframed to $1.4M ARR via continuity-of-care language.

**Key facts**
- Large regional health plan, ~600,000 members.
- Region: frequent wildfires.
- RFP framing: per-transaction outbound messaging during disaster events.
- Competitive bids: ~$0.06 per message.
- RFP-implied deal size: ~$200K.
- Closed: ~$1.4M ARR, multi-year. Roughly 7x.

**The unstated regulatory wall**
- Every outbound message requires state and federal pre-approval.
- Pre-approval cycles run multi-month.
- During an active wildfire, there is no time to procure new messaging.
- Capacity must be pre-approved and pre-staged, or it does not exist when needed.

**The reframe move**
- Rejected the RFP framing at the outset.
- Routed conversation away from procurement.
- New stakeholders: CMO, General Counsel, Government Affairs.
- New budget category: regulatory readiness and risk management, not unit messaging.
- New offer shape: platform subscription, pre-approved workflows, effectively unlimited capacity, multi-year term.

**Named techniques used**
- Five-level structured Discovery, surface symptom down to Revenue Floor.
- Power Messaging (Why This / Why Now anchored to regulatory exposure).
- Plan Letter as the container, with the Mutual Sequence of Events inside it routing the buying committee.
- MEDDPICC running inside the broader framework.

**Snowball translation punchline**
- "Quote me per video, compare to my ad agency on CPM" is the equivalent of "$0.06 per message."
- Reframe: Snowball sells owned-media infrastructure, not videos. CFO will price standing brand-equity capability. Procurement will not.

## Story 2 - US Radiology (OCR to revenue infrastructure)

**Headline:** $30K commodity OCR ask reframed to $1.6M ARR (~$7M TCV) via category expansion and incumbent displacement.

**Key facts**
- Large venture-backed national radiology organization, consolidating imaging centers.
- 5.5M faxed physician orders per year, manually processed.
- Initial ask: OCR. Pricing ~$0.005/page.
- Initial deal as scoped: ~$30K annually.
- Closed: $1.6M ARR, multi-year, ~$7M TCV.
- Cycle: ~40 meetings, ~12 on-site, 6-8 months.

**The unstated revenue physics**
- MRI and CT machines are venture-funded capital assets.
- Their unit economics depend entirely on utilization.
- Centers competed internally to first-contact the patient.
- Slow center loses the patient or the no-show kills the slot.
- Idle machines and missed first-touches add up to seven-figure annual revenue loss.

**The reframe move**
- Walked Discovery up five levels: OCR accuracy, operational delay, first-touch loss, idle-machine revenue loss, board-visible utilization thesis.
- Each level buyer-validated.
- Stakeholder expansion: from IT to CIO, COO, CFO, Operations leadership.
- Scope expansion: from OCR to full intake-to-engagement workflow.

**Incumbent displacement**
- Incumbent owned the patient engagement layer with strong board relationships.
- Did not fight them inside their scope.
- Expanded above (intake, validation, routing) and below (engagement workflows).
- Anchored the deal to the incumbent's contract renewal as the forcing function.
- Redirected already-approved spend into a broader integrated platform.

**Named techniques used**
- Five-level structured Discovery.
- Value Wedge: full-pipeline ownership no point-solution vendor could match.
- Power Messaging (Why This / Why Now anchored to utilization economics).
- Plan Letter discipline at every meeting (~40 of them) with the Mutual Sequence of Events inside it.
- MEDDPICC running inside the framework throughout the long cycle.

**Disney fist-bump moment**
- After walking COO and CFO through the utilization math on-site.
- COO turned and gave a fist bump across the table.
- The signal that the buyer felt understood, not sold to.

**Snowball translation punchline**
- "We want one video, here is our budget for one" is the OCR ask.
- Reframe: brand does not have a video problem; brand has an owned-media revenue infrastructure problem.
- Different budget line, different stakeholder, different scope.
- Where there is an agency of record on retainer, anchor the new deal to that contract's renewal.

## Story 3 - CalOptima (threshold entry to enterprise expansion)

**Headline:** $249K threshold-constrained land expanded to ~$3M ARR / ~$10M TCV. Largest deal in company history.

**Key facts**
- Large regional Medicaid health plan, ~600-700K members.
- End of COVID public health emergency, hard redetermination window.
- Initial ask: Medicaid redetermination outreach.
- Surface deal size: ~$360K.
- Buyer's non-RFP procurement threshold: $250K. Anything above triggers formal multi-month RFP.
- Initial land: $249K. Deliberate, just under threshold.
- Enterprise RFP budget: ~$3.8M/year.
- Closed: ~$3M ARR, just under $10M TCV. Largest deal in company history.

**The four-part play**

1. **Threshold-Constrained Entry.** $249K to fit under the buyer's own procurement rule and act inside the redetermination window. Sequencing, not discounting.

2. **Active Discovery during the land.** Live operational presence to meet stakeholders outside Member Experience. Clinical, Quality, Government Affairs, Compliance. Plan Letter after every meeting.

3. **Partner Orchestration.** Brought in a predictive analytics firm that was originally a competing bidder, as subcontractor under our prime. Added Rex Wallace Consulting as the operational execution partner. Three firms, one prime, one bid scoped at the Root Constraint, not the stated problem.

4. **Internal Resistance + Procurement Discipline.** Refused leadership's pressure to narrow the bid to messaging only (~$1M ceiling). Refused side-channel outreach during the formal RFP silent period. Held discipline. Won on the merits.

**The hidden enterprise problem**
- Redetermination was the visible tip.
- Underneath: significant Star Ratings decline costing the plan hundreds of millions in CMS reimbursement exposure.
- No integrated member intelligence layer.
- Cross-functional fragmentation across Clinical, Quality, Member Experience, IT, Gov Affairs.

**Named techniques used**
- Threshold-Constrained Entry.
- Five-level structured Discovery.
- Value Wedge: prime-contractor scope no single vendor could match.
- Power Messaging (Why This / Why Now anchored to PHE deadline + Star Ratings exposure).
- Plan Letter at every stage (initial engagement, RFI, RFP) with the Mutual Sequence of Events inside it carrying the buying committee.
- MEDDPICC running throughout.
- Procurement Discipline as a doctrine: silence during formal procurement is compliance, not disengagement.

**Snowball translation punchline**
- 50K-150K test buy is the equivalent of the $249K land.
- Use it to get inside, execute, run real discovery on what is actually broken.
- 12 months later when the brand's agency-of-record renewal hits, you are the incumbent with delivery proof.
- The conversation moves from one video to whether the brand becomes a studio.
