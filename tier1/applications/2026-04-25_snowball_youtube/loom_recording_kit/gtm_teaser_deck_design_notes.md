# GTM Teaser Deck — Design Notes

**Companion to:** `gtm_teaser_deck.md`
**Purpose:** Production guidance so Jeff can build the deck in 30 minutes flat without designer help.

---

## Recommended template

**First choice: Google Slides, default "Simple Light" theme.**
- Free, exports to PDF and PPTX cleanly, opens on any device, works in the Loom screen-share without font-substitution issues.
- Strip the theme down to a blank master before adding content. No logos, no decorative shapes.

**Second choice: Keynote, "White" theme.**
- Use only if Jeff is recording the Loom on a Mac and prefers native. Same rule: strip to blank master.

**Avoid for this deck:** Tome, Pitch, Beautiful.ai, Canva. They auto-insert decorative elements that read as marketing-deck instead of operator-grade. The whole point is the absence of flair.

---

## Fonts (system sans only)

- **Primary:** Inter (download free from rsms.me/inter) OR system default sans (Arial on Windows, Helvetica Neue on macOS, Roboto on Google Slides default).
- **Weights:** Regular 400 for body. Semibold 600 for headers. No italics. No light weights below 400 — they wash out in Loom compression.
- **Sizes (1080p safe):**
  - Slide title: 36–40 pt
  - Column headers (Day 30 / Day 60 / Day 90): 24 pt semibold
  - Body bullets: 18 pt regular
  - Footer / source notes: 12 pt regular, 60% opacity

---

## Color palette

- **Background (Slides 1, 3, 4):** off-white. Hex `#F7F7F4` or `#FAFAFA`. Not pure white — pure white blows out on Loom recording.
- **Background (Slide 2 only):** near-black. Hex `#0E0E10` or `#111111`. Not pure black.
- **Primary text:** near-black `#111111` on light slides. Off-white `#F7F7F4` on the dark slide.
- **Accent (use sparingly — column headers, hr lines):** muted deep green `#2F6B4F` OR muted blue `#27496D`. Pick one and use it on every slide.
- **Do NOT use:** Snowball's brand colors. Bright reds, oranges, yellows. Gradients. Drop shadows. Glow effects.

Recommended: muted deep green `#2F6B4F`. It reads as confident and operator-grade, not marketing-bright, and does not compete with anything Snowball is likely doing visually in their own materials.

---

## 1080p safe-margin guidance

- Loom records at 1920x1080 in most setups. Build the deck at 16:9.
- Keep all content inside a 1700x920 safe zone (110 px margin top/bottom, 110 px margin left/right). Anything outside that risks getting cropped by the Loom UI overlay (controls, webcam bubble).
- Do not place the webcam bubble (bottom-right corner by default in Loom) over body text. If Jeff's webcam stays bottom-right during Beat 3, leave the bottom-right 320x320 region of every slide empty of critical content.
- Test by exporting one slide as PNG at 1920x1080 and viewing at 100% on the recording monitor before starting the take.

---

## 30-minute production plan

**Minutes 0–5:** Open Google Slides, create new deck, set theme to Simple Light, blank master, set slide size to 16:9 widescreen.
**Minutes 5–15:** Build Slide 1 (the three-column milestones). This is the heaviest slide. Get the column widths equal, the bullet spacing tight, the Day 30 / 60 / 90 headers in the accent color.
**Minutes 15–20:** Build Slide 2 (the black slide). One text block, centered. Drop in the calendar link or contact line in the lower third.
**Minutes 20–25:** Build Slide 3 (three-bullet category-win argument).
**Minutes 25–30:** Build Slide 4 (two-column hire/don't-hire), add the footer line, do one full preview pass at 1080p, fix any text that runs outside the safe zone.

**Total: 30 minutes if Jeff resists the urge to redesign anything.**

---

## Recording notes

- Open the deck in presenter mode before starting Loom. Do not screen-share the editor view.
- During Beat 3 (6:30–10:30), spend roughly one minute per slide. Slide 2 is the shortest — it's a transition beat to the call-to-action, not a content beat.
- Do not narrate the bullets word-for-word. The deck is the artifact; the voice-over is the meta-commentary about what Hashemi is seeing.
- After Beat 3, return to webcam-only for Beat 4 (the close).

---

## Hard rules honored

- No methodology revealed on the deck itself. Each milestone is an outcome; the method behind it is reserved for the call.
- No personal earnings figures. The Slide 3 bullet 3 lists operating numbers only ($24M TCV, $10M largest deal, $150M career revenue) — these are the public-facing numbers per the never-share-personal-earnings rule.
- No fabricated Snowball internals. The Slide 1 footer explicitly anchors the assumptions to public signals.
- No em dashes in body copy. Em dashes have been replaced with periods or commas throughout.
