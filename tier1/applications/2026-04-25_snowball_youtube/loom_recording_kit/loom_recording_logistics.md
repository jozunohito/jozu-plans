# Loom Recording Logistics

**For:** Jeff Aragon, Coeur d'Alene home office.
**Recording date:** Tomorrow, 2026-04-26.
**Goal:** Single-take 12-minute Loom that reads operator-to-operator, not produced. Authenticity beats polish for this audience.

---

## Loom settings

- **Mode:** Camera + screen. Camera on, screen optional. The talking_points.md file open on a second monitor for glance reference, not screen-shared. The video is YOU on camera, full bubble.
- **Camera bubble size:** Full screen, not floating thumbnail. Ryan needs to read your face for 12 minutes; do not bury yourself in a corner.
- **Resolution:** 1080p HD. Open Loom settings, confirm before pressing record.
- **Frame rate:** Default (Loom auto-handles).
- **Mic check:** Speak a 30-second test, play it back. Listen for room hum, HVAC, refrigerator hum from the kitchen, dog tags, anything mechanical.
- **Pause / restart:** If you fumble a number badly in the first 90 seconds, stop and restart. After 2 minutes in, push through. Self-correction reads authentic; restart kills the energy.
- **Take count:** Plan for take 2 to be the keeper. Take 1 is the warm-up. Take 3 only if take 2 has a hard error.

---

## Lighting

- **Best:** Natural window light from the front, slightly off-camera-left or off-camera-right. Position the desk so the window is in front of you, not behind you. Backlit kills the shot every time.
- **Time of day:** Mid-morning is best in Coeur d'Alene this time of year. Soft directional light, not harsh midday sun.
- **Avoid:** Overhead fluorescent, overhead recessed cans directly above the head. Creates raccoon-eye shadows and ages the speaker by ten years on camera.
- **If natural light is not available:** A single warm bulb desk lamp pointed at the wall behind the camera (bounce light), not at your face. No ring lights. Ring lights read "creator audition" and Ryan will spot it.

---

## Background

- Your home office in Coeur d'Alene is the right setting. Ryan operates out of Venice, CA, in a working office. He understands operator backgrounds.
- **Show:** Books on shelves. A framed degree or two if visible. Personal items that signal you are a real human: a photo, an instrument, a piece of art that means something. These are warmth signals.
- **Hide:** Visible clutter. Stacks of paper. A messy whiteboard with someone else's deal info. A laundry basket. Empty water bottles. Anything corporate-branded with a former employer logo.
- **Do not stage a fake bookshelf or video-conference background.** Ryan reads taste in three seconds. Virtual backgrounds are out.
- **Distance from background:** ~4-6 feet between you and the wall behind. Creates depth, prevents the flat "ID photo" look.

---

## Camera position

- **Eye level.** Camera lens should be roughly the height of your eyes when you sit upright. Not laptop-down (chin shot, looking down at viewer). Not too high (looking up like an interrogation).
- **If using laptop camera:** Stack books under the laptop until the camera lens hits eye level. Cheap, works, no extra equipment.
- **If using external webcam:** Mount on a small tripod or monitor arm at eye level.
- **Distance:** Camera should be 24-36 inches from your face. Closer than 24 inches creates a fish-eye distortion that ages and softens features.
- **Frame:** From mid-chest up. Do not shoot full-face close-up; that is interrogation framing. Do not shoot full-body wide; that is corporate-video framing.

---

## Audio

- **Best:** AirPods Pro or any decent wired headset mic. Crisp voice, eliminates room noise. If using AirPods, confirm Loom is recording from AirPod mic, not laptop mic, in Loom audio settings.
- **Acceptable:** Built-in laptop mic in a quiet room with the laptop within 18 inches of your mouth. Test it first. Listen for room echo.
- **Avoid:** Bluetooth speakerphone speakers. Headphone mics from old wired earbuds with broken cables. Anything that crackles.
- **Pre-record check:** Do a 30-second test, play back through laptop speakers AND through your phone. The phone playback is closer to how Ryan will hear it on his device.

---

## Wardrobe

- **Wear:** A solid-color collared shirt. Navy, charcoal, white, or muted earth tone. No logos, no branding. Pressed but not formal.
- **Do not wear:** A suit jacket. A tie. A blazer. This is operator-to-operator; corporate uniforms read as "trying too hard for a creative role."
- **Also do not wear:** A T-shirt or hoodie. Ryan's audience is creator-adjacent but the role is enterprise-sales-leader. Split the difference; collared casual is correct.
- **Glasses:** If you wear them, wear them. Do not put them on for the camera if you do not normally; reads inauthentic.

---

## Pre-record warm-up (do this 30 minutes before the keeper take)

1. **Read the script aloud once, sitting at your desk, fully out loud.** Not silently. Voice needs to wake up.
2. **Do one full 12-minute run-through TO CAMERA, recording in Loom. DO NOT save it.** Delete after the run-through. The warm-up gets the early-take stiffness out of your delivery.
3. **Take a 5-minute break.** Walk around. Do not stay locked in front of the camera.
4. **One sip of water before take 2 (the keeper).** Not a full glass. You do not want to mid-sentence-cough at minute 7.
5. **Phone face down. Slack closed. Email closed. All notifications muted.** A Slack ping at minute 9 is a re-record.

---

## Recording day routine

- **Morning:** Light breakfast. Skip heavy carbs (afternoon energy crash if recording is later). Black coffee or tea is fine. No new caffeine experiments today.
- **Hydration:** Water 60-90 minutes before recording, not in the 15 minutes before. Otherwise you will need a bathroom break mid-take.
- **Voice:** Talk to someone for 5-10 minutes before recording. Get the voice warm. Recording cold from silence sounds robotic for the first 90 seconds.
- **Mood:** This is not a high-stakes interview. This is one Loom out of many applications you will send. Pressure off. The energy you bring to the camera should be "I want to share something interesting with someone who will appreciate it."

---

## What to do mid-take if something breaks

- **Doorbell rings:** Stop, restart. Do not push through.
- **Dog barks / cat walks across keyboard:** Stop, restart.
- **Slack ping despite the mute:** Note the time, push through, keep going if the audio captured it cleanly. Loom does not pick up most desktop notifications if mic is on AirPods.
- **You fumble a dollar amount:** Self-correct in real time. "1.4 million. Sorry, just over 1.4." Push through.
- **You lose your place in the script:** Pause, breathe, look at the talking_points.md card on the second monitor, find the next beat, continue. A 3-second pause reads thoughtful, not stuck.
- **Your voice cracks or sounds tight:** Push through. Do not clear your throat on camera; sounds amateur. Sip water at the next natural beat (between Story 2 and Story 3 is the natural water break).

## Submit settings

- **Visibility:** Unlisted. Anyone with the link can watch; not searchable, not in your public Loom feed.
- **Password protection:** Off. Adding a password creates friction for Ryan and looks paranoid.
- **Custom thumbnail:** Use the auto-generated still from a clean moment in the recording. Do not design a YouTube-style thumbnail; this is operator-to-operator, not a content piece.
- **Title in Loom:** "Jeff Aragon to Ryan Hashemi - Three Reframes for Snowball - 12 min Loom"
- **Description in Loom:** Short. "Companion Loom for Founding Head of Sales application. Three real reframes from my deal book, translated to Snowball's brand-CMO motion."
- **Sharing:** Copy the Loom URL. Paste into the Snowball application form's "anything else / additional materials" field, or into your cover note as a clickable link. Do not embed; URL is enough.

---

## Final check before pressing share

1. Watch the first 30 seconds and the last 60 seconds of the keeper take. Most viewers (including Ryan) will scrub through the middle; opening and closing carry the most weight.
2. Confirm the audio is clean across both bookends.
3. Confirm your eye contact is steady, not darting.
4. Confirm you said "will" not "would" in the close.
5. Confirm you did NOT enumerate Plan Letter and Mutual Sequence of Events as peers.
6. Copy URL. Send.

---

## Post-record

- Save the keeper take to your Loom library, do not delete it for at least 90 days.
- Note the Loom URL in this folder under a `submission_log.md` or similar (track when sent, to whom).
- Do not refresh the Loom analytics every five minutes. Once a day at most. Watching the watch-count is a productivity tax.

---

## What "good" looks like

- Take 2, ~11:45 to 12:15 in length.
- Eye contact ~70 percent of run-time.
- One natural water sip between Story 2 and Story 3.
- One genuine smile during the Disney fist-bump beat.
- Three to five strategic pauses, never longer than 1.5 seconds.
- No "uhms" longer than half a second.
- No re-records past take 3.

If take 2 lands clean, ship it. Done is better than perfect, and a 4-hour Loom that ships beats a 16-hour video that does not.
