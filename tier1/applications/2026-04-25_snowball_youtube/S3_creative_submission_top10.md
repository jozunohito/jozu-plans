## Why "just send the resume" loses this specific application

The JD is not asking for creative because Snowball is whimsical. It's asking for creative because:

1. **It's a screening filter.** Most candidates who can't write past a resume will not apply. The creative submission is the first qualifying gate.
2. **The job IS creative submissions.** Day-1 work as Founding Head of Sales is producing custom deliverables for Fortune 500 CMOs that don't look like agency RFPs. The application IS the work sample.
3. **Ryan Hashemi's lens is taste-based.** A Jubilee founder reading a stock resume cover letter will pattern-match Jeff to "every other corporate sales VP" and pass. The creative submission is the only path to differentiation in a 30+ inbound week.
4. **Jeff's track record is too good for the standard package.** $150M career rev, $24M TCV at Ushur, $10M CalOptima, US Radiology, California health plan — these stories DIE in resume-bullet format. They breathe in narrative. The creative submission is the medium that lets Jeff's actual edge show.

If Jeff sends a resume + cover letter, he is competing on credentials with 200 other founder-tier sales VPs. If Jeff sends Idea #1 (or #1+#2+#5), he is the only candidate Ryan watched for 12 minutes that week. That is the entire game.
