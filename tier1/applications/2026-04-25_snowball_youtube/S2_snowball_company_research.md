## Methodology — Snowball's Articulated Thinking

From The Marketing Millennials podcast (Ep. 297, December 2024), netinfluencer profile, Rachel Karten newsletter (milkkarten.net), Foundr/leveling-up newsletter, and the homepage:

**1. Engagement model.** 12–18 month engagement. Clients pay Snowball to build an in-house team — Snowball trains them, exits, hands off. Not a traditional outsourced production agency.

**2. Sequence.** (a) Discovery (industry, competition, audience research); (b) Talent hiring (often filmmakers / commercial directors with NO YouTube experience — storytelling is harder to teach than YouTube mechanics); (c) Content roadmap; (d) Knowledge transfer until client is independent.

**3. Packaging-first.** Thumbnail and title BEFORE filming. "If you cannot think about how this awesome idea is very interesting to someone to click... it's not a good video." Brands should dedicate ~40% of effort to packaging upfront.

**4. Two metrics drive ~80% of outcomes.** Click-through rate (CTR) and Average View Duration (AVD/retention). Secondary: subscribers per thousand views, returning viewership, engagement rate.

**5. Three ideation frameworks.**
- Emotion Mapping — branch from topic to identify the strongest human emotions driving viewership.
- Format Adaption — port proven formats across genres.
- Outliers — identify underperforming channels with breakout videos and replicate the structure.

**6. Investment math (Hashemi's public budget guide).**
- Long-form team: 1 producer ($9–10K/mo) + 1 editor ($8–9K/mo) + production = one quality 10–15 min video every 2–3 weeks.
- Short-form team: 1 creator ($10K/mo) + part-time support ($2–3K/mo) = 20–30 pieces/month.
- Typical client annualized creative+production budget: ~$400K.

**7. Thesis: organic owned-media compounds.** Hashemi: "YouTube is the most powerful platform by far. The audience is stickier, they're more engaged. You can convert your audience significantly better over time." Five-year vision: brands as content studios, not interruptive advertisers.

**8. Operating constraint.** Snowball accepts ~2 new clients per month. ~90% of pipeline is inbound from Hashemi's LinkedIn + X. **This is the central commercial fact relevant to Jeff's role:** the constraint is not lead gen; it is qualifying, sequencing, pricing, and operationalizing inbound demand into the right client mix and capacity plan.

## Competitive Landscape

Snowball does not have a direct, identical competitor — most adjacent firms operate in different lanes:

- **Whalar** — creator-led, more short-form / social, paid-amplification heavy. Not a long-form owned-channel build-out specialist.
- **Studio71** — multi-channel network / talent management / monetization, not a methodology-licensing build-out shop.
- **Spotter** — capital-deployment / catalog-licensing for top creators (e.g., MrBeast). Different business model entirely.
- **Beast Industries / MrBeast Studios** — in-house at MrBeast, not a service business.
- **Hilo Media, Single Grain, joseangelostudios, inBeat** — YouTube production / ads agencies; mostly project- or media-buy-based, not 12–18-month internal-team build-outs.
- **Traditional Condé Nast / WIRED / Vogue franchise teams** — Snowball is essentially trying to externalize that capability into client brands. Snowball's team comes from these very franchises, which is the structural moat.

**Snowball's positioning:** The only firm explicitly selling "we will build your YouTube team and exit" rather than "we will run your YouTube channel for you." That is the differentiator and what Jeff should be able to articulate cleanly.

## Five Discovery Questions for Jeff (Specific, Research-Informed)

1. **"You've said publicly that ~90% of pipeline is inbound from your LinkedIn + X, and you currently take roughly two new clients a month. What does the funnel actually look like — how many of those 30+ inbound brands a month do you reply to, how many get a discovery call, and where are you losing the right ones?"**
   *Why this question:* It demonstrates Jeff has read the netinfluencer interview, anchors the conversation on the actual revenue bottleneck, and lets Hashemi reveal whether the constraint is qualification, capacity, or pricing.

2. **"For a 12–18 month engagement, what's your current pricing architecture — pure retainer, milestone-based, performance-tied to subscriber/view targets — and where does it break? My instinct is that with a build-and-exit model, the unit you should be selling is outcome-priced cohorts of clients, not seat-based retainer."**
   *Why this question:* Surfaces Snowball's monetization assumption, signals Jeff is thinking commercial-model not just BD, ties to his enterprise-revenue doctrine.

3. **"You've said three of your largest accounts are B2B SaaS. As a founding sales hire, would you rather I tighten the ICP around B2B SaaS where you have the strongest case studies (Proton, Nectar HR), or keep the aperture wide across creator-personalities, CPG, manufacturers — and what's the strategic reason for that choice?"**
   *Why this question:* Forces a positioning conversation, demonstrates Jeff knows the named clients, frames it as the founder's call (Hashemi will respect that).

4. **"The build-and-exit model means every successful engagement ends. What's the renewal/expansion motion — second show, second region, second team, or are you intentionally net-zero on a given client after handoff? My read is that the LTV math and the GTM hire profile look very different depending on which one you actually want."**
   *Why this question:* Hits the core economic question of the business that no one else applying will likely raise. Shows Jeff is thinking revenue architecture, not pipeline volume.

5. **"You're hiring a Creative Director, Creative Strategist, Creative Recruiter, Video Editor, and Head of Sales simultaneously. What's the constraint right now — do you need me to sell more to fund hiring, or do you need the delivery org built out before I can close more?"**
   *Why this question:* Shows Jeff has actually read the job board, treats the role as one node in a system rather than a standalone slot, and lets Hashemi reveal whether this is a sales-led or delivery-led growth phase.
