## 1. BTP Overview (verified)

- **Legal/operating name:** Black Tech Pipeline (BTP)
- **Founder & CEO:** Pariss Chandler (publicly known as Pariss Athena). Verified on BTP About page and her LinkedIn.
- **Founded:** 2020. Origin: viral tweet "What does Black Twitter in Tech look like?"
- **URL:** https://blacktechpipeline.com
- **Newsletter:** https://blacktechpipeline.substack.com (10,000+ subscribers per Substack landing page)
- **Business model (3 lines per BTP About page + TechCrunch profile):**
  1. Job board (companies pay to post)
  2. Recruitment / sourcing / consulting service
  3. Speaker connections for events
  - Plus a "TalentMatch" recruitment platform and a 90-day post-hire retention/check-in consulting offer.
- **Stated audience:** "Qualified Black tech talent" / Black technologists. Marketing language is engineering-leaning, but BTP does not explicitly limit roles to engineering on its public About page. Job board has historically included non-engineering roles in tech companies.
- **Operating status (2026):** Active. Site live, social channels active (LinkedIn @black-tech-pipeline, X @BTPipeline, IG @btpipeline), Substack still publishing, demo Calendly link live (calendly.com/parisschandler/30-min-call). Total partners since launch: "200+" per BTP's own copy.

## 5. Recommendation

**Pick: (c) Skip — with one micro-exception below.**

Reasoning:
- The dropdown listing is the only signal. There is no public evidence BTP holds the Versori USA Head of Sales search. The 2023 Avalara/BTP relationship was for engineering roles in Durham, not GTM leadership.
- Pitching Pariss Chandler directly with a "saw BTP listed on Versori's app, are you working it?" note risks looking like (i) a cold ask from outside her audience, and (ii) presuming a relationship that may not exist on this specific role.
- Given BTP's explicit mission centers on Black technologists, a non-Black senior sales operator pitching them on a parallel-path basis is a misfire risk. (See section 6.)

**Micro-exception (optional, low effort):** When Jeff submits the Avalara/Versori application, he can simply select "Black Tech Pipeline" in the referral dropdown if it accurately reflects how he heard about the role (he saw BTP listed there — that is a kind of self-referential truth, but the dropdown's intent is to capture genuine referral channels, so this is borderline; recommend selecting "LinkedIn" or "Other" if more accurate).

**Do NOT** DM Pariss or BTP about this role. Low ROI, mission misalignment, no signal.

## 6. Audience-Fit Honesty for Jeff

- **BTP's mission:** Connect Black technologists (engineers, data, security, product) with hiring companies. Mission language centers on Black tech talent retention and DEI-committed employers.
- **Jeff's profile:** 25-year senior sales operator, Tier 1 CRO/Head of Sales target. Not an engineer. Not BTP's stated target demographic on either dimension (function or identity).
- **Honest assessment:** Jeff is tangential to BTP's core audience. Even if BTP held the Versori search, BTP's value to Avalara/Versori on this role would be sourcing Black GTM leadership candidates — a narrower brief than Jeff's profile satisfies. Pitching BTP positions Jeff as outside their mission and dilutes their value to their actual partners.
- **Better parallel-path channels for this role:** Pavilion (CRO community), Bowery Capital / OpenView talent networks, Heidrick / True Search / Daversa for enterprise sales leadership, direct LinkedIn outreach to Avalara/Versori execs (CEO Versori, Avalara CRO), and Versori investors' talent partners. These are higher-signal than BTP for Jeff.
