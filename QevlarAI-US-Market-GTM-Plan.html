Company Overview

Qevlar AI is a Paris-based autonomous SOC platform that uses agentic AI to automate and deepen security investigations. The company raised $30M in Series A funding co-led by Partech and Forgepoint Capital, with participation from EQT Ventures. This follows a $14M seed round led by EQT Ventures and Forgepoint Capital in 2024.

The platform autonomously enriches, correlates, and investigates security signals, expanding security team capacity while reducing incident response times. Qevlar deploys in hours, not months, and integrates with existing SIEM, EDR, XDR, and SOAR systems via API.

02 - Market Analysis

US Cybersecurity and Autonomous SOC Landscape

Market Sizing

AI SOC Automation Segment

The autonomous SOC category is emerging from within the broader SIEM/SOAR market. US enterprises spend billions annually on security operations, yet the average SOC analyst handles 50+ alerts per day with manual investigation. The efficiency gap creates a massive addressable market for AI-driven investigation platforms.

Market Tailwinds

  • SOC analyst burnout crisis - Average SOC analyst turnover exceeds 30% annually. Organizations cannot hire fast enough to cover the alert volume, creating urgent demand for automation that actually reduces workload
  • Alert fatigue epidemic - Enterprise SOCs receive thousands of alerts daily, with over 80% being false positives. Manual triage is unsustainable and causes real threats to be missed
  • Cybersecurity talent shortage - Over 500,000 unfilled cybersecurity positions in the US alone. AI-driven investigation is not a luxury, it is the only viable path to adequate coverage
  • Board-level visibility - SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules have elevated security operations to a board conversation, increasing budget availability for solutions that demonstrably improve detection and response

Buyer Landscape

Segment Primary Buyer Key Pain Point Qevlar Fit
Enterprise (10,000+ employees) CISO, VP Security Operations Alert volume overwhelming SOC capacity, analyst burnout, slow MTTR Autonomous investigation at scale, API integration with existing stack
Mid-Market (1,000-10,000) Director of Security, IT Security Manager Cannot justify full SOC headcount, relying on understaffed teams Force multiplier for small security teams, rapid deployment
MSSPs VP Service Delivery, SOC Director Scaling investigation capacity across hundreds of clients profitably Multi-tenant platform enabling MSSPs to handle more clients with existing staff
Core Market Thesis
The US cybersecurity market is moving from "detect and alert" to "detect, investigate, and resolve." Qevlar sits at the investigation layer, the most labor-intensive and error-prone step in the security operations workflow. Every US enterprise running a SOC is a potential buyer because the problem Qevlar solves is universal: too many alerts, not enough analysts, and the ones you have are burning out.

06 - 90-Day GTM Execution Plan

From European Success to US Market Entry

This plan builds the commercial foundation for Qevlar's US market entry. The goal is not to hire a large US team immediately. It is to validate positioning, build initial pipeline, and establish the partnerships that will generate revenue before a permanent US sales organization is in place.

Phase 1: Days 1-30, Foundation and Positioning

US Market Positioning

  • Refine messaging for US buyers. European cybersecurity messaging often leads with technology capability. US CISOs buy based on risk reduction, compliance outcomes, and analyst productivity metrics. Reframe all messaging around these outcomes
  • Build competitive battle cards for CrowdStrike Charlotte, Palo Alto XSIAM, Prophet Security, and Dropzone AI. Each card needs positioning, objection responses, and proof points specific to US buyer concerns
  • Develop a "stack-agnostic" value proposition that positions Qevlar as complementary to, not competitive with, existing security investments. US buyers will not rip out CrowdStrike to buy Qevlar. They will add Qevlar on top

Target Account List Development

  • Build a named account list of 200 US enterprises with known SOC operations, prioritized by alert volume, analyst headcount, and technology stack compatibility
  • Identify 25 US MSSPs and MDR providers actively seeking investigation automation capabilities
  • Map decision makers at top 50 accounts: CISO, VP Security Operations, SOC Director
Phase 2: Days 31-60, Pipeline Generation and Channel Development

Outbound Pipeline Generation

  • Launch targeted outbound sequences to CISO and VP Security Operations personas at companies with 5,000+ employees. Lead with the analyst productivity thesis: "Your SOC analysts spend 80% of their time investigating alerts that turn out to be nothing. Qevlar automates that investigation"
  • Execute account-based campaigns targeting financial services, healthcare, and technology verticals where SOC pressure is highest and budgets are available
  • Build a conference and event strategy around RSA Conference, Black Hat, and regional CISO roundtables. In-person credibility is essential for a European vendor entering the US security market

MSSP Channel Activation

  • Initiate partnership discussions with 3-5 priority US MSSPs. Use Orange Cyberdefense and Atos case studies as proof of MSSP channel success
  • Develop a US-specific MSSP partner program: deal registration, margin structure, co-sell support, and technical enablement
  • Build an MSSP-specific demo environment showing multi-tenant investigation capabilities and per-client reporting
Phase 3: Days 61-90, Close First Deals and Scale

Deal Progression and Closing

  • Convert initial POCs into paid contracts. Target 3-5 enterprise POCs in progress by day 60, with at least 2 converting to annual contracts by day 90
  • Close first US MSSP partnership with at least one signed partner agreement and a joint go-to-market plan
  • Complete AWS and Azure Marketplace listings to enable frictionless procurement for enterprise buyers

Hiring and Infrastructure

  • Hire first US-based Account Executive with cybersecurity sales experience. Location in New York, San Francisco, or Washington DC depending on target account concentration
  • Establish US-based SE capacity, either through a dedicated hire or by training an existing European SE to cover US time zones
  • Set up US entity, banking, and contracting infrastructure to support direct sales without requiring European legal involvement in every deal
