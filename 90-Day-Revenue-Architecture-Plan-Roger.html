The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

Phase 1: Days 1-30
Foundation
✓ Buyer definition. Segment your ICP by company stage, sales team size, and outbound maturity. A 5-person startup buying AI SDR has different needs than a 50-person sales org replacing manual BDRs. Define who buys, who influences, and what triggers the purchase decision.
✓ Power Message. Build a message that quantifies the economics of AI-driven outbound versus human BDRs. Not "save time on prospecting." Instead: the cost per qualified meeting, ramp time eliminated, and pipeline generated per dollar spent. Your buyers think in sales metrics. Speak their language.
✓ Discovery framework. Design questions that expose the real cost of manual outbound: How many BDRs do you have? What is your cost per qualified meeting? What percentage of outbound sequences get a response? These questions build the business case for Roger inside the conversation.
✓ Pipeline architecture. Build stages that match how sales leaders evaluate new tools: initial demo, pilot with a subset of reps, results review, full rollout. Define what evidence moves a deal forward at each stage.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Execution
✓ Outbound activation. Launch outbound to VP Sales, Heads of Revenue, and founders at B2B companies with 10-100 person sales teams. Use Roger's own product as the outbound engine where possible. Your outreach quality is your product demo.
✓ Live deal work. Run discovery calls and demos with real prospects. Test which economic arguments land: BDR cost replacement, speed to pipeline, or meeting quality improvement. Iterate the pitch based on buyer reactions, not assumptions.
✓ Sequence of Events. Build a buyer-facing evaluation timeline: pilot setup, success metrics definition, results review, procurement. Sales leaders expect a structured evaluation process. Give them one.
✓ Forecast discipline. Implement evidence-based pipeline tracking. A deal advances when a pilot is live with defined success criteria, not when a prospect says "this looks cool." Build the forecast rigor that your Head of Sales will inherit.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale Readiness
✓ Closed revenue. Target 3-6 paying customers across different company sizes to validate pricing and packaging. At least 2 should be converting from pilot to annual contract with measurable ROI data you can use in future sales conversations.
✓ Repeatable process. Document the full sales motion: which ICP segments convert fastest, what objections come up, how to run a pilot that leads to conversion, and what the average deal cycle looks like. This is the operating system for your Head of Sales.
✓ Hiring blueprint. Define the Head of Sales profile based on 90 days of live selling. You will know whether you need a builder who can scale outbound, a closer who can run enterprise deals, or a player-coach who does both. Write the scorecard from evidence, not hope.
✓ Handoff. Transfer the complete system: CRM pipeline, message library, discovery framework, objection handling guide, pilot playbook, and forecast model. Your Head of Sales inherits a working machine, not a theory deck.
