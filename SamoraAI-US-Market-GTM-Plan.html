Market Tailwinds

  • Labor cost escalation in BPOs - US contact center agent wages have risen 15-20% since 2022. BPOs and enterprises are under margin pressure, making voice AI automation an operational necessity
  • Multilingual demand explosion - The US population is increasingly multilingual. Healthcare, insurance, and financial services regulators require language access. Voice AI that handles 20+ languages eliminates the cost of multilingual staffing
  • Managed service preference - Enterprises want outcomes, not platforms to build on. Samora's managed approach (describe your workflow, we handle the rest) aligns with how large buyers prefer to purchase AI: as a service with SLAs, not as an engineering project
  • Regulatory pressure for compliance - HIPAA in healthcare, PCI-DSS in financial services, and state-level insurance regulations all require auditable, compliant call handling. Samora's built-in compliance guardrails address this directly

Competitive Positioning Strategy

Samora's winning position in the US market is "managed voice AI." The competitive landscape breaks into two camps: developer platforms (Vapi, Retell, Bland) that require engineering teams to build and operate, and enterprise suites (NICE, Observe.AI) that require major implementation projects. Samora occupies the valuable middle ground: enterprise-grade voice AI delivered as a managed service with fast deployment, built-in compliance, and outcome-based accountability. The sales conversation should center on time to value and total cost of ownership, including the engineering team the buyer would need to hire if they chose a developer platform.

Competitive Moat

  • Multilingual from inception - 20+ languages with dialect recognition and code-switching. Most competitors offer English-first with other languages as add-ons. Samora's multilingual DNA is a structural advantage in the diverse US market
  • Managed service model - Full lifecycle ownership from design through monitoring and escalation. Buyers get outcomes without building internal AI operations
  • Production-proven metrics - 80% FCR, 12% collection lift, 100K+ candidates reached. Real results from real deployments, not demo-stage promises
  • Speed to deployment - Most pilots live in under a week. In a market where enterprise voice AI projects typically take 3-6 months, sub-week deployment is a powerful sales accelerator

06 - 90-Day GTM Execution Plan

Building the US Revenue Engine from Zero

Samora has strong product-market fit in India with $50K MRR and production deployments across multiple verticals. The US market represents a step-change in deal size, sales cycle complexity, and competitive intensity. This plan focuses on establishing the commercial foundation to land the first US enterprise customers and build a repeatable pipeline engine.

Phase 1: Days 1 to 30, Market Entry Foundation

ICP Definition and Account Targeting

  • Build a target account list of 200+ US organizations across the four priority verticals: BPOs (top 50 by revenue), insurance carriers (top 50 by premium volume), healthcare systems (top 50 by patient volume), and real estate operators (top 50 by unit count). Prioritize accounts with existing multilingual operations because that is where Samora's advantage is sharpest
  • Map decision makers at each target account. BPO buyers are COOs and VP Operations. Insurance buyers are VP Claims and Head of CX. Healthcare buyers are VP Patient Access and Director of Revenue Cycle. Each persona requires a distinct messaging approach tied to their specific operational pain
  • Analyze Samora's India customer base for US-adjacent opportunities. Global BPOs operating in India (Sutherland, ResultsCX, Teleperformance, Concentrix) may already use Samora in their India operations. These warm relationships create the fastest path to US deployments

US Market Messaging

  • Develop outcome-based messaging for each vertical. BPO messaging leads with margin improvement and agent attrition reduction. Insurance messaging leads with claims handling cost and compliance. Healthcare messaging leads with patient access, no-show reduction, and language access compliance
  • Build ROI calculators for each vertical. BPO: cost per call vs. cost per AI-handled call, agent attrition savings. Insurance: cost per claim touch, policyholder satisfaction impact. Healthcare: no-show revenue recovery, scheduling capacity increase
  • Create competitive battle cards for every major competitor (Bland, Retell, Vapi, Observe.AI, NICE CXone). US buyers will evaluate Samora alongside these established players. The sales team needs to articulate Samora's managed service advantage in every competitive scenario
Phase 2: Days 31 to 60, Pipeline Generation and First Pilots

Outbound Pipeline Development

  • Launch targeted outbound campaigns to VP Operations and COO personas at the top 50 BPOs and contact center operators. Lead with production results: "80% FCR in support, 12% lift in collections, live in under a week." These are the metrics that BPO operators evaluate
  • Execute a "Global BPO Bridge" campaign targeting US operations leaders at BPOs with India presence. Position Samora as a proven technology already operating in their India delivery centers, ready to extend to US client engagements
  • Begin outbound to insurance carriers during their Q3 planning cycle when automation budgets for the next fiscal year are being defined. Insurance buyers plan technology investments 6-12 months ahead of deployment

First US Pilots

  • Close 3 to 5 US pilot engagements across at least two verticals. Target fast-deployment use cases: outbound collection calls, appointment reminders, inbound FAQ handling, or lead qualification. These use cases have clear success metrics and short evaluation cycles
  • Stand up US-based infrastructure for latency-critical deployments. US buyers, especially in healthcare and insurance, require data residency and low-latency call handling. Cloud infrastructure in US regions is a prerequisite for enterprise deals
  • Establish a US business entity and compliance framework. SOC 2 certification, HIPAA BAA capability, and PCI-DSS awareness are table stakes for enterprise conversations in insurance and healthcare
Phase 3: Days 61 to 90, Convert Pilots and Scale Pipeline

Pilot Conversion

  • Convert initial pilots into annual contracts. Target 2 to 3 signed US customers by day 90 with combined ACV of $150K to $500K. Focus on pilots with the strongest outcome metrics because those customers become reference accounts for the rest of the pipeline
  • Document US case studies with quantified results: calls handled, resolution rates, cost savings, deployment timelines. US enterprise buyers rely heavily on peer references and published case studies during evaluation. The first three US case studies accelerate every subsequent deal
  • Begin expansion conversations within initial accounts. A BPO pilot on collections can expand to customer service, appointment scheduling, and outbound sales. A healthcare pilot on appointment reminders can expand to patient intake, follow-up, and revenue cycle communication

Sales Infrastructure

  • Build a repeatable sales process with defined stages, conversion metrics, and forecasting methodology. At $50K MRR targeting $100K, every deal matters. The sales process needs to be instrumented from day one so the team can identify what is working and double down
  • Establish a demo environment optimized for US buyers. Live demonstrations showing multilingual calls, real-time compliance enforcement, and instant CRM integration close deals faster than slide decks. The demo is the product and the product is the pitch
  • Initiate partnership conversations with 2 to 3 BPO operators and 1 to 2 CCaaS platforms. Channel partnerships take 3 to 6 months to formalize, so starting the relationship-building process in the first 90 days ensures revenue contribution by month 6 to 9
