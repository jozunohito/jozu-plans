04 - The Scaling Challenge

What Breaks When You Go from 5 AEs to 20

This is not a zero-to-one problem. Qodo has product-market fit, enterprise customers, a proven sales motion, and an SVP of Global Sales. The challenge now is scaling that motion by 4x without losing what made it work. This is a different kind of hard, and most companies get it wrong.

Tribal Knowledge Collapse

The first 5 AEs learned the motion from founders and early customers. They know which objections matter, how to position against Copilot, when to bring in engineering for a technical deep dive. Rep #15 will not have that context unless it is documented, trained, and reinforced systematically.

Discovery Quality Degradation

With 5 AEs, leadership can inspect every deal. With 20, you cannot. If discovery is not structured and verified through process, new reps will run demos without qualifying. Pipeline will inflate. Forecasts will miss. The board will ask questions nobody wants to answer.

Territory and Segment Confusion

Five AEs can work a general territory model. Twenty cannot. Without clear segmentation by company size, industry vertical, or geography, reps will overlap, compete for the same accounts, and create confusion in the market. Developers talk to each other.

Ramp Time Economics

At 5 AEs, a slow ramp costs you one quota. At 20, four or five reps ramping slowly costs you an entire quarter of growth. If average ramp is 6 months instead of 4, the annual revenue impact is measured in millions. Every week of ramp time reduction is real money.

Technical Sales Capacity

Developer tools require technical depth in the sales process. Your current SE-to-AE ratio works at 5. At 20, you need to decide: do you hire more SEs, build self-serve demo environments, or train AEs to go deeper technically? The wrong answer creates a bottleneck that kills deal velocity.

Forecast Reliability

More reps means more variability. Without consistent qualification criteria and stage definitions tied to buyer actions (not seller optimism), the forecast becomes noise. Boards funded $70M expecting predictable growth, not volatile guessing.

The Core Principle
Scale the process before you scale the people. Every rep you hire before the motion is codified is a bet that tribal knowledge will transfer through osmosis. It will not. The companies that win this inflection point invest in the scaffolding - sales methodology, deal inspection cadence, territory design, ramp curriculum - before they sign the offer letters.

06 - 90-Day Scaling Execution Plan

From 5 AEs to 20 - The Operational Playbook

This is not a build-from-scratch plan. Qodo has product-market fit, proven enterprise customers, and an SVP of Sales leading the motion. This plan focuses on codifying what works, building the infrastructure for scale, and executing the hiring ramp without losing deal quality or forecast reliability.

Phase 1: Days 1-30 - Codify the Winning Motion

Sales Process Documentation

  • Audit every closed-won deal from the last 12 months. Map the actual buyer journey, not the assumed one. Identify which discovery questions, proof points, and competitive positioning actually moved deals forward
  • Build a structured discovery framework specific to each buyer persona (VP Engineering, CISO, CTO, DevOps Lead). Each persona has different pain points and different evidence requirements
  • Document the technical validation process - what demo flow works, when to bring in an SE, what POC structure converts at the highest rate

Territory and Segmentation Design

  • Define three segments: Enterprise (5,000+ developers), Mid-Market (200-5,000), and Growth (50-200). Each requires a different sales motion, cycle length, and resource allocation
  • Build named account lists for Enterprise. Territory-based coverage for Mid-Market. Inbound-driven for Growth
  • Assign current top AEs to Enterprise accounts where deal sizes justify dedicated coverage. New hires start in Mid-Market where the motion is more repeatable and ramp time is shorter

Forecast and Pipeline Discipline

  • Define stage criteria based on buyer actions, not seller activities. Stage 2 requires a confirmed technical evaluation. Stage 3 requires access to economic buyer. Stage 4 requires a signed mutual action plan
  • Implement weekly deal inspection for all deals above $50K ACV. Focus on evidence of buyer commitment, not rep confidence levels
  • Build pipeline coverage requirements by segment: 4x for Enterprise, 3.5x for Mid-Market, 3x for Growth
Phase 2: Days 31-60 - Build the Ramp Machine

Hiring Plan Execution

  • Hire in cohorts of 3-4, not individually. Cohort hiring creates peer accountability, enables structured training, and reduces the cultural risk of one-at-a-time onboarding
  • Prioritize candidates with developer tools or security platform experience. Reps who have sold to engineering buyers will ramp 40-60% faster than those learning the persona for the first time
  • Add one front-line sales manager for every 6-8 AEs. Do not stretch managers beyond 8 direct reports during a scaling phase. The quality of coaching is the single biggest driver of rep productivity

Enablement Infrastructure

  • Build a 30-day ramp curriculum: Week 1 is product and market, Week 2 is competitive positioning and objection handling, Week 3 is live call shadowing, Week 4 is supervised solo calls with real prospects
  • Create a competitive battle card library covering Copilot, Snyk, SonarQube, Codacy, and CodeRabbit. Each card includes positioning, objection responses, and proof points
  • Record and catalog the best discovery calls and demos from current top AEs. New reps learn faster from watching real conversations than from reading playbooks

SE Capacity Planning

  • Current SE-to-AE ratio needs to scale proportionally. Target 1 SE for every 3-4 AEs in Enterprise, 1 SE for every 5-6 AEs in Mid-Market
  • Build self-serve POC environments that reduce SE involvement in early technical validation. The goal is reserving SE time for high-value enterprise evaluations
  • Create standardized demo environments with pre-loaded scenarios showing Qodo finding real bugs in realistic codebases. This eliminates setup time and ensures consistent demo quality
Phase 3: Days 61-90 - Accelerate and Measure

Pipeline Generation at Scale

  • Launch outbound sequences targeting VP Engineering and CISO personas at companies with 500+ developers. Message should lead with the code verification thesis, not features
  • Build an SDR team or outbound motion that feeds the growing AE base. At 20 AEs, you need consistent top-of-funnel volume that does not depend on inbound alone
  • Activate cloud marketplace listings to capture procurement-ready enterprise buyers. Every deal that flows through AWS or Azure Marketplace closes faster and with less procurement friction

Partner Channel Activation

  • Execute first SI co-selling motion with one priority partner. Start with a joint customer reference, build a co-branded solution brief, and run a joint webinar
  • Complete AWS and Azure Marketplace listings if not already live. Train the team on marketplace deal mechanics and procurement workflows
  • Launch technology partner co-marketing with GitHub or Anthropic. Position the "generation + verification" narrative in joint content

Performance Metrics and Inspection

  • Track ramp metrics weekly: time to first meeting, time to first qualified opportunity, time to first closed deal. Benchmark against the first 5 AEs and course-correct immediately when new hires fall behind
  • Monitor pipeline quality ratios: meetings-to-opportunities, opportunities-to-proposals, proposals-to-closed. If conversion rates drop as you scale, the problem is discovery quality, not market demand
  • Run bi-weekly deal reviews with full team. Not just pipeline updates, but live deal coaching where reps present their strategy and get real-time feedback from leadership and peers
