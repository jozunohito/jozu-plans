The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

JEFF
ARAGON
Phase 1: Days 1-30
Carry the Bag - Learn by Closing
✓ Close deals personally. Start by owning live enterprise opportunities. Run discovery, demos, solution mapping, and negotiations. Understand the product, the buyer objections, and the sales cycle from the inside before building anything on top of it.
✓ Map the buyer and the sale. Define the ICP across verticals: which industries are deploying voice agents at scale, who owns the evaluation decision (VP Engineering, Head of AI, CTO), and what triggers the buying conversation (production failures, compliance requirements, scale thresholds).
✓ Audit current motion. Document what is working in founder-led sales: which messaging lands, what the close rate looks like, where deals stall, and what the average deal cycle is. This is the baseline the machine gets built on.
✓ Build the ROI case framework. Create a repeatable ROI model for enterprise buyers: cost of unverified voice agents (failed conversations, customer churn, engineering rework) vs. cost of Coval. Every proposal uses this math.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Build the Machine
✓ Define the sales playbook. Codify the sales process into a repeatable system: ICP, outbound sequences, discovery framework, demo structure, objection handling, competitive positioning, closing motion. This is the document the next 5 hires onboard from.
✓ Outbound and inbound strategy. Determine the right mix. With strong inbound already flowing, build the outbound motion to attack verticals and accounts that are not finding Coval on their own. Define the sequencing, messaging, and targeting.
✓ Account expansion. Build the expansion playbook for existing Fortune 500 customers: new business units, additional use cases, volume increases. Enterprise land-and-expand is where the real revenue multiplier lives.
✓ Sales strategy ownership. Own the end-to-end revenue strategy: which verticals to prioritize, how to balance inbound vs. outbound, what the pricing and packaging should look like as the market matures. Present to CEO with data-backed recommendations.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale - Build the Team
✓ Revenue delivered. Multiple enterprise and mid-market contracts closed. Pipeline growing month over month. Revenue trajectory validates the GTM model and gives confidence to invest in team expansion.
✓ Hiring plan defined and executing. Based on 90 days of real deal data, define the sales team structure: roles needed, ideal profiles, comp plans, ramp expectations. Begin recruiting the first hires.
✓ Founder transition complete. CEO is out of day-to-day sales. The machine runs. Founder time redirects to product, fundraising, and strategic partnerships. Sales has its own leader and its own operating rhythm.
✓ Culture set. Sales team culture established: people who know how to sell a technical product into enterprise, who move at AI speed, and who build systems instead of just running plays.
