Product Overview

The Algolia Platform

Algolia has evolved from a search API into a comprehensive AI search, discovery, and retrieval platform. The platform combines the precision of keyword search with AI-powered semantic understanding, delivering sub-50ms results at global scale. With the recent launch of Agent Studio, Algolia is positioning itself as the intelligence and memory layer for the next generation of AI-native applications.

Core Products

Algolia AI Search

NeuralSearch technology fuses vector and keyword search in a single query. Neural hashing compresses vectors for speed without sacrificing relevance. Configurable ranking weights let teams balance semantic understanding with keyword precision. The core product that powers 1.75 trillion searches per year.

Algolia Recommend

AI-powered product recommendations for related items, frequently bought together, and trending content. Drives personalized discovery experiences that increase conversion rates, average order value, and time on site. Built for real-time responsiveness at e-commerce scale.

Agent Studio

Launched September 2025. Platform for building AI agents powered by Algolia's retrieval infrastructure. Grounded Retrieval keeps agents anchored in enterprise data. MCP Server for developer integration. Positions Algolia as the retrieval backbone for agentic AI applications.

Browse AI and Merchandising

AI-powered category pages and merchandising tools. Business users can generate product collections using natural language. Reduces manual merchandising work and accelerates response to seasonal trends. Visual rules editor for non-technical teams.

DocSearch, Ask AI, and SiteSearch

Purpose-built solutions for developer documentation, conversational AI answers, and website search. DocSearch is free for open-source projects, driving massive developer adoption. Ask AI delivers conversational search experiences powered by retrieval-augmented generation.

Three Growth Vectors

  • Expansion selling into the existing base. With 17,000+ customers and a usage-based pricing model, the single greatest lever for revenue growth is increasing consumption, product adoption, and contract value within accounts that already trust the platform. Every customer using Search is a prospect for Recommend, Browse AI, and Agent Studio.
  • New enterprise acquisition. Algolia's Gartner Leader positioning and Agent Studio launch open conversations with CIOs, CDOs, and VPs of Engineering at large enterprises evaluating AI retrieval infrastructure. These are six and seven-figure annual deals with multi-year expansion potential.
  • Agentic AI and retrieval infrastructure. The emerging market for AI agent infrastructure is growing faster than any segment in enterprise software. Algolia's 1.75 trillion query track record, sub-50ms latency, and grounded retrieval capabilities make it uniquely positioned to become the retrieval layer that AI agents depend on.

Segment Prioritization

  • E-commerce and retail (largest current segment). Enterprise retailers deploying AI search, recommendations, and merchandising. Algolia already serves brands like Lacoste, Gymshark, Birkenstock, Under Armour, Staples, and Petsmart.   • Enterprise and B2B (emerging segment). Agent Studio and MCP Server open an entirely new buyer: the VP of AI, Chief Data Officer, or Head of AI Engineering building retrieval infrastructure for internal agents and customer-facing AI applications.

The competitive strategy depends on the buyer, the use case, and the technical depth of the evaluation. Algolia's advantages are speed, developer experience, relevance quality, and now AI-native retrieval for agents. The key is positioning correctly against each competitor.

The Plan

90-Day Americas Revenue Acceleration

This is not a startup GTM build. Algolia has an established Americas business, an existing sales team, a recognized brand, and a customer base generating over $100M in revenue. The plan is to accelerate what exists: sharpen execution, expand deal sizes, improve win rates, build repeatable enterprise motions, and capture the agentic AI retrieval opportunity before competitors establish themselves.

Phase 1: Days 1 to 30
Assess, Align, Accelerate
  • Full revenue audit. Day 1. Open every pipeline opportunity, inspect every deal. Map the existing team's strengths, coverage gaps, and quota distribution. Identify the deals that can close fastest, the accounts with the highest expansion potential, and the segments where Algolia is winning versus losing and why. Interview every AE, SE, and CSM on the team within the first two weeks.
  • Segment the installed base for expansion. Algolia's 17,000+ customers represent the single largest revenue acceleration opportunity. Identify accounts using Search that have not adopted Recommend or Browse AI. Identify accounts with flat or declining query volume. Identify customers on legacy contracts that could be restructured into higher-value agreements. Build a ranked list of the top 100 expansion targets by revenue potential.
  • Align with new CEO priorities. Stephen Lynch joined in April 2026 with a mandate to scale Algolia as the intelligence and memory layer for AI-native applications. Ensure the Americas sales strategy reflects this positioning. Work with product and marketing to align messaging around Agent Studio, Grounded Retrieval, and the AI retrieval infrastructure narrative.
  • Establish forecast discipline. Implement evidence-based pipeline management across the Americas team. Every deal at Commit stage requires: economic buyer identified and engaged, business case quantified, technical validation complete, procurement process confirmed, and timeline agreed. No deal advances on seller activity alone. Build a shared language around pipeline quality, not just pipeline volume.
  • Assess team structure and capacity. Evaluate whether the current team is organized for growth. Map coverage by segment (e-commerce, SaaS, media, enterprise), by deal size (self-serve, mid-market, enterprise), and by geography (US, Canada, LATAM). Identify where additional headcount or specialization is needed and begin building the hiring plan for Phase 3.
Phase 2: Days 31 to 60
Execute, Expand, Win
  • Run the top 20 deals directly. Get into the most important enterprise opportunities alongside AEs. Join discovery calls, review proposals, pressure-test business cases, and engage with economic buyers personally. The fastest way to impact revenue is to improve the quality and velocity of deals already in motion. I have done this at every company I have joined and it produces immediate results.
  • Launch the expansion selling motion. Activate the top 100 expansion targets identified in Phase 1. Work with CSMs and AEs to build account plans for each. Here is how Agent Studio lets you build AI-powered shopping assistants using your existing Algolia data." Expansion revenue should be the fastest path to accelerating Americas growth.
  • Build the enterprise playbook for Agent Studio. Agent Studio opens a new buyer (VP of AI, Head of AI Engineering, Chief Data Officer) and a new use case (retrieval infrastructure for AI agents). This requires a distinct sales motion: different discovery questions, different ROI models, different competitive positioning. Build the playbook, train the team, and launch targeted outbound into enterprises actively building AI agent infrastructure.
  • Sharpen competitive execution. Build or refresh battle cards for Elasticsearch, Coveo, Bloomreach, and Google Vertex AI Search. Analyze recent win/loss data to identify patterns. Where are we losing and why? Where are we winning and what can we replicate? Competitive intelligence should flow into weekly deal reviews and inform how we position in every opportunity.
  • Implement account-based outbound for enterprise targets. Identify 50 high-value enterprise accounts in the Americas that are not yet Algolia customers. Build targeted messaging by segment and persona. Coordinate with marketing on ABM campaigns. The goal is not volume. The goal is getting meetings with the right buyers at the right companies with a message that speaks to their specific needs.

Deliver a data-backed plan covering: revenue targets by segment and product, pipeline coverage requirements, team size and investment needed, expansion revenue forecasts, competitive positioning priorities, and the timeline for capturing the agentic AI retrieval market. Partnership and Channel Strategy

Scaling Through Strategic Partnerships

Algolia's API-first architecture makes it naturally embeddable within partner ecosystems. The partnership strategy should target multiple tiers, from technology alliances that generate co-sell pipeline to commerce platform ecosystems that drive self-serve adoption at scale.

Commerce Platform Ecosystem

Shopify, BigCommerce, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, Adobe Commerce (Magento), and Commercetools all have merchants who need better search than their native capabilities provide. Build certified integrations, co-marketing programs, and joint selling motions with platform sales teams. Every commerce platform deal where the merchant needs AI search is an Algolia opportunity. The Shopify ecosystem alone represents thousands of mid-market and enterprise merchants.

System Integrators and Digital Agencies

Accenture, Deloitte Digital, Publicis Sapient, EPAM, and boutique e-commerce agencies implement digital commerce and content platforms for enterprise clients. These firms influence technology selection and often have existing MSAs that accelerate procurement. Get Algolia into their recommendation set and implementation playbooks. Train their teams to implement and configure Algolia. Every replatforming project is a search decision.

Technology and Cloud Alliances

AWS Marketplace, Google Cloud Marketplace, and Azure Marketplace listings simplify procurement for enterprise buyers with existing cloud commitments. Build co-sell relationships with cloud sales teams. Partner with CMS platforms (Contentful, Sanity, Hygraph), headless commerce platforms (Commercetools, Fabric), and CDP vendors (Segment, mParticle) where search is a natural complement. Each integration makes Algolia stickier and harder to replace.

AI and Developer Ecosystem

Algolia's MCP Server and Agent Studio position the platform as retrieval infrastructure for the AI agent ecosystem. Partner with AI development platforms (LangChain, LlamaIndex, Vercel AI SDK), AI application builders, and enterprises building custom agents. The companies investing in agentic AI infrastructure today will generate the highest-value Algolia contracts over the next 24 months. Build relationships with AI-focused VCs, accelerators, and developer communities to ensure Algolia is the default retrieval layer for AI-native applications.

Channel Revenue Target

Partner-sourced and partner-influenced pipeline should represent 20% of Americas pipeline by month 6 and scale to 30%+ by month 12. The commerce platform ecosystem and system integrator channels will produce the fastest results. The AI and developer ecosystem partnerships will generate the largest long-term contract values.
