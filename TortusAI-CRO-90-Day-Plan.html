1. The System I Run

Every plan I build for every client and every employer runs on one integrated method.

Deep Discovery (5 layers). Surface Symptoms become Stated Problems become Operational Impact become Economic Impact become Root Constraint and Unconsidered Need. You do not earn the right to claim a Power Message until you reach Layer 4. Most sellers stop at Layer 2 and then wonder why their forecasts miss. In clinician AI, Layer 4 is where the CFO and the Medical Director meet: hours of clinician admin burden eliminated, locum and agency spend avoided, clinic throughput uplift, outpatient waiting-list reduction, retention of senior clinicians at risk of attrition, STAR-equivalent quality measures in EMEA, and KHZG deduction avoidance in Germany.

Power Messaging. A Power Message links a unique capability to a buyer-specific problem that can be defined in clear economic terms. It emerges from discovery, never from marketing. "Tortus is faster than Heidi" is not a Power Message. "Your trust is running a GBP 14M annual overspend on locum and agency cover driven in material part by outpatient clinician burnout. Across nine NHS sites and 17,000 encounters Tortus delivered a 23.5 percent increase in patient-interaction time and an 8.2 percent appointment-length reduction, which independently modelled translates to roughly GBP 3.8M of addressable workforce spend inside your current outpatient footprint" is a Power Message.

Two gates before I sell. Gate 1 is Active Evaluation. The buyer is actually in motion, with a named stakeholder, a timeline, and a procurement path such as NHS SBS framework call-off, trust capital allocation, or KHZG project funding. Gate 2 is Quantified Severity. They can name the pound, euro, or dollar cost of doing nothing. If either gate fails, I exit with discipline. I keep the relationship warm and move on. Most deals die because sellers refuse to accept that a buyer is not actually buying.

Mutual Sequence of Events. Co-created with the buyer. Includes procurement, clinical governance, IG Toolkit or DSPT, DTAC re-confirmation, Caldicott Guardian sign-off, DPIA, MHRA context, EU AI Act posture, trust or group legal, and implementation through first live clinic use. The sequence ends beyond signature, at the first measured clinician-hour saved, because that is when the buyer actually sees the outcome they bought.

The Plan Letter. Sent within 24 hours of every substantive conversation. It restates value in the buyer's economic language, delivers every commitment made in the meeting, lays out the complete timeline to first live use, and asks the buyer to confirm in writing. If they write back yes, the deal belongs in the forecast. If they rewrite the sequence, that is better data and the deal belongs in the forecast with updated milestones. If they go silent, they are not in the forecast.

The forecast rule. No buyer-confirmed Plan Letter, no deal in the forecast. I learned this at UKG, where the culture enforced it without exception. My manager's line was "great job on the forecast. Show me the Plan Letter." That rule made forecasts defensible by construction, not by hope. It kept my number real year after year and produced top-5-percent-globally ranking for nearly six years.

The forcing function. If I cannot write a good Plan Letter after a call, I ran a bad meeting. Period. So, run better meetings. That discipline is how I coach sellers without sitting in every one of their meetings.

Every initiative below exists to make Plan Letters writable and forecasts accurate. That is the whole point.

4. The Three Problems I Solve at Tortus

Problem 1: Make NHS and EMEA ROI defensible at procurement

NHS and EMEA public-healthcare buyers buy in two modes: clinical outcomes and workforce economics. Every Tortus deal wins or loses on whether the buyer can walk into their own CFO, Medical Director, or ICB Finance Committee with defensible numbers. Marketing produces case studies that show outcomes. Sales must translate those outcomes into buyer-specific economic numbers in every deal.

The economic anchors for Tortus are known:

  • Clinician time released. Hours per clinician per week returned to direct patient care, modelled against workforce cost per hour.
  • Outpatient throughput uplift. Additional appointments per clinic session using the measured 8.2 percent appointment-length reduction, translated into waiting-list reduction and elective-recovery revenue.
  • Locum and agency spend avoided. Retained senior clinician capacity reduces reliance on premium-rate cover, typically 40 percent to 100 percent above substantive pay.
  • Clinician retention economics. Attrition avoidance modelled at GBP 40k to GBP 100k per senior clinician replaced. NHS workforce churn is a documented CFO line item.
  • Coding accuracy and revenue capture. In private groups and mixed payer settings, correct clinical coding is directly linked to billing integrity.
  • KHZG compliance. For German hospitals, avoidance of up to 2 percent billing deduction is the single highest-value economic anchor for 2026-2027 procurement.

Tortus has the clinical proof. The challenge is not knowing these anchors exist. It is making every seller translate every prospect's specific situation into those numbers in every deal. That is the discipline I install. Initiative 3 builds the framework.

Problem 2: Close the NHS framework and trust-rollout gap

The NHS AVT Supplier Registry and NHS SBS Digital Dictation 2 framework are the two most valuable procurement artefacts in the UK market. Framework position converts single-trust selling into multi-trust call-off. The CRO is hired to architect framework-led selling as its own dedicated motion, not as an add-on to direct trust outreach.

The structural differences:

  • Procurement cycle: 6 to 12 months for trust-direct; 3 to 6 months for framework call-off after framework position is won.
  • Buyer: CCIO, Chief Medical Information Officer, Director of Digital, plus Director of Finance and Clinical Director in secondary care; Clinical Director plus Practice Business Manager plus PCN Lead in primary care.
  • Contract structure: per-clinician seat, annual; framework typically sets ceiling rates and trust-level call-offs settle actuals.
  • Regulatory surface: DTAC, DSPT, IG Toolkit, MHRA Class 1 minimum, EU AI Act high-risk posture where relevant, DPIA, Caldicott Guardian.

Each requires dedicated muscle. Initiative 1 builds it.

Problem 3: Convert clinical credibility into enterprise commercial discipline

Tortus was founded by a doctor and built on clinical rigor. That is the asset. The commercial challenge is that clinical rigor alone does not close multi-stakeholder enterprise deals on predictable timelines. Deals close on procurement fluency, Plan Letter discipline, mutual sequence execution, and seller accountability against a standard process.

The clinical team's relationship with clinician champions is the leading indicator. The commercial team's discipline is the conversion rate. Initiative 3 codifies the operating model that turns clinician champions into contracted ARR at predictable cadence.

The California health plan wildfire reframe: live proof of this framework

The discipline above is not theoretical. The following is how I ran it live at a large California health plan, and the reason this matters for Tortus is that the buying mechanics translate directly to NHS trusts, European sick funds, and private hospital groups.

The plan had a commoditised RFP for a member-facing messaging platform: roughly $200K fixed-fee procurement, procurement-led buying committee, four or five vendors competing on price. An AE running a feature-led motion would have competed on platform features, lost on price, and moved on.

I ran the five-layer discovery:

  • Layer 1 (surface symptom): inefficient member messaging.
  • Layer 2 (stated problem): low engagement on routine touchpoints.
  • Layer 3 (operational impact): rising member-service call volume, missed care-gap outreach deadlines.
  • Layer 4 (economic impact): unmeasured but material impact on STAR-rating scorecards and on medical-claims drift in unmanaged cohorts. Nobody had connected the messaging platform to the claims economics.
  • Layer 5 (root constraint, unconsidered need): during active wildfire season, the plan had no centralised mechanism to maintain continuity of care for displaced members. Messaging platform was being bought to solve a cost problem. The real unconsidered need was a continuity-of-care platform that could pivot the entire plan's communications in 24 hours when a wildfire displaced a ZIP code.

I reframed the RFP from messaging platform to continuity-of-care platform. The wildfire scenario made the case visible to the CMO and Chief Actuary. Messaging was a feature. Continuity of care was the system. The RFP committee had to go back to executive leadership to re-scope.

The result: $200K commodity RFP became $1.4M ARR strategic platform. 7x expansion. The plan used the deal internally as the template for how they scope vendor procurement going forward.

Why this matters for Tortus. NHS trusts, German Krankenhauser, and UK private groups buy ambient scribe tools the same way that California health plan bought messaging platforms. Procurement-led, feature-scored, price-anchored. The reframe to a trust-wide clinician productivity and workforce continuity system, evidenced by Tortus's 17,000-encounter dataset, is the same structural move. It converts a per-clinician seat tender into a trust-level or group-level commercial conversation. The discipline that closed the California deal at 7x expansion is the discipline that closes a GBP 250K trust pilot at GBP 1.5M trust-wide enterprise within 12 months.

13. Integration with AI in the GTM Model

Selling clinician AI requires a commercial team that uses AI in its own GTM. Buyers ask. Sellers answer with credibility or without.

13.1 Where AI compounds revenue

  • Trigger-event detection. Continuous monitoring of NHS trust board papers, CCIO appointments, NHS SBS framework activity, ICS AVT announcements, private-group press. Surface named trigger events to BDRs within 24 hours.
  • Pre-call research synthesis. Every AE gets a one-page research brief on each target account, auto-generated from public sources including NHS trust annual reports, RTT data, and CCIO LinkedIn, validated by the AE. Saves 90 minutes per call.
  • Plan Letter drafting. AE provides the call summary; AI drafts the Plan Letter in the buyer's language using the standard structure. AE validates, edits, sends within 24 hours.
  • Workforce economic modelling. NHS trust and private-group deals require analysis of workforce, agency, and attrition data. AI accelerates the analysis, not the conclusion.
  • Forecast-integrity checking. Pattern-match open deals against closed-won and closed-lost patterns. Flag deals at risk of slipping early enough to intervene.

13.2 Where AI does not belong

  • Final Plan Letter approval. The seller owns the relationship; the seller owns the letter.
  • Pricing approval. Discount ceilings are human decisions with seller and CEO accountability.
  • Clinical evidence interpretation. Tortus's clinical team owns this.
  • Direct buyer interaction without human review (outbound email at scale, automated chat with NHS procurement).

Guiding principle: AI is speed. Humans are accountability. The CRO owns where that line sits, and it sits in a specific place that can be defended in a board meeting and in a buyer's procurement review.
