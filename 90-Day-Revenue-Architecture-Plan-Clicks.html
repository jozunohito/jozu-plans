The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

ARAGON
Phase 1: Days 1-30
Foundation
✓ Buyer definition. Map the exact operations leader profile that buys: title, company size, industry vertical, and the specific back-office pain (AP, procurement, data entry, compliance reporting) that triggers urgency. No broad "operations teams" targeting.
✓ Power Message. Build a message that connects Clicks AI agents to a quantified cost problem. Example structure: "Mid-market companies spend $X per month on manual [process]. Clicks agents handle that work in hours, not weeks, at a fraction of headcount cost." Test it in 10 live conversations.
✓ Discovery framework. Design 8-10 questions that uncover how many FTEs a prospect dedicates to back-office tasks, what their error rates look like, and what it costs them when processes break. These questions must surface the economic case for AI agents without requiring the buyer to do the math.
✓ Pipeline architecture. Define stages, exit criteria, and the data model in your CRM. Every deal gets a stage, a next action, and a close date. No deals sitting in "interested" for 45 days with no activity.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Execution
✓ Outbound activation. Launch targeted outbound to VP/Director of Operations at mid-market companies (500-5,000 employees) in verticals where back-office pain is most acute: logistics, financial services, healthcare admin. 50-75 qualified accounts in the first sequence.
✓ Live deal work. Work inside the first 5-10 active opportunities. Run discovery calls, build business cases with real numbers, and identify the decision-making structure at each account. Document what works and what stalls.
✓ Sequence of Events. Build a mutually agreed buyer-seller roadmap for each deal. Every step requires a buyer action, not just a seller follow-up. This is how you separate real deals from polite interest.
✓ Forecast discipline. Implement weekly pipeline reviews with evidence-based criteria. No deal advances without a confirmed next step, an identified economic buyer, and a documented business case. Gut feel does not count.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale Readiness
✓ Closed revenue. Target 2-4 signed contracts with mid-market operations teams. These are reference accounts that prove the sales motion works and give your GTM hire deals to point to when opening new conversations.
✓ Repeatable process. Document the full sales cycle from first touch to close: what messaging converts, what discovery questions surface real pain, what objections come up (build vs. buy, security, integration), and how to handle each one.
✓ Hiring blueprint. Define the exact profile for your Founding GTM Lead based on what the sales motion actually requires. Include the comp structure, quota expectations, and 30/60/90 day onboarding plan so they can start producing in weeks, not months.
✓ Handoff. Transfer the full system - pipeline, playbook, message library, CRM configuration, and deal history - to your GTM hire or internal team. Nothing lives in someone's head. Everything is documented and operational.
