2. The System I Run

Every plan I build for every client and every employer runs on one integrated method. This is the Jozu Enterprise Revenue System, my own synthesis across 25 years of US enterprise sales and 12 years of coaching under the CustomerCentric Selling practice.

2.1 Five-Level Discovery

Surface Symptoms become Stated Problems become Operational Impact become Economic Impact become Root Constraint and Unconsidered Need. You do not earn the right to claim a Power Message until you reach Level 4. Most sellers stop at Level 2 and then wonder why their forecasts miss. In jumbo-employer benefits, Level 4 is where the CFO, the CHRO, and the CMO sit together: annual medical trend running 7.5 to 9 percent, pharmacy trend running 11 to 13 percent with GLP-1s, absenteeism cost per 1,000 FTE per year, preventable cardiometabolic event cost per avoided case, behavioral-health claims trend, and the compounding cost of a fragmented benefits stack that hits the same employee with eight point solutions and no longitudinal record.

2.2 Power Messaging

A Power Message links a unique capability to a buyer-specific problem defined in clear economic terms. It emerges from discovery, never from marketing. "Function Health is the AI operating system for health" is not a Power Message. "Your 42,000 covered employees generated $48M in medical trend over plan last year, 62 percent concentrated in cardiometabolic, endocrine, and behavioral health. Function Health's proactive biomarker and longitudinal care platform detects and intervenes on those three categories at a member level, with an AI operating system that closes the loop between lab data, clinical decision support, and the member's primary care relationship. At your population, the expected reduction in preventable high-cost events is modeled at $12M to $18M over three years against a seven-figure annual platform fee, with a defensible actuarial audit trail your consulting firm can underwrite" is a Power Message.

2.3 Two Gates, widen not walk

Gate 1 is Active Evaluation. The buyer is in motion, with a named stakeholder, a timeline, and a procurement path. Gate 2 is Quantified Severity. They can name the dollar cost of doing nothing. If either gate fails, I do not walk. I widen. I surface a larger use case, follow the economic pain downstream, and tie the solution to an existing forcing function inside the account, a plan-year RFP, an ACA reporting deadline, a post-merger benefits harmonization, a board-level healthcare cost number, or a Total Rewards strategy refresh. That is how a large national radiology provider became $1.6M ARR from a $30K OCR request. The OCR engagement alone had no active evaluation, so I anchored the deal to the incumbent vendor's contract renewal. The renewal was the forcing function. My solution replaced the incumbent's majority-revenue contract. The same discipline applies inside a jumbo employer: the narrow program entry widens to the full-population deployment by anchoring to the plan-year or Total Rewards forcing function already on the CHRO's calendar.

2.4 Mutual Sequence of Events (nine steps)

Co-created with the buyer. Evaluation phase: problem severity validation, success criteria, clinical and product alignment, stakeholder expansion across HR, Total Rewards, Clinical, Finance, and the consulting firm, security and privacy review. Execution phase: commercial structuring, executive approval, contract execution, implementation and member-launch plan. Every step is validated in writing before the next one runs. This is the sequence that closes jumbo-employer procurement.

2.5 The Plan Letter

Sent within 24 hours of every substantive conversation. It restates value in the buyer's economic language, delivers every commitment made in the meeting, lays out the complete timeline to launch and to the 6 to 9 month expansion decision, and asks the buyer to confirm in writing. If they write back yes, the deal belongs in the forecast. If they rewrite the sequence, that is better data and the deal belongs in the forecast with updated milestones. If they go silent, they are not in the forecast.

2.6 The forecast rule

No buyer-confirmed Plan Letter, no deal in the forecast. I learned this at UKG where the culture enforced it without exception for 5.5 straight years against ADP, Workday, and Oracle. That rule made forecasts defensible by construction, not by hope. It is what produced the top 5 percent global ranking at UKG and the $8M ARR and $24M TCV run at Series A Ushur as the senior IC.

Every initiative below exists to make Plan Letters writable and forecasts accurate. That is the whole point.

How the gatekeeping actually works. When a jumbo employer runs a health-benefits RFP, the consulting firm typically authors the RFP, sets the evaluation criteria, runs the finalist scoring, and advises the client on the contract. A vendor that is not known to the consulting firm at the practice-group level often does not get on the invite list. A vendor that is known and liked gets a shortlist slot and a procedural advantage in the scoring. A vendor that is preferred gets recommended. The difference in win rate across those three states is massive.

5. The Problems I Solve at Function Health

Three problems are specific to this seat. I solve each one with a repeatable play.

5.1 Jumbo-employer buying-committee navigation

A 15,000+ employee health-benefits decision runs through a committee of five to nine stakeholders: CHRO, VP Total Rewards or Chief Benefits Officer, Chief Medical Officer or Medical Director, CFO or VP Finance for the benefits line, VP of HR Operations, Chief People Officer, Chief Wellbeing Officer (where it exists), the benefits consulting firm lead, and procurement. Each has a different success criterion. The CHRO is measured on talent attraction and retention. The CFO is measured on bent medical trend. The CMO is measured on clinical outcomes and malpractice exposure. Total Rewards is measured on program NPS and utilization. Procurement is measured on cycle time and commercial terms.

The play. I build a stakeholder-by-stakeholder Power Message before the third meeting, with a single-page artifact per role that lands the economic case in that stakeholder's language. The Plan Letter after each meeting restates the stakeholder-specific message so the champion can forward it inside the account. I multi-thread by design, not by accident, with a named sequence of internal stakeholders on a 60 to 90 day expansion arc before commercial terms are discussed. I ran this exact play inside CalOptima Health's Medicaid redetermination RFP and at multiple Fortune 500 HCM procurements at UKG.

5.2 Consulting-firm gatekeeper strategy

Most vendors treat consulting firms as an afterthought. That is the miss. Marsh McLennan, Mercer, WTW, Aon, and Gallagher are not in the deal as neutral parties. They are the client's advisor, and their recommendation carries material weight. A vendor that is not on their shortlist is usually not on the RFP. A vendor that is on their shortlist but not briefed is on the RFP with a handicap. A vendor that is briefed, known, and has provided the consultant with usable intellectual property gets the inside track.

The play. I build a deliberate consulting-firm influence motion with three layers: practice-group-level relationships at the partner and principal level in population health, wellbeing, Rx, and Total Rewards; co-authored point-of-view artifacts on preventative biomarker programs that the consulting firm can use with their clients; and a quarterly briefing rhythm at each firm's annual benefits planning cycle (typically January and June). The goal is to move Function Health from "unknown" to "known and recommended" inside each firm's practice group within two quarters. Details in Initiative 2.

5.3 Multi-year full-population deployment contracting

A jumbo-employer full-population Function Health deployment is a three to five year, member-level, commercially complex contract with clinical, legal, privacy, IT, and actuarial review. It is not a benefits-vendor MSA. It is a payer-grade commercial agreement with data-access clauses, HIPAA and state privacy posture, 42 CFR Part 2 where behavioral health crosses, eligibility-file integration, carrier coordination, risk-sharing construct (where relevant), and a board-reportable outcome commitment.

The play. I install the Mutual Sequence of Events as a written, buyer-confirmed artifact starting at stakeholder expansion and running through implementation plan. Legal, privacy, IT security, and actuarial review run in parallel with pricing discussions, not behind them. The Plan Letter restates milestones after every substantive meeting. This is exactly what I did at Ushur for the $10M public-health-authority deal at CalOptima Health, which is a CMS-regulated, procurement-governed, legal-heavy commercial motion that shares 80 percent of its DNA with jumbo-employer full-population contracting.

6.1 Numbered trigger events

  1. Health-plan RFP or carrier renewal. Typical 2 to 4 year cycle. RFP release usually Q1 to Q2 for plan-year start January 1. Function Health is not the carrier, but the RFP window is the open decision door for adjacent programs. A sharp outbound into the CHRO and Total Rewards lead in the 90 days before RFP release is a forcing-function play.
  2. Medical trend exceeding 8 percent and CFO attention. Business Group on Health 2026 trend forecast is 7.8 percent. Any account reporting higher in its 10-K MD&A (public filers) or in public-benefits-disclosure filings is a live account.
  3. GLP-1 coverage policy review. Pharmacy trend is compounding at 11 to 13 percent driven by GLP-1s. Every jumbo employer has an active GLP-1 policy conversation in 2026. Function Health's biomarker platform is a credible story for lifestyle-plus-metabolic programs that make GLP-1 spending more defensible to the CFO.
  4. Post-merger benefits harmonization. M&A creates a mandate to consolidate benefits stacks. The window is typically 12 to 24 months post-close. Any Fortune 500 merger in 2024 to 2026 is a forcing-function target.
  5. New CHRO, Chief People Officer, or CMO in seat. New executives typically run a 90-day listening tour and a 180-day strategic refresh. That window is when new vendor selection can accelerate.
  6. Board-level healthcare cost commitment. Any public company that has committed to a specific healthcare-cost or absenteeism-reduction number in its investor day or proxy is an account with an executive incentive to act.
  7. Wellbeing or longevity program launch. Public announcements of longevity programs, executive health benefits expansion, or biomarker pilots are direct trigger events.
  8. Population health or value-based care initiative at the parent company. Health systems that operate as both employer and provider (Kaiser, Providence, Intermountain, Ascension, CommonSpirit as provider-side) have internal dual forcing functions.
  9. ACA reporting, IRS Section 6055 and 6056, and state-mandate reporting cycles. Annual reporting windows put benefits data on the executive table.
  10. CMS and HHS policy shifts. New preventative-services mandates or ACA regulatory guidance create opening conversations with Total Rewards on program design.

9. Pricing and Packaging Thesis

9.1 Current state inference

Function Health's public-facing pricing is a consumer annual membership at approximately $499 per member per year for the consumer SKU. The employer SKU is not public, which is appropriate for a Series B enterprise motion. Public competitor pricing suggests jumbo-employer pricing is typically a blended PMPM or PEPM construct, with floors and caps, and with performance guarantee structures on outcomes where the vendor has defensible data.

9.2 Jumbo-employer packaging thesis

  • Platform tier. Full-population eligibility, biomarker panel, longitudinal AI operating system, clinician access. Priced PEPM with a floor at population and a cap at utilization.
  • Targeted cohort tier. Executive, high-risk, or specific-program populations. Priced per-enrolled at a premium PMPM.
  • Module pricing. Selected biomarker packages, condition-focused pathways, integrations with primary care, navigation, or behavioral health. Priced per module.
  • Outcome guarantee layer. Optional performance construct on specific clinical or economic outcomes, with actuarial validation, priced as a percentage at-risk on platform fee.

9.3 Pricing principles

  • Never lead with price. Price is a function of validated Level 4 economic impact. A Power Message that has not been economically validated cannot be priced responsibly.
  • Multi-year preference. Three-year terms are the standard ask. Year-1 list, Year 2 and 3 capped escalators, expansion pricing defined up front to reduce friction at the 6 to 9 month decision.
  • Consulting-firm defensibility. Any pricing construct must be explainable in actuarial terms to Mercer, WTW, Aon, or Gallagher in a 20-minute finalist review. If the consulting firm cannot model it, it will not clear the RFP.
  • Discount ceiling discipline. Standard discount envelope at each ACV band, with escalation to a named pricing committee. Discount overrides always tied to documented strategic logo value, not quota-driven convenience.

10. Win/Loss Hypotheses

10.1 Where Function Health wins

  • Account has talent-competitive Total Rewards posture. Longevity and biomarker programs are a differentiator the CHRO can use in the war for talent. Tech, financial services, professional services, life sciences.
  • Account has a Chief Medical Officer or Chief Wellbeing Officer. The CMO or CWO is the natural clinical sponsor; their absence forces the seller to build the role inside HR, which slows cycles.
  • Consulting firm is briefed and favorable. A known and endorsed vendor wins more often than a cold entrant.
  • Trigger event is live. A named forcing function (RFP, merger, new CHRO, board commitment) shortens cycles.
  • Point-solution fatigue is explicit. When the CHRO or Total Rewards lead names program fatigue as a driver, Function Health's platform narrative sells itself.

10.2 Where Function Health loses

  • Account has recently renewed a competing navigation or population-health platform. Displacement cycles are long and the widen-not-walk move usually anchors to the renewal window two years out.
  • CFO-led procurement with commodity framing. If the account frames the decision as a commodity wellness buy at lowest PEPM, Function Health is not the lowest bidder and is not positioned to win on price.
  • Consulting firm is not engaged or is skeptical. A cold consulting firm is a material drag on finalist scoring.
  • Legal or privacy posture is unusually heavy. Heavily regulated environments (defense cleared, healthcare payer-employer dual-role) can produce cycles that exceed the seat's 12-month quota cadence.
  • No CMO and no CWO. Clinical ownership inside the account is a prerequisite for full-population deployment at scale.

10.3 Widen-not-walk pattern when the gates fail

When Gate 1 (Active Evaluation) fails, anchor to a forcing function the account already owns: plan-year RFP, CHRO transition, board commitment, merger-harmonization. When Gate 2 (Quantified Severity) fails, the discovery was shallow. Return to Level 3 and Level 4 discovery with a new stakeholder (typically CFO or CMO) before giving up on the account. I do not walk unless the account has a standing two-year stasis and no forcing function on the calendar.

14. Integration with AI in the GTM Model

Function Health is an AI operating system for health. The GTM motion should model the same posture. AI is a force multiplier on a disciplined human system. It is not a substitute for one.

14.1 Where AI accelerates the seller

  • Account research. Every named account gets an AI-assisted first-pass brief: public 10-K and proxy healthcare cost signals, Total Rewards and wellbeing posture, named consulting firm, named executives, trigger events, and public board-level commitments. Seller time shifts from research to synthesis.
  • Power Message drafting. First-pass Power Message is AI-assisted. Every line is pressure-tested by the seller before it leaves the building. The speed is AI. The message is the seller's.
  • Plan Letter drafting. First-pass Plan Letter is AI-assisted from meeting notes. Final review and customization stays with the seller. The discipline rule still holds: no buyer-confirmed Plan Letter, no deal in the forecast.
  • Committee-map enrichment. AI-assisted stakeholder mapping at the named-account level, including public title changes, LinkedIn signal, public conference appearances, and published thought leadership. Refreshed monthly.
  • Competitive battle-card refresh. AI-assisted quarterly refresh of the competitive-landscape matrix, pulled from public product pages, funding announcements, case studies, and trade-media coverage.
  • Forecast quality check. AI-assisted check on every forecasted deal against a rules engine: Plan Letter confirmed, committee coverage, security and privacy stage, MSOE step, named consulting-firm briefed. Any exception escalates to a weekly forecast review.

14.2 Where AI does not replace the seller

  • The Level 4 and Level 5 discovery moment with a CFO or CMO. That is a human judgment about pain and about political posture that AI does not read reliably.
  • The Plan Letter send moment. The customization that makes a Plan Letter land is the seller's read on the buyer's internal politics.
  • The consulting-firm relationship. Practice partners and principals want to know the person they are working with. Relationship equity does not transfer through a model.
  • The commercial negotiation. Pricing, term structure, and performance guarantee constructs sit inside a negotiation dynamic that is still a human game.

Every AI use above accelerates a human activity that is already part of the system. None replaces the human judgment at the buyer-facing moment. That philosophy is the same posture Function Health sells externally with its AI operating system for health. The GTM motion should model it internally.
