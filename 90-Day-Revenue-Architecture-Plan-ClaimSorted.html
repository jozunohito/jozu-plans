The Core Challenge

ClaimSorted is a services-led sale into the insurance industry. You are not asking buyers to add new software to their stack - you are asking them to replace their existing TPA. That means the sales cycle is faster (under 6 months, tapping existing TPA budget allocation, not new software budget) but the sale requires deep trust and credibility with claims leaders. The buyer is a VP of Claims, Head of Operations, or Chief Claims Officer at a carrier, MGA, or managing general underwriter who is frustrated with slow cycle times, high leakage, and poor transparency from their current TPA.

The Plan

90 Days to a Working Sales Engine

Phase 1: Days 1-30
Map the Market, Build the Target List
✓ Map the US insurance buyer landscape. Identify and segment the key buyer categories: carriers, MGAs, program administrators, and managing general underwriters. Understand who controls TPA selection decisions at each type of organization and what drives their buying criteria.
✓ Build the target list. Research and prioritize the top MGAs and specialty carriers in the US market. Focus on organizations with high claims volume, complex lines, and known frustration with legacy TPA performance. Build ideal-customer profiles and map buyer personas: VP Claims, Head of Operations, Chief Claims Officer.
✓ Develop ROI messaging. Translate ClaimSorted's results into language that resonates with insurance buyers: faster claims cycle times, lower leakage, reduced TPA costs, improved NPS. Build the economic case that makes switching from a legacy TPA to ClaimSorted a straightforward decision.
✓ Align with Pavel and the team. Work directly with leadership to understand the product roadmap, competitive positioning, and lessons learned from UK and existing US deals. Refine the US go-to-market approach weekly based on early outreach signals.
Phase 2: Days 31-60
Launch Outbound, Close First Enterprise Clients
✓ Launch outbound campaigns. Execute multi-channel outbound into the target list: email sequences, LinkedIn, cold calls, and warm introductions. Lead with the pain of legacy TPAs and the proof from existing customers. Every message ties back to measurable outcomes.
✓ Work deals through insurance procurement. Navigate the evaluation and procurement processes at carriers and MGAs. Build relationships with claims leaders, compliance teams, and operations stakeholders. Manage the buying committee and drive deals to close.
✓ Close first outbound enterprise clients. Convert pipeline into signed contracts. Use the Axis Capital, Lloyd's, and Collinson references to build credibility with new prospects. Make the risk of switching feel smaller than the cost of staying with a legacy TPA.
✓ Build the referral motion. Leverage existing happy customers to generate warm introductions. Axis, Lloyd's syndicates, and Benchmark are proof points that open doors. Structure a repeatable referral process that turns every successful implementation into pipeline.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Scale Pipeline, Define Channels, Plan for Growth
✓ Scale the pipeline. Expand outbound volume and refine targeting based on 60 days of real data. Identify which segments convert fastest, which messaging resonates, and where the highest-value opportunities live. Double down on what works.
✓ Define partnership channels. Map and pursue channel partnerships with insurance brokers, claims consultants, and reinsurers who influence TPA selection. These relationships create leverage beyond direct outbound and accelerate deal velocity.
✓ Hire the first AE. Based on 90 days of real selling, define the profile for the next sales hire. Document the playbook, the qualification criteria, and the deal stages so the first AE can ramp quickly and produce results.
✓ Present the 12-month revenue plan. Deliver a data-driven revenue plan to leadership: pipeline projections, conversion rates by segment, hiring plan, channel strategy, and revenue targets. The plan is built on real numbers from real selling, not assumptions.
