What Garner Has Built

Garner uses a proprietary analytics engine built on 320 million patient claims to identify the top 20% of in-network providers based on clinical outcomes and adherence to evidence-based guidelines. Members who use Garner to find top-performing doctors see 60% fewer hospitalizations and 75% lower complication rates. Employers see an average 12% reduction in total healthcare spending in the first year. The incentive model is elegant: Garner reimburses employees' out-of-pocket costs when they visit top-ranked providers, creating an 80% reduction in member costs alongside employer savings. The product works because it aligns incentives. Better doctors cost less, and now members have a financial reason to choose them.

Why Channel Is the Growth Multiplier

Garner's direct sales organization has driven 130% revenue growth and 700+ employer clients. That is exceptional execution. The channel opportunity multiplies that growth by putting Garner in front of every employer benefits decision in the country through the people who already influence those decisions: benefits brokers, consultants, health plans, PEOs, and captive insurance groups. Over 70% of employer benefits decisions involve a broker or consultant recommendation. Every major health plan is looking for care navigation products to differentiate their offerings. Every PEO and benefits captive needs tools to reduce claims costs for their members. The VP Channel Partner Sales role takes Garner from selling directly to employers to scaling through the entire employer benefits ecosystem.

The Competitive Window

Transcarent completed its $621M acquisition of Accolade, creating the largest health benefits navigation platform in the market. That merger consolidates their channel relationships and creates urgency for brokers and health plans to diversify their navigation partnerships. Nomi Health is building direct contracting networks that bypass traditional insurance structures. Quantum Health, Included Health, and Rightway are all investing in broker distribution. Garner has the strongest clinical outcomes data in the category (verified by analysis of 320 million claims) and the most aligned incentive model. The window to establish channel dominance is now, while the competitive landscape is reshuffling and brokers are actively evaluating alternatives to the Transcarent-Accolade combined entity.

Pillar 2: Health Plans

Garner already works with health plans to power provider rankings, directories, and network strategies. The channel opportunity expands this into a distribution model where health plans embed Garner as a value-added service within their employer offerings. When a health plan can tell an employer "Our network includes Garner's doctor quality analytics and member incentive program," that differentiates the plan in a commoditized market. Target the top 20 national and regional health plans with embedded partnership proposals that give them a competitive advantage in employer renewals while driving Garner membership at scale.

Pillar 3: PEOs and Captive Insurance Groups

PEOs manage benefits for hundreds of thousands of small businesses. Captive insurance groups pool employer risk to reduce costs. Both are actively seeking solutions that lower claims costs across their member populations. Garner's 12% average savings directly improves the PEO's or captive's loss ratio, making their offering more competitive and more profitable. Build a PEO and captive partnership track with pooled analytics, group implementation playbooks, and dedicated account management. A single PEO partnership can deliver tens of thousands of members in one agreement.

Pillar 4: Private Equity Portfolio Companies

Private equity firms managing portfolio companies are aggressively focused on healthcare cost reduction across their holdings. A PE firm with 30 portfolio companies and 50,000 total employees represents a single channel relationship that generates massive membership volume. Build a PE-focused offering with portfolio-wide analytics, standardized implementation across all portfolio companies, and executive reporting for the PE operating partners. The ROI conversation with PE is pure math: 12% healthcare savings across a portfolio generates millions in EBITDA improvement. That is a language PE operating partners speak fluently.

Growth Architecture

How Channel Revenue Compounds

Channel revenue compounds differently than direct sales. Each activated partner becomes a persistent source of pipeline that generates opportunities year after year through their own client base and renewal cycles. A broker partner activated in Q2 generates referrals every benefits renewal season indefinitely. The investment in partner enablement pays returns across multiple years.

The Benefits Renewal Cycle

Employer benefits decisions cluster around annual renewal windows, primarily Q4 and Q1. Every broker manages dozens to hundreds of employer renewals per year. A broker trained on Garner includes it in every renewal conversation where healthcare costs are a concern. That means a single broker partner generates a predictable cadence of qualified opportunities tied to their client renewal calendar. Build the channel program around this cycle: Q2 and Q3 for partner enablement and certification, Q4 and Q1 for deal execution. The channel team should know every major broker's renewal calendar and be prepared with updated ROI data and case studies for each cycle.

Multi-Product Expansion Through Partners

Garner's current product focuses on doctor quality navigation and incentive-based steering. As the product expands (AI-driven appointment booking, care plan management, pharmacy navigation), each new capability creates a new conversation for channel partners to have with existing clients. A broker who sold Garner's provider quality product to 20 clients now has 20 upsell conversations for each new product launch. Channel partners become the expansion sales force for the entire product roadmap, at zero incremental acquisition cost per feature launch.

Data Network Effects

Every employer that joins Garner through channel adds claims data to the analytics engine. More data means more accurate provider rankings, which means better outcomes, which means stronger ROI evidence for the next broker conversation. This creates a flywheel: channel growth improves the product, which improves channel conversion, which accelerates growth further. By the time a competitor tries to replicate Garner's approach, the data advantage will be insurmountable. The channel strategy is the growth strategy and the competitive moat, simultaneously.

Geographic Density

Garner's provider rankings are strongest in markets with the deepest claims data. Channel partners in those markets convert at the highest rates because the product delivers the most precise doctor recommendations. Build the partner recruitment strategy around geographic density: identify the top 20 MSAs where Garner's provider data is strongest, recruit the top 3 to 5 broker firms in each MSA, and create a density-driven expansion model. When Garner is the default recommendation from every major broker in a metro area, competitive displacement becomes nearly impossible.

Partnership and Channel Strategy

Building the Ecosystem That Sells Garner

Broker Advisory Council

Create a Broker Advisory Council of 10 to 15 top-performing broker partners who meet quarterly to provide product feedback, share market intelligence, and co-develop channel strategy. These partners get early access to new products, priority co-sell support, and direct input into Garner's roadmap. The Advisory Council creates a loyalty loop: partners who feel heard and valued become Garner's most aggressive advocates. They also provide real-time competitive intelligence from the field that no amount of market research can replicate.

Benefits Technology Integrations

Brokers and employers use benefits administration platforms (Benefitfocus, PlanSource, bswift, Employee Navigator) to manage enrollment and plan design. Build integrations with the top benefits administration platforms so Garner appears natively within the enrollment workflow. When an employee enrolling in their health plan sees Garner's provider quality tool embedded in the enrollment experience, activation rates increase dramatically. These integrations also make Garner easier for brokers to recommend because implementation requires zero additional technology deployment.

Industry Conference and Event Strategy

The benefits broker and consultant community gathers at specific industry events: BenefitsPRO Broker Expo, SHRM Annual Conference, Health Plan Alliance Forums, SIIA National Conference, and regional broker association meetings. Build a conference strategy that positions Garner as the outcomes-focused alternative in every channel conversation. Sponsor key events, host broker dinners, present clinical outcomes data, and use each event as a partner recruitment opportunity. The channel sale begins with awareness and credibility, and industry events are where both are built.

Co-Marketing and Thought Leadership

Build a co-marketing program that positions Garner and its broker partners as thought leaders in healthcare quality and cost management. Joint webinars, co-authored white papers on provider quality analytics, and shared case studies give brokers content they can use in their own client conversations. A broker who hosts a webinar with Garner on "How to Cut Healthcare Costs by 12% Without Changing Carriers" generates a pipeline of interested employers from their own client base. Every co-marketing effort the channel team invests in produces pipeline that the broker delivers.
