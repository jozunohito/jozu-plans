What Miro Has Built

Miro has evolved from a digital whiteboard into the AI Innovation Workspace. Canvas 2025 delivered Flows (visual AI workflows), Sidekicks (conversational AI agents), MCP integrations connecting Miro to developer tools, and Miro for Product Acceleration. The acquisition of Butter adds interactive workshop facilitation. AI Workflows launched in January 2026, and early adopters report cutting innovation cycle time by more than 50%. The product is no longer competing with whiteboards. It is competing for the operating system of how teams think, design, and build together.

What Needs to Happen Next

The Americas sales organization must evolve alongside the product. Miro's installed base of 90 million users is a massive asset, but the majority of that revenue comes from collaboration use cases sold to team leads and department managers. The AI Innovation Workspace creates an entirely new buying conversation: CIOs evaluating AI strategy, VPs of Product rethinking innovation workflows, and CTOs seeking platforms that connect strategy to code. Bigger buyers, bigger deals, longer commitments. The sales motion, the messaging, the skills of the 60+ sellers, and the segment strategy all need to shift in parallel with the product evolution.

The Core Challenge

Miro has dominant market share in collaborative whiteboards. That category is now commoditizing as Microsoft Whiteboard, Figma/FigJam, Zoom Whiteboard, and Canva add free or bundled whiteboard capabilities. The strategic imperative is clear: accelerate the transition to AI Innovation Workspace positioning before bundling pressure erodes the collaboration revenue base. Every quarter the Americas sales team spends defending whiteboard positioning is a quarter where the AI Innovation Workspace category leadership opportunity narrows. The sales organization needs to lead this transition with the market, pulling customers into the new value proposition before competitors define it.

Growth Architecture

Unlocking Expansion Revenue at Scale

Miro's installed base is the single largest revenue acceleration lever available. With 90 million users and 99% Fortune 100 adoption, the expansion opportunity dwarfs net-new acquisition. The Americas team should be generating 40% or more of annual bookings from expansion within 18 months.

Department-by-Department Expansion

Most Miro deployments start in Product or Design. The AI Innovation Workspace opens doors to Engineering (MCP integrations with Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code), Marketing (campaign planning and visual strategy), Operations (process mapping with AI Workflows), and Strategy (executive planning with Sidekicks). Each department is an incremental deal within the same account. Map the whitespace in every Strategic and Enterprise account and build expansion sequences for each new department.

Use Case Expansion

Miro started as a brainstorming tool. AI Workflows transform it into an execution platform. Customers using Miro for workshops should be introduced to Flows for automated multi-step processes. Customers using templates should see how Sidekicks generate custom AI agents for their specific workflows. Customers running design sprints should see Miro for Product Acceleration compressing weeks into hours. Every use case upgrade is a licensing upgrade, and the proof is already in the product: early adopters report more than 50% reduction in delivery time.

License Consolidation

Large enterprises often have dozens of separate Miro accounts across business units, each with different licensing tiers, different admins, and no centralized governance. This is both a risk (shadow IT, ungoverned AI usage) and an opportunity (enterprise license agreements that consolidate everything under one contract with volume pricing, centralized governance, and SSO/SCIM integration). The security and compliance conversation alone can justify the consolidation premium.

Platform Stickiness Through AI

Once teams build Flows and Sidekicks customized to their workflows, switching costs increase dramatically. A customer using Miro for brainstorming can switch to Microsoft Whiteboard in a week. A customer running their entire product development lifecycle through custom AI Workflows, with institutional knowledge embedded in Sidekicks, cannot leave without rebuilding their innovation infrastructure. AI adoption is the retention strategy. The sales team should be measured on AI feature activation rates alongside revenue targets.

Partnership and Channel Strategy

Accelerating Through Partnerships

Technology Partnerships

Miro's MCP integrations with Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Windsurf, and Claude Code create a natural co-sell motion with the developer tools ecosystem. Every engineering team using these AI coding tools is a prospect for Miro as the visual planning layer that feeds directly into their development workflow. Build joint pipeline programs with Atlassian (Jira integration is already deep), Slack, and Microsoft Teams. The integration story ("Miro lives where your teams already work") is a powerful competitive defense against bundled alternatives.

Consulting and SI Partnerships

The Big Four (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) and major consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Accenture) use Miro extensively for client engagements, design thinking workshops, and transformation programs. Every consulting engagement that uses Miro becomes a deployment recommendation to the client. Build a formal partner program that gives consulting firms branded Miro Engage capabilities, co-branded workshop templates, and client deployment acceleration packages. When Accenture recommends Miro as the innovation platform for a Fortune 500 transformation program, that is a seven-figure deal that costs zero acquisition dollars.

Reseller and Channel Expansion

As Miro moves upmarket into Strategic accounts, channel partners become critical for coverage and deal support. Identify regional VARs and technology distributors who already serve the Enterprise and Strategic buyer. Build a channel program with deal registration, margin structure, and enablement that equips partners to sell the AI Innovation Workspace narrative, not just whiteboard seats. International partners serving Americas-based subsidiaries of global companies are particularly valuable for multinational account coordination with the EMEA and APAC sales teams.

Strategic Platform Alliances

AWS, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure all have marketplace programs and co-sell motions. Listing Miro on cloud marketplaces allows enterprise customers to purchase through existing cloud commitments (drawdown against pre-committed cloud spend), which dramatically simplifies procurement and collapses the deal cycle for Strategic accounts. A CIO who has $50M in unused AWS credits and a mandate to consolidate innovation tools will choose the platform available on their marketplace every time. Prioritize cloud marketplace listings and co-sell agreements with cloud provider sales teams who are incentivized to drive marketplace consumption.

These sellers manage the largest relationships and need to think like business consultants, not product salespeople. Coaching emphasis on account strategy and executive engagement.

Coaching Philosophy

I coach from the deal, not from the classroom. Every coaching conversation starts with a live opportunity. What did the buyer say? What did you hear? What is the business problem? What is the cost of inaction? When we have those answers, the product demo is a formality. This approach works at scale because it creates a common language across the entire sales org. Whether I am reviewing a $50K Commercial deal or a $3M Strategic agreement, the questions are the same. The rigor is the same. The expectation is the same. That consistency is what separates high-performing sales organizations from teams that rely on individual heroics.
