Section 02

US Market Opportunity

The US AP automation market was valued at $6.94 billion in 2026 and is growing at a 12.44% CAGR, projected to reach $12.46 billion by 2031. North America commands 37% of global revenue. Large enterprises represent 60% of the market, but small and medium enterprises are the fastest growing segment at 18.15% CAGR. The market is large, growing, and fragmented enough for a differentiated entrant to capture meaningful share.

Why the US Market Is Ready for LayerX

Demand Drivers

  • US companies process an average of 500+ invoices per month, with AP teams spending 60% of their time on manual data entry and coding.
  • FedNow rollout is enabling instant interbank settlement, creating demand for AP systems that can keep pace.
  • CFOs are under pressure to reduce back-office headcount while maintaining compliance and control.
  • Remote and hybrid work has exposed the fragility of paper-based and legacy AP processes.

Technology Gap

  • Incumbent US platforms (Bill.com, Tipalti, Ramp) digitize AP but do not truly automate coding and categorization at LayerX's level.
  • Most US AP tools still require significant manual intervention for invoice matching, approval routing, and exception handling.
  • No US platform offers LayerX's combination of AI invoice processing, expense management, and corporate cards in a single integrated product.
  • The AI agent capability (90%+ automatic coding) is a genuine technical differentiation, not marketing language.

The Core Value Proposition for US Buyers

Every US finance leader has the same problem: their AP team is drowning in invoices, expense reports, and manual reconciliation. They have tried digitizing the process with tools like Bill.com or Tipalti, but the work still requires human intervention at every step. LayerX's Bakuraku platform is fundamentally different. Its AI agents learn your coding patterns and automate 90% of the work that your team currently does manually. The result is not just faster processing. It is a structurally different cost model for back-office operations, one that scales without adding headcount.

Section 03

Competitive Landscape

The US AP automation and spend management market is crowded but not consolidated. Each incumbent has carved out a niche, leaving meaningful white space for a platform that combines genuine AI automation with comprehensive spend management in a single product.

Competitor Positioning LayerX Advantage
Bill.com (BILL) Dual AP/AR platform for SMBs. Strong brand but limited AI automation. Relies on manual coding. LayerX's AI agents automate 90%+ of invoice coding. BILL requires manual categorization and matching for most transactions.
Tipalti Global AP automation for mid-market. Strong in multi-currency payments and tax compliance. LayerX offers deeper AI automation on the invoice processing side. Tipalti's strength is payment execution, not intelligent coding.
Ramp Corporate card and spend management. Free tier with strong UX. Growing fast in SMB. Ramp leads with cards and adds AP. LayerX leads with AI-powered AP automation and adds cards. Different entry point, different depth of automation.
Brex Corporate cards, expense management, bill pay. Unified spend platform for startups and mid-market. Brex is a financial product company. LayerX is an AI company that happens to serve finance teams. The automation depth is structurally different.
SAP Concur Enterprise expense management and travel. Dominant in large enterprise but aging UX. LayerX's modern AI-native architecture vs. Concur's legacy platform. Faster to deploy, lower total cost, better automation.
Coupa Enterprise spend management (BSM). Acquired by Thoma Bravo. Comprehensive but complex. Coupa requires 6 to 12 month implementations. LayerX deploys in weeks with AI that learns from day one.

Competitive Positioning Summary

LayerX should not try to out-feature Ramp on cards or out-scale BILL on SMB volume. The winning position is clear: LayerX is the AI-first back-office platform. Every competitor digitized the AP workflow. LayerX automates it. The 90%+ automatic coding rate is not a roadmap item for competitors. It is the result of years of AI development and 15,000 customer deployments in Japan. That technical lead is real, and it translates directly to measurable cost savings for US buyers. Position against the category, not against individual competitors.

Partnership and Channel Strategy

LayerX's US market entry requires partnerships across four categories to generate deal flow, establish credibility, and create distribution that a standalone sales team cannot match in year one.

Accounting and ERP Platforms

Integration partnerships with the platforms US finance teams already use daily. These integrations are table stakes for enterprise AP automation and create marketplace distribution.

  • NetSuite (Oracle): The dominant mid-market ERP. Integration is essential for the priority buyer segment. NetSuite's SuiteApp marketplace provides visibility to thousands of target accounts.
  • Sage Intacct: Growing fast in mid-market accounting. Deep integration with approval workflows and GL coding.
  • QuickBooks Enterprise (Intuit): Entry point for companies graduating from basic accounting into more sophisticated AP automation.
  • SAP S/4HANA: LayerX already integrates with SAP in Japan. Extend this integration to the US for larger enterprise opportunities.
  • Partnership Model: Certified integrations, marketplace listings, co-marketing with ERP partner sales teams.

Accounting Firms and Outsourced Finance Providers

Accounting firms influence technology decisions for thousands of mid-market companies. A single partnership with a Top 25 firm can generate more qualified pipeline than months of direct outbound.

  • Top 25 Accounting Firms: BDO, Grant Thornton, RSM, Crowe, CohnReznick. Each has hundreds of mid-market clients who need AP automation.
  • Outsourced CFO/Controller Firms: Fractional finance firms that manage AP for multiple companies simultaneously. Each firm is a multi-account opportunity.
  • Japan Practice Desks: Big Four firms (EY, PwC, KPMG, Deloitte) all maintain Japan practice desks serving Japanese companies operating in the US. These desks are warm introduction channels for the beachhead segment.
  • Partnership Model: Referral agreements, co-branded implementation support, volume licensing for multi-client deployments.

Industry Associations and Trade Organizations

Finance and technology associations provide conference access, buyer engagement, and credibility in a market where LayerX has no US brand recognition yet.

  • AFP (Association for Financial Professionals): 16,000+ members including treasury, AP, and finance operations leaders. Annual conference is the premier US event for finance technology.
  • IMA (Institute of Management Accountants): Controllers and management accountants who evaluate and approve AP technology purchases.
  • AICPA: Certified public accountants who advise clients on financial systems. Thought leadership and certification partnerships.
  • SaaStr: US SaaS community. Showcase LayerX's T2D3 growth story and AI differentiation to attract technology-forward US buyers.

Japan-US Business Networks

LayerX's Japanese heritage is a strategic asset for the beachhead segment. Japan-US business organizations provide warm introductions, market research, and credibility with Japanese companies operating in the US.

  • JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization): Market entry support, business matching events, and conference sponsorships designed specifically for Japanese companies expanding to the US.
  • Japanese Chamber of Commerce (US chapters): Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, and San Francisco chapters provide direct access to Japanese company US executives.
  • US-Japan Business Council: Executive-level introductions between Japanese and American business leaders.
  • TCV Network: TCV's portfolio companies (Netflix, Spotify, Zillow alumni) represent a warm network of US technology executives who can serve as advisors, early adopters, and references.

90 Day GTM Execution Plan

LayerX has the funding, the product, and the growth trajectory. The US execution plan needs to answer the critical questions first: which segments convert, what messaging resonates, and what the US sales motion looks like, before committing to headcount and office space.

Days 1 to 30: Foundation

Build US Market Intelligence and Infrastructure

Market Validation

  • Conduct 15 to 20 discovery conversations with US finance leaders to validate positioning and pricing
  • Map the US AP automation buying process: who initiates, who evaluates, who approves, what triggers the purchase
  • Define US ICP: company size, invoice volume, current tools, pain thresholds, budget authority
  • Identify 3 to 5 Japanese companies operating in the US as beachhead targets

Messaging and Localization

  • Develop US-market positioning from the ground up. Not translated Japanese materials. US buyer language.
  • Create ROI calculator: "What does your AP team cost per invoice processed? What if 90% of that was automated?"
  • Build competitive battle cards for Bill.com, Tipalti, Ramp, Brex, and Concur
  • Produce product demonstration optimized for US buyer expectations (speed, integration, ROI focus)
Days 31 to 60: Activation

Launch Outbound and Channel Development

Direct Pipeline

  • Begin outbound sequences to 100+ target accounts across mid-market and beachhead segments
  • Lead with AI automation differentiation: "90% of your manual invoice coding, eliminated from day one."
  • Target AFP annual conference and 2 to 3 regional finance technology events
  • Schedule 10 to 15 live product demonstrations with qualified prospects

Channel Development

  • Initiate NetSuite and Sage Intacct integration certifications
  • Approach 3 to 5 mid-market accounting firms about referral partnerships
  • Engage Japan practice desks at Big Four firms for beachhead introductions
  • Connect with JETRO US programs and Japanese Chamber of Commerce chapters
Days 61 to 90: Acceleration

Convert Conversations to Pipeline and First Revenue

Pipeline Targets

  • 20+ qualified meetings completed across all segments
  • 5 to 8 active evaluation or pilot engagements
  • 1 to 3 signed customers (prioritize beachhead Japanese companies for speed)
  • Pipeline value: $1M to $2M in identified opportunities

Strategic Decisions

  • Validate US pricing model based on real market feedback (per-invoice, per-user, or platform fee)
  • Determine US entity and compliance requirements (SOC 2, data residency, state-level regulations)
  • Refine ICP based on 90 days of data: which segments convert, which objections are hardest, which channels produce
  • Present 12-month US expansion plan to LayerX leadership and TCV with data-backed recommendations
