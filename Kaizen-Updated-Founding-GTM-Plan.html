Core Thesis

Why Browser Automation Wins in Healthcare

The Problem Is Not the Portal. The Problem Is the Labor Inside It.

Healthcare runs on portals. Payer portals, provider portals, credentialing portals, pharmacy benefit portals, CMS portals. Every stakeholder in the system - plans, providers, RCM companies, BPOs - employs people whose entire job is to log in, navigate, enter data, extract data, and move to the next portal.

These portals are not going away. They are deeply integrated into claims adjudication, member databases, provider networks, and regulatory reporting. Replacing a major payer portal is a multi-year, $50M-$200M IT project with massive compliance risk. Nobody is doing it.

Kaizen does not replace the portal. It makes the portal irrelevant as a friction point. The workflows still execute inside the portal - but without a human spending 25 minutes per transaction navigating them manually.

Outside the Scope - Be Honest About These

  • Voice/phone workflows - Call center interactions, IVR systems, phone-based support. Browser automation does not replace phone calls.
  • Document processing - Reading faxes, extracting data from scanned documents, OCR. Requires upstream document AI.
  • Clinical decision-making - Any workflow requiring medical judgment, diagnosis, or treatment decisions.
  • Desktop application automation - Legacy systems running on Windows/Mac desktop apps, not in a browser.
  • Outbound communication - Sending emails, texts, or making calls to patients or members.
Why Honesty About Scope Matters

The fastest way to lose credibility with a VP of Revenue Cycle is to promise that one tool solves everything. Positioning Kaizen as the definitive solution for portal-based workflow automation - and being clear about what it does not do - builds trust and shortens the sales cycle. The buyer already knows their problem is complex. They respect a vendor who scopes honestly.

Competitive Landscape

Why Deterministic Execution Is the Moat

The Reliability Problem in AI Automation

Most AI browser agents (Skyvern, generic LLM agents) re-reason through every step of a workflow using large language models. This means the AI "thinks" about what to do at every click, every form field, every navigation step - every single time it runs.

The result: 85-95% success rates. That sounds good until you realize that at healthcare scale, a 5-15% failure rate on prior auth submissions means thousands of patients not getting their medications, millions in lost revenue, and compliance exposure.

Kaizen's architecture is fundamentally different. Computer vision maps the workflow once. Every action is cached. Subsequent executions replay the cached path identically. No re-reasoning, no variation, no drift. The same input produces the same output every time. That is deterministic execution - and for healthcare buyers, it is the difference between a pilot and a production deployment.

Market Dynamics

RCM and BPO Companies Are Customers, Not Competitors

Two distinct buyer types sit in this space. The distinction matters because their motivations, contract structures, and decision-making processes differ - and conflating them in a sales conversation signals a lack of domain depth.

Healthcare RCM Companies

Healthcare-specific. Revenue cycle is their entire business.

  • R1 RCM - Largest pure-play RCM, $2B+ revenue
  • Ensemble Health Partners - End-to-end revenue cycle
  • Conifer Health Solutions - Tenet Healthcare subsidiary
  • nThrive / Finvi - Claims and AR management
  • Optum Revenue Cycle - UnitedHealth Group arm

These companies employ thousands of people whose primary job is navigating payer portals - checking claims, submitting PAs, managing denials, updating credentialing records. Portal work is their core labor cost.

Cross-Industry BPO/BPaaS Companies

Outsourcing firms that serve healthcare as one of many verticals.

  • Accenture - Cut 22,000 roles, rehiring 4,000 in AI/data
  • Sutherland Global - Building proprietary "Cognitive Knowledge Engine," repositioning as "business process transformation"
  • Cognizant - Launched AI Multi-Agent Accelerator
  • Conduent, EXL, WNS - Healthcare BPO practices

These firms bill on time-and-materials with 20-30% markup on labor. Their healthcare divisions handle claims, enrollment, PA, and member services, but their technology decisions are often made at the enterprise level, not the healthcare practice level.

The Innovator's Dilemma Hits Both

Every workflow they automate reduces the hours they can bill. This is the classic innovator's dilemma - they cannot aggressively adopt AI without cannibalizing their own revenue model.

But the market is forcing their hand. Healthcare providers and plans are demanding automation-linked pricing, pay-for-performance penalties, and outcome-based contracts. Organizations that do not automate will lose deals to competitors who do.

Kaizen is the tool that lets these companies automate without building the technology themselves. They keep the client relationship, keep the domain expertise, keep the contract - but serve more clients with fewer people. Kaizen makes each employee 5-10x more productive on portal work.
