Why This Market, Why Now

The eye care EHR market is dominated by legacy platforms that were built for general healthcare and adapted for ophthalmology and optometry. Providers in this specialty face unique clinical workflows, from refraction data and contact lens management to surgical planning and myopia tracking, that generic EHR systems handle poorly.

The consolidation wave in eye care (private equity roll-ups, DSO formation, and multi-location expansion) is creating demand for platforms that can scale across practices while maintaining clinical specificity. At the same time, the labor shortage in healthcare administration is pushing practices toward AI-powered automation. Barti is positioned at the intersection of both trends.

Buyer Pain in Eye Care

  • "Our EHR slows us down instead of helping us." - Eye care providers spend hours documenting exams in systems designed for primary care. Click-heavy interfaces and template limitations force workarounds that reduce patient volume and increase burnout.
  • "We cannot find front desk staff." - The healthcare labor shortage is acute in small and mid-size practices. Phone management, appointment scheduling, and patient intake consume time that practices cannot staff for.
  • "We are stuck on a platform from 2010." - Legacy EHR vendors have not invested in AI or modern workflows. Practices are locked into multi-year contracts with vendors that deliver incremental updates rather than transformational capabilities.
  • "Our billing is disconnected from our clinical system." - Many practices run separate billing and practice management tools alongside their EHR, creating data silos, reconciliation headaches, and revenue leakage.
Market Timing
Barti enters its growth phase at the exact moment when eye care practices are seeking AI-native alternatives to legacy EHR platforms. The AOAExcel endorsement provides institutional credibility that would take any competitor years to replicate, and the ophthalmology expansion doubles the addressable market.

Buyer Segments

Target Accounts, Decision Makers, and Deal Mechanics

Segment 1: Independent Optometry Practices

Profile

Solo and small group optometry practices (1 to 5 providers) looking for a modern, all-in-one platform that reduces administrative overhead and improves patient throughput. These practices typically run on legacy EHR systems with separate billing tools.

Decision Makers

  • Practice Owner / OD - Clinical and business authority. Makes the technology decision directly.
  • Office Manager - Operational influencer. Evaluates workflow impact and training requirements.

Deal Characteristics

  • ACV range: $8K to $25K per practice
  • Sales cycle: 30 to 60 days
  • Demo-to-close motion with free trial or pilot period
  • Volume play with high velocity

Segment 2: Multi-Location Groups and DSOs

Profile

Dental Support Organization (DSO) equivalents in eye care, private equity-backed groups, and multi-location practices with 5 to 100+ locations. These buyers need platform standardization, centralized reporting, and scalable infrastructure.

Decision Makers

  • CEO / Managing Partner - Strategic buyer. Cares about operational efficiency across locations.
  • VP of Operations - Evaluates platform scalability and implementation complexity.
  • Clinical Director - Validates clinical workflow quality and provider satisfaction.

Deal Characteristics

  • ACV range: $50K to $500K+ (based on location count)
  • Sales cycle: 60 to 120 days
  • Pilot at 2 to 3 locations before enterprise rollout
  • Multi-year contracts with expansion built in

Segment 3: Ophthalmology Practices

Profile

Surgical ophthalmology practices and ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs) expanding from optometry into surgical workflows. This segment represents Barti's growth market as the platform extends into ophthalmology capabilities.

Decision Makers

  • Lead Surgeon / Medical Director - Clinical authority. Evaluates surgical workflow support.
  • Practice Administrator - Operational buyer. Manages revenue cycle and compliance.

Deal Characteristics

  • ACV range: $30K to $150K
  • Sales cycle: 60 to 90 days
  • Clinical validation critical before commitment
  • Higher switching costs from incumbent platforms

Segment 4: Academic and Institutional Eye Care

Profile

Optometry schools, university eye clinics, and teaching hospitals with eye care departments. These institutions train the next generation of providers, creating long-term brand adoption patterns.

Decision Makers

  • Department Chair / Dean - Curriculum and technology selection authority
  • IT Director - Integration and compliance requirements

Deal Characteristics

  • ACV range: $15K to $75K
  • Sales cycle: 90 to 180 days (academic procurement)
  • Strategic value beyond revenue: graduates trained on Barti become lifelong users
  • Reference and credibility value for commercial sales
