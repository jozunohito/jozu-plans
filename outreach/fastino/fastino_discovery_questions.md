# Fastino 20-Minute Discovery Questions

Six questions Jeff would ask in the first 20-minute call with Fastino founders. Calibrated to LLM and agentic system infrastructure, the founding-sales charter, and Fastino's specific cap-table position. Each question is engineered to surface a Level 4 economic anchor and validate one of the load-bearing assumptions in the 90-day plan.

Use in order. Do not rush. Listen for the operator-grade follow-on signals listed under each question.

---

## Question 1 (The wedge in the buyer's voice)

When a design partner CFO or VP of Platform Engineering finally signs off on Fastino as a production vendor, what is the one sentence they say back to you that proves they understood the wedge?

**Why this question.** Surfaces the Power Message in the buyer's actual language, not in Fastino's marketing language. The gap between the two is the work the Founding Head of Sales is hired to close.

**Listen for:** specific economic anchors (cost per workflow, latency p95, GPU footprint), specific stakeholder titles, specific objection patterns the buyer overcame internally.

---

## Question 2 (The Series A revenue story)

What is the revenue number you need committed by the time you raise the Series A, and what is the deal-shape mix (PLG-converted versus enterprise top-down versus OEM versus partner-attached) that you think gets you there?

**Why this question.** Forces explicit alignment on the Year One forecast contribution shape. Surfaces whether the founders see this as a PLG-led story, an enterprise-led story, or a mixed motion. Determines how the founding seller's time is spent in the first 90 days.

**Listen for:** dollar number, deal mix, timing pressure, board expectations, current pipeline coverage, current gap.

---

## Question 3 (The hyperscaler reality)

Microsoft is direct on the cap table, and the GitHub and Docker CEOs are personal investors. How operationalized is that distribution today: Azure Marketplace listing status, Microsoft AI Co-Sell readiness, GitHub partner motion, portfolio-introduction cadence with Khosla and Insight?

**Why this question.** Validates the assumption in Initiative 3 of the 90-day plan that hyperscaler co-sell is reachable inside Year One. Surfaces whether the founders treat the cap-table relationships as active distribution or passive credibility.

**Listen for:** named contacts at Microsoft, named portfolio-introduction conversations, marketplace listing status, whether anyone has been asked to operationalize this layer yet.

---

## Question 4 (The competitive shape)

When you lose a deal to OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, or Cohere, what is the actual reason the buyer named in writing, and how often does the loss come from "we already have an OpenAI relationship" versus a real product gap?

**Why this question.** Surfaces the realistic competitive map. Switching cost and brand inertia versus product gap are very different sales problems and require different battle cards. Validates the win/loss hypotheses in Section 9 of the 90-day plan.

**Listen for:** specific competitor names, specific deal-loss reasons, switching-cost language, brand-inertia language, accuracy-gap language.

---

## Question 5 (The agentic-system production reality)

How many of your live design partners are running production agentic systems where Fastino sits inside a multi-step agent loop, and what is the per-task economics gap you see between Fastino and a frontier model on those agentic workloads?

**Why this question.** Tests the assumption in the 90-day plan that 2026 agentic-system production deployment is a high-leverage workflow wedge for Fastino. Surfaces whether agentic workloads are a real wedge or a marketing wedge today.

**Listen for:** named design partners running agentic in production, per-task cost numbers, latency-budget conversation, agentic-orchestration framework names (LangChain, LlamaIndex, internal frameworks).

---

## Question 6 (The founder-attached cadence)

How much of your week, realistically, can you spend on customer-facing calls in the first 90 days of a new Founding Head of Sales onboarding, and which stakeholder moments do you want to be in the room for versus delegate?

**Why this question.** Validates the assumption in Initiative 2 of the 90-day plan that the founders are available for the first three calls of every enterprise opportunity. Founding-stage sales at $25M seed lives or dies on founder-attached cadence in the first six months. If the founders are unavailable, the plan needs to adjust.

**Listen for:** named hours per week, named call types they are willing to take (kickoff, technical deep-dive, executive close), comfort level with structured cadence, comfort level with Plan Letter discipline.

---

## Closing the discovery call

End the 20 minutes with one direct ask: "Based on this conversation, what would the next conversation look like, and who else needs to be in it?" That question forces the Mutual Sequence of Events into the open and tests whether the founders treat the next step as a buyer-confirmed step or a hopeful step. The answer is forecast data.
