Company Overview

Vidext is a Spanish AI startup redefining business process automation through artificial intelligence. The platform transforms written content into professional, personalized videos using AI, enabling companies to automate repetitive tasks, increase operational efficiency, and gain actionable insights from their content workflows.

The EUR 6M Series A was led by Flashpoint, an international tech investment firm with approximately $500M in AUM. HWK, 4Founders Capital, Sabadell Venture Capital, and DraperB1 also participated. The company has publicly stated that the US is the 2026 expansion priority.

Leadership

  • Benat Arrizabalaga, Co-Founder and CSO - Driving sales strategy and customer acquisition
  • Andoni Enriquez, Co-Founder and CPTO - Leading product development and technical platform architecture

Expansion Timeline

UK operations opened in 2025. US market entry planned for 2026. The company is building toward becoming a global player in AI business automation.

Product Platform

AI Video Generation

Converts written content, documents, and training materials into professional video presentations with AI-generated presenters. Supports multiple languages and personalization at scale.

Enterprise L&D Automation

Purpose-built for corporate training, onboarding, and compliance video production. Eliminates the cost and time of traditional video production while maintaining professional quality.

Business Process Automation

Goes beyond video creation to automate entire content workflows, from document ingestion to multi-format output distribution across internal communications channels.

02 - Market Analysis

US AI Video and Enterprise L&D Landscape

The AI video generation market is growing at over 30% annually. Within this, the enterprise segment focused on training, L&D, and internal communications represents the fastest-growing category. US enterprises spend over $100B annually on employee training, with video becoming the dominant delivery format.

Market Tailwinds

  • Remote and hybrid workforce - Distributed teams need scalable video content for training and communication. Traditional in-person training does not reach remote employees effectively
  • L&D budget growth - US companies increased L&D spending 8% year-over-year in 2025. Video-based training shows 65% higher retention than text-based materials
  • Compliance training mandates - Regulated industries require documented training completion. AI video production reduces the cost of creating and updating compliance content by 80%+
  • Content velocity pressure - Product updates, policy changes, and market shifts require constant training updates. Manual video production cannot keep pace with the speed of change in modern enterprises

Buyer Landscape

Segment Primary Buyer Key Pain Point Vidext Fit
Enterprise (5,000+ employees) VP Learning and Development, CHRO Training content production is slow, expensive, and cannot scale across global teams Enterprise-grade AI video at scale, multilingual support, LMS integration
Mid-Market (500-5,000) Director of L&D, Director of HR Cannot afford video production teams but need professional training content Self-service platform eliminating need for production resources
Regulated Industries Compliance Officer, VP Operations Compliance training must be updated frequently, documented, and trackable Rapid content updates, version control, completion tracking
Core Market Thesis
Every US enterprise with more than 500 employees produces training content. Most of it is either expensive video (produced by agencies at $5K-$50K per video), low-engagement text documents, or outdated slide decks recorded over Zoom. Vidext sits at the intersection of quality and scalability, delivering professional video content at the cost of a document and the speed of a template. The US market is ready for this because the alternatives all have obvious limitations.

Vidext should not position against Synthesia on features. The winning message: "Synthesia is a video creation tool. Vidext is a business automation platform that happens to produce video." This positions Vidext as the solution for buyers who need to transform entire content workflows, not just create individual videos. The platform story is bigger than the video story.

Competitive Moat

05 - Partnership Channel Strategy

Building US Revenue Through L&D and HR Tech Ecosystem

The US L&D technology market is driven by ecosystem relationships. HR buyers trust recommendations from their existing LMS, HCM, and consulting partners. For Vidext, channel partnerships are the fastest path to credibility and pipeline in a market where Synthesia has a head start.

LMS and HCM Integration Partners

Priority Partners

  • Workday Learning - Largest enterprise HCM platform. SCORM-compatible video content flows directly into Workday Learning modules
  • Cornerstone OnDemand - Leading LMS for enterprise training. Integration enables seamless content delivery and completion tracking
  • SAP SuccessFactors - Enterprise HCM with strong mid-market and enterprise adoption. Video training integration adds immediate value
  • Docebo - AI-powered LMS growing rapidly in the US market. Natural partnership for AI-generated video content

Value Exchange

LMS partners need content to fill their platforms. Most L&D teams struggle to produce enough quality video. Vidext solves the content bottleneck for LMS partners, making their platforms more valuable. Joint customers get content creation and delivery in a single workflow.

HR Consulting and L&D Advisory Firms

Target Partners

  • Deloitte Human Capital - Workforce transformation practice recommending L&D technology to enterprise clients
  • Korn Ferry - Leadership development and talent management advisory with deep enterprise relationships
  • GP Strategies - Managed learning services provider handling L&D operations for Fortune 500 companies
  • Training Industry consultants - Influencer network that shapes enterprise L&D technology decisions

Value Exchange

Consulting firms earn implementation and optimization revenue. Vidext earns trusted recommendations from advisors who are already in the room when L&D technology decisions are made.

Content and Communications Partners

Target Partners

  • Internal communications platforms - Staffbase, Simpplr, and Firstup distribute video content to employees. Integration creates a content-to-distribution pipeline
  • Knowledge management tools - Guru, Notion, and Confluence hold the source content that Vidext transforms into video
  • Compliance platforms - ComplianceWire, SAI360, and Diligent handle compliance training workflows. Video integration improves completion rates

Value Exchange

Content and communications partners need engaging formats to drive employee adoption. Video consistently outperforms text in engagement metrics. Vidext transforms their existing content into higher-performing formats.

Industry-Specific Channel Partners

Vertical Focus

  • Healthcare training providers - Clinical training, safety protocols, and HIPAA compliance require frequent video updates. Healthcare-specific partners provide domain credibility
  • Financial services compliance - SEC, FINRA, and AML training mandates create recurring content needs. Regulated industries pay premium pricing
  • Manufacturing safety training - OSHA compliance and safety protocol videos are required by law. Multilingual support is essential for diverse factory workforces

Value Exchange

Industry-specific partners bring domain expertise and buyer trust. Vidext brings the technology platform. Together they create vertical solutions that command higher pricing and longer contracts than horizontal tools.

Channel Revenue Target
Within 12 months of US market entry, LMS integrations and consulting partnerships should generate 20-30% of new pipeline. A single LMS integration partnership (Cornerstone or Docebo) can influence 200+ enterprise accounts annually. This is higher-leverage than hiring 5 additional sales reps.
06 - 90-Day GTM Execution Plan

From European Traction to US Market Entry

This plan builds the commercial foundation for Vidext's US market entry. The priority is not hiring a large US team immediately. It is validating positioning against US competitors, building initial pipeline in high-value segments, and establishing the LMS partnerships that will generate demand before a permanent sales organization is in place.

Phase 1: Days 1-30, Positioning and Market Validation

US Market Positioning

  • Reframe messaging for US L&D buyers. European SaaS messaging often leads with technology. US L&D buyers care about time-to-content, cost per video, completion rates, and LMS compatibility. Every piece of messaging should tie to one of these metrics
  • Build competitive positioning against Synthesia, HeyGen, and Colossyan. Synthesia is the primary competitor in the US enterprise L&D space. The battle card must address their SCORM advantage and show how Vidext's broader automation platform provides more value
  • Develop ROI calculators comparing Vidext to traditional video production. US enterprise buyers need to justify spend to finance. A clear "cost per video" comparison that shows 10-50x savings is the fastest path to budget approval

Target Account Development

  • Build a named account list of 150 US enterprises with 1,000+ employees in healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and technology. These verticals have the highest training content volume and budget
  • Map VP L&D, Director of Training, and CHRO contacts at top 75 accounts. L&D buyers are accessible through LinkedIn, industry conferences, and professional associations
  • Identify 25 accounts currently using Synthesia or HeyGen as competitive displacement targets. These buyers already understand the category, making the sales conversation about differentiation rather than education
Phase 2: Days 31-60, Pipeline Generation and Partnerships

Outbound Pipeline Generation

  • Launch targeted outbound sequences to VP L&D and Director of Training personas. Lead message: "Your L&D team gets 50 training video requests per quarter and can produce 5. Vidext closes that gap without hiring a production team"
  • Execute account-based campaigns in healthcare and financial services, where compliance training volume creates the strongest ROI case
  • Attend ATD (Association for Talent Development) conference and Learning Technologies events. US L&D buyers rely heavily on industry events and peer recommendations for technology decisions

LMS Partnership Activation

  • Initiate integration partnerships with Cornerstone OnDemand and Docebo. Both have partner programs designed for content creation tools. Getting listed in their marketplace creates passive demand generation
  • Build SCORM-compatible output to match Synthesia's LMS integration advantage. This is a table-stakes requirement for enterprise L&D buyers
  • Develop a partner demo environment showing the document-to-video-to-LMS workflow. Partners need to see the complete value chain to recommend Vidext to their clients
Phase 3: Days 61-90, Close First Deals and Scale

Deal Progression

  • Convert pilot programs into annual contracts. Target 5-8 pilots in progress by day 45, with at least 3 converting by day 90
  • Close first US mid-market deals ($15K-$50K ACV) to prove the sales motion before pursuing larger enterprise opportunities
  • Document first US customer case studies with measurable outcomes: videos produced per month, cost savings, time reduction, and completion rate improvements

US Infrastructure

  • Hire first US-based Account Executive with L&D or HR tech sales experience. Location in New York or Chicago, where L&D buyer density is highest
  • Establish US data residency capabilities if not already in place. Enterprise L&D buyers in healthcare and financial services require US-based data processing
  • Set up US entity for direct contracting and payment processing
