The Company

Astelia is an AI-native exposure management platform built by the former leaders of Israel's National Red Team. The company was founded in late 2024 through Team8's Venture Creation model and is headquartered at 488 Madison Avenue, New York. Astelia has raised $35M in combined seed and Series A funding led by Index Ventures and Team8, with participation from Holly Ventures.

The platform replaces vulnerability guesswork with proof-based exposure analysis. By mapping real network topology and applying agentic AI to analyze exploit requirements, Astelia identifies the small fraction of vulnerabilities - roughly 2% - that are genuinely reachable and exploitable. In one documented deployment, the platform reduced nearly 3 million vulnerabilities down to 30 that required urgent attention.

Platform Capabilities

  • Network Topology Mapping - Integrates with infrastructure tools to analyze configurations and policy rules, building comprehensive topology models from the inside out
  • Agentic Vulnerability Analysis - AI-driven analysis of each CVE's execution context, attack vectors, and real-world usage patterns to determine actual exploitability
  • Reachability Analysis - Correlates network topology, asset context, and runtime data to classify true exposure risk, not theoretical CVSS scores
  • Attack Path Visualization - Maps attack paths to critical assets through network controls, open ports, and runtime context

  • Multi-Path Remediation - Offers remediation beyond patching, including compensating controls and network configuration changes to reduce MTTR

Why This Market, Why Now

Gartner predicts that by 2026, organizations prioritizing security investments based on a Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM) program will be three times less likely to suffer a breach. This is driving a massive shift in security budgets from reactive vulnerability scanning toward proactive exposure management.

The vulnerability management market alone is projected to reach $24B by 2030. But the real opportunity sits at the intersection of vulnerability data and actionable exposure intelligence. Legacy tools generate millions of findings. Security teams drown in noise. The winners in this market will be the platforms that answer one question: "Which of these actually matter?"

Buyer Pain - What CISOs Are Saying

  • "We have too many vulnerabilities and no way to prioritize." - Enterprise security teams manage millions of CVEs. CVSS scores alone do not account for network context, asset criticality, or actual reachability. Teams waste cycles patching low-risk issues while critical exposures persist.
  • "Our vulnerability scanner tells us what is wrong but not what is dangerous." - Traditional scanners produce volume. They do not correlate findings with network topology, runtime context, or real attack paths. The gap between "vulnerable" and "exploitable" is enormous.
  • "We cannot show the board our actual risk posture." - Boards want clear answers. How exposed are we? Where are the gaps? What should we fix first? Legacy tools deliver spreadsheets, not answers.
  • "Patching everything is impossible, and our teams are burned out." - Security and IT teams are understaffed and overwhelmed. They need evidence-based prioritization that reduces the remediation backlog to what genuinely matters.
Market Timing
Astelia enters the US market at the exact inflection point where CTEM adoption is accelerating, legacy vendors are struggling to evolve beyond scanning, and enterprise buyers are actively seeking proof-based approaches. The founders' red team pedigree gives Astelia a narrative advantage that no incumbent can replicate.

Competitive Positioning
Astelia is not competing to replace vulnerability scanners. It is competing to become the intelligence layer that sits above them. The positioning is "proof-based exposure management" - a category that assumes you already have scanning tools and need something to make their output actionable. This is a wedge strategy, not a platform war.
