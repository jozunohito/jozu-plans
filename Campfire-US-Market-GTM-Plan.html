Why This Market, Why Now

The ERP market is undergoing its most significant disruption in two decades. Legacy platforms like NetSuite, Sage Intacct, and SAP were built before AI was viable and have bolted on automation features without reimagining the core architecture. Finance teams are stuck in manual month-end close processes, spreadsheet-based reconciliation, and workflow bottlenecks that AI-native platforms can eliminate.

The timing is driven by three converging forces. First, growing companies are hitting the "QuickBooks ceiling" earlier as financial complexity accelerates. Second, CFOs are under pressure to close faster, forecast more accurately, and operate with leaner teams. Third, the emergence of large language models trained on domain-specific data (like Campfire's LAM) makes it possible to automate accounting tasks that previously required human judgment. Campfire is positioned at the intersection of all three.

Buyer Pain in Finance Operations

  • "Month-end close takes our team two weeks." - Finance teams at growing companies spend days on manual reconciliation, journal entries, and variance analysis. Every hour spent closing the books is an hour not spent on strategic analysis or forecasting.
  • "We outgrew QuickBooks but cannot afford a NetSuite implementation." - The gap between starter accounting tools and enterprise ERP systems forces growing companies into expensive, complex migrations that consume 6 to 12 months and hundreds of thousands in implementation fees.
  • "Our ERP requires an army of consultants to maintain." - Legacy ERP platforms create ongoing dependency on implementation partners for configuration changes, report building, and system upgrades. Finance teams lose control of their own systems.
  • "We cannot trust our forecast numbers." - Manual data aggregation, disconnected systems, and spreadsheet-based models produce forecasts that are outdated before they are presented. Boards and investors demand accuracy that manual processes cannot deliver.
Market Timing
Campfire's 10x revenue growth validates that the market is ready for an AI-native ERP alternative. The $100M+ raised in 12 weeks signals investor conviction that this disruption window is real and that Campfire is the company best positioned to capture it. The next 90 days are about converting market momentum into scalable, repeatable revenue operations.

Phase 2: Days 31 to 60 - Pipeline Generation

Partnership Development

Phase 3: Days 61 to 90 - Pipeline Conversion

Deal Progression

  • Advance 15 to 25 qualified opportunities into technical evaluation or pilot stage. Deploy Campfire with prospect financial data to demonstrate LAM accuracy, close time reduction, and workflow automation in their actual environment.

  • Establish QBR cadence with channel partners to measure partner-sourced pipeline, joint win rates, and integration adoption.

Metrics and Milestones

  • Pipeline target: qualified pipeline representing significant ARR growth by Day 90

  • Pilot conversion: 50 to 60% of pilots converting to signed contracts
