1. The System I Run

Every plan I build for every client and every employer runs on one integrated method.

Deep Discovery, five layers. Surface Symptom becomes Stated Problem becomes Operational Impact becomes Economic Impact becomes Root Constraint and Unconsidered Need. You do not earn the right to claim a Power Message until you reach Layer 4. In physical security, Layer 4 is where the CFO lives: fully-loaded guard labor cost per square foot, incident investigation hours per month, insurance premium exposure per incident severity, duty-of-care liability reserves, and workforce-safety ESG disclosure risk. An AE who stops at Layer 2 wins a camera-pilot deal at $50K and never sees the CFO.

Power Messaging. A Power Message links a unique capability to a buyer-specific problem that can be defined in clear economic terms. It emerges from discovery, never from marketing. "Ambient has better computer vision than Verkada" is not a Power Message. "Your 28-site East Coast footprint runs approximately 340 guard FTEs at a loaded cost of $18.7M annually. Two-thirds of their hours are spent on monitored patrol and camera review, work our platform automates with near human-level visual perception. A conservative 30 percent shift reduction translates to $5.6M in year-one run-rate savings, before the insurance-premium adjustment your broker would write against a documented AI monitoring control" is a Power Message.

Two gates before I sell. Gate 1 is Active Evaluation. The buyer is actually in motion, with a named stakeholder, a named timeline, and a named procurement path. Gate 2 is Quantified Severity. They can name the dollar cost of doing nothing. If either gate fails, I do not walk. I widen. I surface a larger use case, follow the economic pain downstream, and tie the solution to an existing forcing function inside the account, a contract renewal, a regulatory deadline, a board-level number. That is how the large national radiology provider became $1.6M ARR from a $30K OCR request: the OCR engagement alone had no active evaluation, so I anchored the deal to the incumbent's contract renewal. The renewal was the forcing function. My solution replaced the incumbent's majority-revenue contract.

Mutual Sequence of Events. Co-created with the buyer. Includes procurement, legal, SOC 2 and privacy review, integration scoping against existing camera infrastructure (ONVIF compatibility), security operations center training, rollout plan across sites, and post-go-live guard-reduction measurement milestones. The sequence ends beyond signature, at the first documented guard-FTE reduction report, because that is when the CFO sees the outcome they bought. That is how we never get caught selling.

The Plan Letter. Sent within 24 hours of every substantive conversation. It restates value in the buyer's economic language, delivers every commitment made in the meeting, lays out the complete timeline to first measurable guard-reduction report, and asks the buyer to confirm in writing. If they write back yes, the deal belongs in the forecast. If they rewrite the sequence, that is better data and the deal belongs in the forecast with updated milestones. If they go silent, they are not in the forecast.

The forecast rule. No buyer-confirmed Plan Letter, no deal in the forecast. I learned this at UKG, where the culture enforced it without exception. My manager's line was "great job on the forecast. Show me the plan letter." That rule made forecasts defensible by construction, not by hope. It is what kept my number real year after year and what produced top-5-percent-globally ranking for 5.5 years.

The forcing function. If I cannot write a good Plan Letter after a call, I ran a bad meeting. Period. Run better meetings. That discipline is how I coach RSMs without sitting in every one of their meetings.

Every initiative below exists to make Plan Letters writable and forecasts accurate for the East territory. That is the whole point.

2.3 Guard-labor replacement, the real economic engine

The physical security market reporting above undersells what Ambient.ai actually competes with. The larger, messier, budget-rich line item is human guard labor.

  • US contract guard industry revenue: approximately $38B in 2025 per ASIS and IBISWorld directional data.
  • Top-4 US guard firms (Allied Universal, Securitas, GardaWorld, G4S Secure Solutions) employ well over 800,000 guards in North America.
  • Guard labor shortage is structural. Post-2022, starting wages have risen 20 to 35 percent in most East Coast MSAs. Turnover at large guard firms runs 100 to 200 percent annually. Large enterprise clients are absorbing both higher rates and coverage gaps.
  • Fully-loaded guard hour (wages, benefits, training, PPE, supervision, insurance, margin) runs $28 to $45 per hour in NYC, DC, Boston, and Philadelphia metros.

The Fortune 100 East Coast security P&L. Typical profile: 30 to 80 sites. 250 to 600 guard FTEs. $15M to $35M annual guard contract spend. 60 to 75 percent of total physical security operating budget is labor. Of those labor hours, 50 to 70 percent are spent on monitoring activities (camera review, patrol logging, access-event triage) that an AI video platform can automate with high accuracy. That is the dollar pool Ambient.ai addresses. It is an order of magnitude larger than the VMS-replacement dollar pool.

Implication for the sell. The largest Ambient.ai East deals will not be priced against Verkada or Genetec. They will be priced against the guard contract the customer is trying to shrink. That is a CFO conversation, not a camera conversation.

2.4 Insurance and duty-of-care tailwind

  • Commercial property and casualty premiums rose 9 to 12 percent year-over-year in 2024 and 2025 for East Coast enterprise clients with workplace-violence exposure, per major broker (Marsh, Aon, WTW) public commentary.
  • Workers compensation premium on front-line retail, branch, hospital, and distribution workforces has hardened. Insurers are demanding documented security controls at renewal.
  • Workplace violence and active-shooter riders are being re-underwritten. Brokers are telling CSOs that documented AI-monitoring reduces loss-ratio exposure and produces measurable premium adjustments at renewal.
  • Duty-of-care exposure for distributed workforces and hybrid office populations is a board-reported risk for every Fortune 500 East Coast HQ.

Implication for the sell. Every East Coast CSO has an insurance renewal conversation on their calendar in the next 12 months. Ambient.ai's product is a documented control that brokers will underwrite. Initiative 2 operationalizes the broker-parallel sell.

2.5 Gaps and flags to validate in the first 30 days

  • No public analyst has priced the East-Coast-specific AI physical security TAM. My triangulation above is directional and should be validated against Ambient.ai's internal salesforce data.
  • The federal civilian opportunity depends on FedRAMP or equivalent posture. I would not commit to federal ARR targets in the first 90 days without understanding the compliance roadmap.
  • Guard-firm partnership strategy is an open question. Allied Universal and Securitas could be channel partners or competitive threats. I would want to understand whether Ambient.ai's go-to-market treats them as either.
  • Insurance-broker co-sell economics are unknown to me. I would want to see whether brokers have been engaged as an influence channel.

3. Competitive Landscape

The competitive map determines where Ambient.ai East wins without a fight, where Ambient.ai must win against a named competitor, and where Ambient.ai should walk away.

3.1 Verkada, the primary displaceable competitor

Financial and organizational profile.

  • $757M total funding across 11 rounds. Latest round: December 2025, $100M led by Alphabet's CapitalG at $5.8B valuation. Prior round February 2025, $200M at $4.5B.
  • Revenue growth cited at 30 percent YoY. Surpassed $1B in annualized bookings (milestone typically cited pre-IPO).
  • ~30,000 customers at end of 2025.
  • Expanding into EMEA (Dubai office, 2026) and federal via Carahsoft partnership (2026).

Vulnerabilities.

  • 2021 breach. Hacker group "APT-69420 Arson Cats" accessed 150,000+ live Verkada customer cameras, including hospitals, schools, prisons, Tesla factories. 36-hour intrusion. Public trust damage still referenced in CSO RFP criteria.
  • August 2024 FTC settlement. $2.95M penalty, largest CAN-SPAM penalty in FTC history, for data security failures plus deceptive statements on HIPAA certification and Privacy Shield compliance, plus fake-positive-review practices. Verkada signed a comprehensive information security order. Every CSO with audit committee oversight is aware of this filing.
  • Facial recognition accuracy concerns. Independent testing documented 15 to 85 percent false positive rates across Verkada's facial recognition, including in public housing deployments. This is in public academic and investigative record.
  • Pricing controversy. Multi-year contracts with pre-paid hardware plus perpetual-SaaS structures have generated widely-discussed renewal friction among mid-sized customers rotating off.
  • IPO pressure. $5.8B valuation post-Series F requires near-term IPO-path bookings discipline. Sales org incentives tilted toward gross bookings, not customer outcomes, especially into calendar-end quarters.

Competitive read. Verkada is the largest current incumbent in Ambient.ai's target accounts and also the most vulnerable. Every Verkada deal signed 2020-2022 is now inside a 3-to-5-year contract review window. Every CSO who has read the FTC settlement has a documented reason to entertain alternatives. The displacement narrative writes itself: "Verkada gives you cameras with a compliance shadow. Ambient.ai is the AI layer that makes your existing camera infrastructure smarter without the data-security baggage." Framed that way, Ambient.ai can sit on top of Verkada hardware for some customers rather than rip and replace. That is a faster sales cycle and a lower switching cost. Initiative 1 codifies the displacement play. The messaging needs seller-usable battle-card format.

In pure AI performance (false alarm rate, scene understanding depth, activity classification), Ambient.ai leads. In single-vendor consolidation, Avigilon leads. 3.8 Category dynamic, not a single competitor

The Ambient.ai East competitive set resolves into three buckets. The VP Sales East's job is to run a different playbook against each.

  • Cloud VMS incumbents (Verkada, Eagle Eye, Rhombus, Meraki MV). These are cameras plus a cloud dashboard. They compete on hardware-plus-subscription bundles. Ambient.ai displaces them at the AI analytics layer and where possible sits on top of their hardware. The story is "AI brain, your cameras."
  • Enterprise VMS and access control incumbents (Genetec, Milestone, Motorola Avigilon Alta). These are deep-integration VMS stacks in large Fortune 500 and government accounts. Ambient.ai coexists with them as the AI analytics layer on top of the customer's existing VMS. The story is "upgrade what you already trust."
  • Human guard labor (Allied Universal, Securitas, GardaWorld, in-house security teams). The largest budget pool and the hardest to displace. Ambient.ai does not replace guards; it reduces the guard hours required to produce the same or better security outcomes. The story is "same coverage, fewer hours, better incident data."

Ambient.ai wins the East when it runs all three plays in parallel. Lose to any one bucket means losing the deal.

4. The Three Problems I Solve at Ambient.ai East

Problem 1: Convert East Coast density into forecastable named-account pipeline

Ambient.ai has a category-leading AI platform and a customer list that includes seven of the top ten US tech companies, Adobe, VMware, Impossible Foods, and TikTok USDS. That is West-Coast-concentrated proof. The East Coast expansion thesis is banks, healthcare IDNs, data centers, logistics, federal civilian, and large retail. Each of those verticals has a different buyer archetype, a different procurement path, and a different compliance posture. Without discipline, the team will chase everything, close none of it on time, and produce a lumpy forecast that does not support the Series C narrative. My job is to install named-account discipline by the end of week 4 and to have 40+ active East Coast enterprise sequences by week 8.

Problem 2: Sell guard-labor replacement economics, not camera features

The East Coast CSO is not asking for better cameras. The CSO is being asked by the CFO to cut $3M to $8M out of a $20M security operating budget while maintaining or improving coverage. That is a labor replacement conversation. Ambient.ai is uniquely positioned to lead it because the technology is measurably labor-saving, and because seven of the top ten tech customers already prove the labor math. The missing piece is a codified, seller-usable framework that every RSM can run in every CSO discovery meeting. Initiative 2 builds it.

Problem 3: Displace Verkada at the renewal window without fighting them feature-for-feature

The FTC settlement, the 2021 breach, and the facial-recognition accuracy record give Ambient.ai a legitimate, documented, CSO-grade displacement story. The trap is fighting Verkada feature-for-feature. Verkada sells hardware-plus-SaaS; Ambient.ai sells AI analytics on top of any camera infrastructure. The correct displacement play is to preserve the customer's hardware investment and replace the analytics layer. That compresses the switching cost and moves the conversation from rip-and-replace to AI upgrade. Initiative 1 operationalizes the displacement play.

The large national radiology provider reframe: live proof of this framework

The discipline above is not theoretical. The following is how I ran it live at a venture-backed, national radiology organization consolidating imaging centers across the US.

The client had a commoditized technical ask: OCR for 5.5 million faxed physician orders per year, at approximately $0.005 per page, estimated deal value $30K annually. An AE running a feature-led motion would have won the OCR RFP, won a $30K deal, and moved on.

I ran the five-layer discovery.

  • Layer 1 (surface symptom): OCR accuracy on physician order faxes.
  • Layer 2 (stated problem): reduce manual processing errors and cost at scale.
  • Layer 3 (operational impact): intake delays meant imaging centers competed internally for the same patient; first center to reach the patient won the scheduling. Manual exception handling added hours per order.
  • Layer 4 (economic impact): each delay-driven no-show cost the full margin of the patient's visit plus lifetime value. Underutilized imaging equipment (MRI, CT) was venture-funded capex with utilization-driven economics. Idle machines were direct revenue loss.
  • Layer 5 (root constraint, unconsidered need): the organization had no integrated intake-to-engagement system. OCR was one symptom. Patient engagement was another. Center-to-center routing was another. The root constraint was the absence of a revenue optimization infrastructure covering the full workflow from physician order to patient procedure.

I reframed the conversation from OCR tool to revenue optimization infrastructure. The stakeholder set expanded from IT Director to CIO plus COO plus CFO plus Operations leadership. The budget line moved from IT (where $30K is the ceiling) to revenue optimization (where $1.6M ARR is the natural scale). The competitive structure changed because no other vendor had assembled the complete pipeline.

The result: $30K commodity ask became $1.6M ARR, multi-year contract, incumbent displaced. 53x expansion.

Why this case is the right live proof for Ambient.ai. Every East Coast physical security RFP starts looking like the large national radiology provider OCR ask. "We need better cameras" is the OCR-level framing. "We need to reduce guard labor and document an insurance-grade AI monitoring control across our footprint" is the revenue optimization infrastructure framing. The structural move is identical: technical symptom to economic reframe, stakeholder expansion from IT to C-suite, incumbent displacement via category expansion rather than feature competition, deal control through Plan Letters and Mutual Sequence of Events across a 6 to 9 month enterprise cycle. Ambient.ai's category is ready for this move. The East territory is where it pays off fastest.

13.2 Where AI does not belong

  • Final Plan Letter approval. The RSM owns the relationship; the RSM owns the letter.
  • Pricing approval. Discount ceilings are human decisions with RSM and VP Sales East accountability.
  • Customer-facing discovery questions. AI drafts, humans deliver.
  • Direct customer-facing outreach at scale with no human review. The East market is too small and the CSO community too tight-knit to run sloppy automated outreach.

Guiding principle. AI is speed. Humans are accountability.
